Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *sérpeti. Cognates include Sanskrit सर्पति (sarpati, “to glide, crawl”), and Latin serpō (“to creep, crawl”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /hér.pɔː/ → /ˈer.po/ → /ˈer.po/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /hér.pɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈ(h)er.po/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈer.po/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈer.po/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈer.po/

Verb

[edit]

ἕρπω • (hérpō)

  1. to move slowly, walk; crawl, creep, slink
    • 467 BCE, Aeschylus, Seven Against Thebes 17
    • 422 BCE, Aristophanes, The Wasps 552
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 17.447
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 12.395
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 17.158
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Olympian Ode 7.52
    • 409 BCE, Sophocles, Philoctetes 207
  2. (Doric) to go or come
    • 525 BCE – 455 BCE, Aeschylus, Fragments 195
    • 525 BCE – 455 BCE, Aeschylus, Prometheus Bound 810
    • 425 BCE, Euripides, Andromache 722
    • 480 BCE – 406 BCE, Euripides, Cyclops 423
    • 416 BCE, Euripides, Herakles 1154
    • 412 BCE, Euripides, Helen 316
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Ajax 287
    • 406 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus at Colonus 1643
    • 409 BCE, Sophocles, Philoctetes 1223
  3. to come, happen (of things, events, etc.)
    • 424 BCE, Aristophanes, The Knights 673
    • 425 BCE, Aristophanes, Lysistrata 129
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Nemean Ode 4.43
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Nemean Ode 7.68
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Olympian Ode 13.105
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Ajax 157
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Ajax 1087
    • 442 BCE, Sophocles, Antigone 585
    • 442 BCE, Sophocles, Antigone 618
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Electra 1231
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Fragments 255
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Fragments 593
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Women of Trachis 547
