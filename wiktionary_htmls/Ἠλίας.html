Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • Ἡλίᾱς (Hēlíās), Ἠλείᾱς (Ēleíās), Ἡλείᾱς (Hēleíās)

Etymology

[edit]

Borrowed from Hebrew אֱלִיָּה (ʾĕliyyā).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /iˈli.as/ → /iˈli.as/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /iˈli.as/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /iˈli.as/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /iˈli.as/

Proper noun

[edit]

Ἠλίᾱς • (Ēlíās) m (genitive Ἠλίου); first declension

  1. a male given name from Hebrew, equivalent to English Elias or Elijah
  2. Elijah, an Israelite prophet.
    • 93 CE – 94 CE, Josephus, Antiquities of the Jews 8.331

Usage notes

[edit]

The variant Ἠλίας (Ēlías) is found in the earliest manuscripts and is considered to most likely be the authentic version.
