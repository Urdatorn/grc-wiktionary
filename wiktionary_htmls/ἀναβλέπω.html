Ancient Greek

[edit]
FWOTD – 3 February 2018

Etymology

[edit]

From ᾰ̓νᾰ- (ana-, “up”) +‎ βλέπω (blépō, “to look at”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /a.na.blé.pɔː/ → /a.naˈβle.po/ → /a.naˈvle.po/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /a.na.blé.pɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /a.naˈble.po/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /a.naˈβle.po/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /a.naˈvle.po/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /a.naˈvle.po/

Verb

[edit]

ᾰ̓νᾰβλέπω • (anablépō)

  1. (intransitive) to look up, especially as a mark of confidence
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.111
    • 416 BCE, Euripides, Herakles 563
    • 423 BCE, Aristophanes, The Clouds 346
    • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 515C
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Hellenica 7.1.30
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cyropaedia 1.4.12
    1. (transitive) to look up at [with accusative or dative]
      • 413 BCE, Euripides, Ion 1467
      • 423 BCE, Euripides, The Suppliants 322
    2. (with cognate accusative) to cast a look of
      • 413 BCE, Euripides, Ion 1263
  2. (intransitive) to see again, to recover one's sight
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.111
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Phaedrus 243B
    • 50 CE – 100 CE, The Gospel of John 9:11
    • 388 BCE, Aristophanes, Plutus 95
    • 388 BCE, Aristophanes, Plutus 117
