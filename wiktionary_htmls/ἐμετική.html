Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Feminine form of ἐμετῐκός (emetikós, “emetic”, adjective). Cognate with Latin emetica.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.me.ti.kɛ̌ː/ → /e.me.tiˈci/ → /e.me.tiˈci/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.me.ti.kɛ̌ː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /e.me.tiˈke̝/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /e.me.tiˈci/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /e.me.tiˈci/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /e.me.tiˈci/

Adjective

[edit]

ἐμετῐκή • (emetikḗ)

  1. nominative/vocative feminine singular of ἐμετῐκός (emetikós)

Noun

[edit]

ἐμετῐκή • (emetikḗ) f (genitive ἐμετῐκῆς); first declension

  1. (nominalized) course of emetics
    • 68 BCE – 44 BCE, Cicero, Epistulae ad Atticum 13.52.1:
      Ἐμετῐκὴν (Emetikḕn) agebat.
      He was taking a course of emetics.
