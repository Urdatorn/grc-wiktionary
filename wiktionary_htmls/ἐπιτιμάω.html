Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ἐπῐτῑμέω (epitīméō) — Attic

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐπῐ- (epi-, “upon”) +‎ τῑμᾰ́ω (tīmáō, “to honor”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.pi.tiː.má.ɔː/ → /e.pi.tiˈma.o/ → /e.pi.tiˈma.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.pi.tiː.má.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /e.pi.tiˈma.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /e.pi.tiˈma.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /e.pi.tiˈma.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /e.pi.tiˈma.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἐπῐτῑμᾰ́ω • (epitīmáō)

  1. to lay a value upon
    1. to show honor to
    2. to raise in price
      • 342 BCE – 291 BCE, Diphilus, Collected Works 32.27
      • 175 CE – 235 CE, Claudius Aelianus, On the Nature of Animals 10.50
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Phormio 39
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Polycles 6
  2. (of judges) to lay a penalty on a person
    • 389 BCE – 314 BCE, Aeschines, Against Timarchus 113
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 4.43
    1. to object to one as blamable
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Phaedrus 237C
      • 436 BCE – 338 BCE, Isocrates, To Demonicus 17
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Poetics 1455A.26
      1. (with accusative of object) to censure
        • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Leptines 148
        • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Memorabilia 1.2.31
        • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Nicomachean Ethics 1114A.29
      2. (with dative) to censure
        • 445 BCE – 380 BCE, Lysias, On the Refusal of a Pension 17
        • 436 BCE – 338 BCE, Isocrates, On the Peace 52
        • 330 BCE, Demosthenes, On the Crown 64
        • 70 CE – 110 CE, The Gospel of Matthew 8:26
        • 200 BCE – 118 BCE, Polybius, The Histories 8.9.1
        • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, Philip 7
        • 366 BCE – 348 BCE, Plato, Theaetetus 169D
        • 200 BCE – 118 BCE, Polybius, The Histories 7.12.9
      3. (absolute)
        • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 3.38
        • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 4.28
        • 349 BCE, Demosthenes, First Olynthiac 16
        • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Politics 1284A.27
