Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ἐκδύνω (ekdúnō)

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐκ- (ek-, “out”) +‎ δῠ́ω (dúō, “to get in”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /eɡ.dý.ɔː/ → /eɡˈðy.o/ → /eɡˈði.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /eɡ.dý.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /eɡˈdy.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /eɡˈðy.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /eɡˈðy.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /eɡˈði.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἐκδῠ́ω • (ekdúō)

  1. to take off, remove, strip
    • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, Agamemnon 1269
    • 480 BCE – 411 BCE, Antiphon of Rhamnus, First Tetralogy 2.5
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Timocrates 204
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Conon 8
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 14.341
    • 445 BCE – 380 BCE, Lysias, Against Theomnestus 1 10
    • 200 BCE – 118 BCE, Polybius, The Histories 15.27.9
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cyropaedia 1.3.17
    • Palatine Anthology 5.72
  2. (middle voice, active or passive in middle sense) to strip oneself (of a thing), take off oneself
    • 425 BCE, Aristophanes, Lysistrata 686
    • 421 BCE, Aristophanes, Peace 336
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, History of Animals 549b25
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, History of Animals 600b15
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Conon 35
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.8
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 5.106
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 3.114
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 1.437
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 14.460
    • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Pompey 28
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Hellenica 2.4.19
  3. (aorist ἐξέδῡν, perfect ἐκδέδῡκᾰ)
    1. (with genitive) to get out of, to emerge from
      • 423 BCE, Euripides, The Suppliants 416
      • 413 BCE, Euripides, Iphigenia in Tauris 602
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 22.334
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Phaedo 109d
    2. (with accusative) to escape; to shun
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Leptines 1
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 16.99
      • 204 CE – 270 CE, Plotinus, Collected Works 6.6.8
      • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Pompey 30
      • 6th century BC, Theognis of Megara, Elegies 358
