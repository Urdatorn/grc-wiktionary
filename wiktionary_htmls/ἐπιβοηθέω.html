Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ἐπιβοᾱθέω (epiboāthéō) — Doric

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐπι- (epi-) +‎ βοηθέω (boēthéō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.pi.bo.ɛː.tʰé.ɔː/ → /e.pi.βo.iˈθe.o/ → /e.pi.vo.iˈθe.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.pi.bo.ɛː.tʰé.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /e.pi.bo.e̝ˈtʰe.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /e.pi.βo.iˈθe.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /e.pi.vo.iˈθe.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /e.pi.vo.iˈθe.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἐπῐβοηθέω • (epiboēthéō)

  1. to come to someone’s aid, succor [with dative]
