Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]
PIE word
*dléwkus

From γλυκύς (glukús, “sweet”) +‎ πικρός (pikrós, “bitter”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ɡly.ký.piː.kros/ → /ɣlyˈcy.pi.kros/ → /ɣliˈci.pi.kros/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ɡly.ký.piː.kros/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ɡlyˈky.pi.kros/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ɣlyˈcy.pi.kros/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ɣlyˈcy.pi.kros/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ɣliˈci.pi.kros/

Adjective

[edit]

γλῠκῠ́πῑκρος • (glukúpīkros) m or f (neuter γλῠκῠ́πῑκρον); second declension

  1. bittersweet
