Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ὕψι (húpsi, “high”), ὕψος (húpsos, “height, summit”) +‎ -λός (-lós). Compare Proto-Celtic *ouxselos, whence Old Irish úasal, Welsh uchel and the first part of Gaulish Uxellodunum.[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /hyp.sɛː.lós/ → /yp.siˈlos/ → /ip.siˈlos/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /hyp.sɛː.lós/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /(h)yp.se̝ˈlos/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /yp.siˈlos/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /yp.siˈlos/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ip.siˈlos/

Adjective

[edit]

ῠ̔ψηλός • (hupsēlós) m (feminine ῠ̔ψηλή, neuter ῠ̔ψηλόν); first/second declension

  1. high, lofty
    • 110 CE – 180 CE, Pausanias Periegeta, Graeciae Descriptio 5.13.9
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 1.426
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 3.384
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.110
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 3.97
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 732C
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Hellenica 4.5.4
    • 125 CE – 200 CE, Lucian, A Professor of Public Speaking 6
    • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Eumenes 17
    • 366 BCE – 348 BCE, Plato, Theaetetus 175D
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Hellenica 6.2.29
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 7.4
    • 5th century BC, Pherecrates, Fragmenta 64
  2. (figuratively) high, lofty, stately, proud
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Olympian Ode 2.22
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Olympian Ode 5.1
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Pythian Ode 3.111
    • 380 BCE, Plato, Euthydemus 289E
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Epistles 341E
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Ajax 1230
    1. (of persons)
      • 412 BCE, Euripides, Helen 418
      • 430 BCE, Euripides, Herakles' Children 613
      • 428 BCE, Euripides, Hippolytus 730
      • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 494D
      • 440 BCE – 390 BCE, Andocides, On the Peace 7
      • 389 BCE – 314 BCE, Aeschines, On the Embassy 174
      • 423 BCE, Euripides, The Suppliants 555
      • Metrodorus, Fragments 57
      • 125 CE – 200 CE, Lucian, Nigrinus 25
      • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Dion 4
    2. (of poets) sublime
      • 213 CE – 273 CE, Cassius Longinus, Collected Works 40.2
      • 213 CE – 273 CE, Cassius Longinus, Collected Works 43.3
      • 60 BCE – 7 BCE, Dionysius of Halicarnassus, Commentaries on the Attic Orators: On Lysias 13
      • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Pericles 5
      • 129 CE – 216 CE, Galen, Collected Works 10.12
