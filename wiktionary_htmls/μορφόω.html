Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From μορφή (morphḗ, “form, type”) +‎ -όω (-óō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /mor.pʰó.ɔː/ → /morˈɸo.o/ → /morˈfo.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /mor.pʰó.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /morˈpʰo.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /morˈɸo.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /morˈfo.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /morˈfo.o/

Verb

[edit]

μορφόω • (morphóō)

  1. to shape, form
    • 400 BCE – 300 BCE, Aeneas Tacticus, How to Survive under Siege 40.4
    • 315 BCE – 240 BCE, Aratus, Phaenomena 375
    • New Testament, Epistle to the Galatians 4:19
    • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Moralia 2.1013c
    • 371 BCE – 287 BCE, Theophrastus, On the Causes of Plants 5.6.7
