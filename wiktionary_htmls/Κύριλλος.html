Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From κῡ́ριος (kū́rios, “lord”) or Κῦρος (Kûros, “Cyrus”) +‎ -ιλλος (-illos, diminutive suffix).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ˈcy.ril.los/ → /ˈci.ri.los/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈky.ril.los/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈcy.ril.los/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈcy.ril.los/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈci.ri.los/

Proper noun

[edit]

Κῡ́ριλλος • (Kū́rillos) m (genitive Κῡρίλλου); second declension

  1. a male given name, equivalent to English Cyril
