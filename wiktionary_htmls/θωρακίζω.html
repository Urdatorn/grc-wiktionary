Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

θώρᾱξ (thṓrāx, “corselet, breastplate”) +‎ -ίζω (-ízō)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /tʰɔː.raː.kíz.dɔː/ → /θo.raˈci.zo/ → /θo.raˈci.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /tʰɔː.raː.kíz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /tʰo.raˈki.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /θo.raˈci.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /θo.raˈci.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /θo.raˈci.zo/

Verb

[edit]

θωρᾱκίζω • (thōrākízō)

  1. to arm with a breastplate
    Synonym: θωρήσσω (thōrḗssō)
