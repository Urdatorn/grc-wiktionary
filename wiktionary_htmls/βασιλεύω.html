Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • βασιλέω (basiléō)

Etymology

[edit]

From βασιλεύς (basileús, “king”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ba.si.lěu̯.ɔː/ → /βa.siˈle.βo/ → /va.siˈle.vo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ba.si.lěu̯.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ba.siˈle.wo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /βa.siˈle.βo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /va.siˈle.vo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /va.siˈle.vo/

Verb

[edit]

βᾰσῐλεύω • (basileúō)

  1. to be king, to rule, reign as king
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 2.203
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 9.616
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 2.47
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 22.52
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 6.425
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 11.285
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.2
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 1.401
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 7.59
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Septuagint, 1 Maccabees 1.16
    • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 576D–E
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Politics 1284B.39
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 680E
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Pythian Ode 4.106
    • 445 BCE – 380 BCE, Lysias, Funeral Oration 19
    • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Sulla 12
    1. (ἡ βασιλεύσουσα πόλις (hē basileúsousa pólis)) the imperial city, Rome
      • 250 CE – 350 CE, Athenaeus, Deipnosophistae 3.98C
    2. to enjoy as absolute master
      • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Theocritus, Collected Works 21.60
    3. (absolute) to live right royally
      • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Moralia 2.101D
      • 53 CE – 55 CE, Paul the Apostle, First Epistle to the Corinthians 4:8
    4. (causal) to appoint as king
      • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Septuagint, Judges 9.6
      • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, 1 Samuel 8.22
      • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, 1 Samuel 12.1

Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Inherited from Ancient Greek βασιλεύω (basileúō). Sense 'sunset' since mediaeval times.

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): /va.siˈle.vo/
  • Hyphenation: βα‧σι‧λεύ‧ω

Verb

[edit]

βασιλεύω • (vasilévo) (past βασίλεψα, passive —)

  1. (monarchy) to reign, rule (also figurative)
  2. (astronomy) to set, go down, wester (sun, stars, etc)
    1. (figuratively) to decline
