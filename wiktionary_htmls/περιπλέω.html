Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • περῐπλώω (periplṓō) — Ionic

Etymology

[edit]

περι- (“round”) +‎ πλέω (“sail”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pe.ri.plé.ɔː/ → /pe.riˈple.o/ → /pe.riˈple.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pe.ri.plé.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /pe.riˈple.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /pe.riˈple.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /pe.riˈple.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /pe.riˈple.o/

Verb

[edit]

περῐπλέω • (peripléō)

  1. to sail or swim around; circumnavigate
  2. (figuratively) to slip about
    1. (Koine, medicine) to recur
  3. (Koine, passive voice) to be wrapped up

  • περῐπλώω (periplṓō, “Ionic form of περιπλέω”)
  • σῠμπερῐπλέω (sumperipléō, “sail about with”)

Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Ancient Greek περιπλέω. Morphologically, from περι- (peri-, “round”) +‎ πλέω (pléo, “to sail”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): /pe.riˈple.o/
  • Hyphenation: πε‧ρι‧πλέ‧ω

Verb

[edit]

περιπλέω • (peripléo) (past περιέπλευσα, passive —)

  1. to sail round, circumnavigate
