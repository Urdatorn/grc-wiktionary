Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • χρῡσοῦς (khrūsoûs) — Attic
  • χρῡ́σειος (khrū́seios) — Epic

Etymology

[edit]

From χρῡσός (khrūsós, “gold”) +‎ -εος (-eos).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /kʰry̌ː.se.os/ → /ˈxry.se.os/ → /ˈxri.se.os/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /kʰry̌ː.se.os/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈkʰry.se.os/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈxry.se.os/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈxry.se.os/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈxri.se.os/

Adjective

[edit]

χρῡ́σεος • (khrū́seos) m (feminine χρῡσέη, neuter χρῡ́σεον); first/second declension

  1. golden, made of gold, decked or inlaid with gold

    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 8.121
    • 125 CE – 200 CE, Lucian, The Mistaken Critic 15
    1. (χρύσεια μέταλλα) gold mines
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 4.105
    2. (masculine substantive) gold coin
      • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Septuagint, Genesis 24.22
      • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Pericles 25
