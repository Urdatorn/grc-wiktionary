Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • τολμέω (tolméō)

Etymology

[edit]

From τόλμᾰ (τόλμη) (tólma (tólmē), “(feminine) courage”) +‎ -άω (-áō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /tol.má.ɔː/ → /tolˈma.o/ → /tolˈma.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /tol.má.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /tolˈma.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /tolˈma.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /tolˈma.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /tolˈma.o/

Verb

[edit]

τολμᾰ́ω • (tolmáō) (contracted: τολμῶ (tolmô))

  1. to dare

Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

τολμ(ώ) (tolm(ó)) + modern suffix -άω (-áo), from the Ancient Greek τολμῶ (tolmô), contracted form of τολμάω (tolmáō, “I dare”), from τόλμη (tólmē)

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): /tolˈma.o/
  • Hyphenation: τολ‧μά‧ω

Verb

[edit]

τολμάω • (tolmáo) / τολμώ (past τόλμησα, passive τολμώμαι, p‑past τολμήθηκα, ppp τολμημένος)

  1. (transitive) to dare
  2. (intransitive) to dare
  3. (intransitive) to be daring, take risks
  4. and see: τολμώ (tolmó) (for learned expressions)
