Ancient Greek

[edit]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /po.liː.ti.kɛ̌ː/ → /po.li.tiˈci/ → /po.li.tiˈci/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /po.liː.ti.kɛ̌ː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /po.li.tiˈke̝/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /po.li.tiˈci/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /po.li.tiˈci/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /po.li.tiˈci/

Adjective

[edit]

πολῑτῐκή • (polītikḗ)

  1. feminine nominative/vocative singular of πολῑτικός (polītikós)

Noun

[edit]

πολῑτῐκή • (polītikḗ) f (genitive πολῑτῐκῆς); first declension

  1. the art of government, the science of politics
  2. concubine, mistress

Greek

[edit]

Noun

[edit]

πολιτική • (politikí) f (plural πολιτικές)

  1. (politics) politics, policy

Declension

[edit]

Related terms

[edit]
  • απολιτικός (apolitikós, “apolitical”, adjective)
  • αντιπολιτικός (antipolitikós, “unpolitical”, adjective)
  • and see: πόλη f (póli, “city, townspeople”)
