Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *tḱn̥-, zero-grade of *tḱen- (“strike”), with a *y progressive aspect marker and simplification of Proto-Indo-European *tḱ- to *ḱ- before a syllabic nasal (compare χαμαί (khamaí) and Proto-Indo-European *ḱm̥tóm).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /kǎi̯.nɔː/ → /ˈcɛ.no/ → /ˈce.no/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /kǎi̯.nɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈkɛ.no/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈcɛ.no/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈce.no/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈce.no/

Verb

[edit]

καίνω • (kaínō)

  1. to kill, slay
