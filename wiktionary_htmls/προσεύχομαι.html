Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From πρός (prós, “to”) + εὔχομαι (eúkhomai, “to pray, vow”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pro.sěu̯.kʰo.mai̯/ → /proˈseɸ.xo.mɛ/ → /proˈsef.xo.me/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pro.sěu̯.kʰo.mai̯/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /proˈsew.kʰo.mɛ/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /proˈseɸ.xo.mɛ/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /proˈsef.xo.me/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /proˈsef.xo.me/

Verb

[edit]

προσεύχομαι • (proseúkhomai)

  1. to pray, vow
    • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, Agamemnon 317
    • 385 BCE – 380 BCE, Plato, Symposium 220D
    • 366 BCE – 348 BCE, Plato, Critias 136A, (compare Xenophon, Cyropaedia 2.1.1)
    1. (with accusative)
      • 388 BCE, Aristophanes, Plutus 958, (compare Euripides, The Trojan Women 887)
    2. (absolute) to offer prayers, worship
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.48
      • 525 BCE – 455 BCE, Aeschylus, Prometheus Bound 937
      • 442 BCE, Sophocles, Antigone 1337
  2. to pray for a thing
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Hellenica 3.2.22

Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Ancient Greek προσεύχομαι. Morphologically, προσ- (“towards”) + εύχομαι (“wish”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): /proˈsef.xo.me/
  • Hyphenation: προ‧σεύ‧χο‧μαι
  • Old Hyphenation: προσ‧εύ‧χο‧μαι

Verb

[edit]

προσεύχομαι • (proséfchomai) deponent (past προσευχήθηκα)

  1. to pray
