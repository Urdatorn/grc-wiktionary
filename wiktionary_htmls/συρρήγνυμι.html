Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ξυρρήγνῡμι (xurrhḗgnūmi) — Old Attic
  • συρρηγνῡ́ω (surrhēgnū́ō)

Etymology

[edit]

From συρ- (sur-) +‎ ῥήγνῡμι (rhḗgnūmi).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /syr̥.r̥ɛ̌ː.ɡnyː.mi/ → /syrˈri.ɣny.mi/ → /siˈri.ɣni.mi/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /syr̥.r̥ɛ̌ː.ɡnyː.mi/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /syrˈre̝.ɡny.mi/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /syrˈri.ɣny.mi/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /syrˈri.ɣny.mi/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /siˈri.ɣni.mi/

Verb

[edit]

συρρήγνῡμῐ • (surrhḗgnūmi)

  1. to break in pieces, break down
  2. to dash together
  3. (passive voice) to break out (of war)
  4. to break forth (of rivers)
  5. to meet in battle, engage
