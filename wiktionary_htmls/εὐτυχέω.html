Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From εὐτυχής (eutukhḗs) +‎ -έω (-éō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /eu̯.ty.kʰé.ɔː/ → /eɸ.tyˈçe.o/ → /ef.tiˈçe.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /eu̯.ty.kʰé.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ew.tyˈkʰe.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /eɸ.tyˈçe.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ef.tyˈçe.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ef.tiˈçe.o/

Verb

[edit]

εὐτῠχέω • (eutukhéō)

  1. (intransitive) to have good fortune, to be prosperous, to be well off, to be successful
