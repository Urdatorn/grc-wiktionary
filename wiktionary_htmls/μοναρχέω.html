Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • μουναρχέω (mounarkhéō) — Ionic

Etymology

[edit]

Derived from μόναρχος (mónarkhos, “monarch”) +‎ -έω (-éō, verb-forming suffix).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /mo.nar.kʰé.ɔː/ → /mo.narˈçe.o/ → /mo.narˈçe.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /mo.nar.kʰé.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /mo.narˈkʰe.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /mo.narˈçe.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /mo.narˈçe.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /mo.narˈçe.o/

Verb

[edit]

μοναρχέω • (monarkhéō)

  1. to rule alone, to be a monarch
