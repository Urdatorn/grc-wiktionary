Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Hellenic *démō, per Beekes, ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *dem-h₁-, an extension of the root *dem- (“to build”). Cognate with Mycenaean Greek 𐀆𐀕𐀃𐀳 (de-me-o-te).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /dé.mɔː/ → /ˈðe.mo/ → /ˈðe.mo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /dé.mɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈde.mo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈðe.mo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈðe.mo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈðe.mo/

Verb

[edit]

δέμω • (démō)

  1. to build, construct, prepare, make
