Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐπῐ- (epi-, “upon”) +‎ φαίνω (phaínō, “to shine, bring to light”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.pi.pʰǎi̯.nɔː/ → /e.piˈɸɛ.no/ → /e.piˈfe.no/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.pi.pʰǎi̯.nɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /e.piˈpʰɛ.no/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /e.piˈɸɛ.no/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /e.piˈfe.no/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /e.piˈfe.no/

Verb

[edit]

ἐπιφαίνω • (epiphaínō)

  1. to shine, shed light upon
  2. to show, display
  3. (middle voice, passive voice) to come to light, appear
