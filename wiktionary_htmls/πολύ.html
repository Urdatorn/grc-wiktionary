Ancient Greek

[edit]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /po.lý/ → /poˈly/ → /poˈli/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /po.lý/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /poˈly/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /poˈly/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /poˈly/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /poˈli/

Etymology 1

[edit]

See the etymology of the corresponding lemma form.

Adjective

[edit]

πολῠ́ • (polú)

  1. nominative/accusative/vocative singular neuter of πολῠ́ς (polús)

Etymology 2

[edit]

Adverbial accusative of πολῠ́ς (polús, “much, many”).

Adverb

[edit]

πολῠ́ • (polú)

  1. much, very
  2. too, much too

Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Ancient Greek πολύ (polú). Cognate with Mariupol Greek пулы́ (pulý).

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): /poˈli/
  • Rhymes: -i
  • Homophones: πολλή (pollí), πολλοί (polloí)

Adjective

[edit]

πολύ • (polý)

  1. Accusative masculine singular form of πολύς (polýs).
  2. Nominative neuter singular form of πολύς (polýs).
  3. Accusative neuter singular form of πολύς (polýs).

Adverb

[edit]

πολύ • (polý)

  1. very, too, long
    Η Ελένη είναι πολύ χαρούμενη.
    I Eléni eínai polý charoúmeni.
    Eleni is very happy.
    Ο καφές είναι πολύ γλυκός.
    O kafés eínai polý glykós.
    The coffee is too sweet.

Derived terms

[edit]
  • χαίρω πολύ (chaíro polý, “pleased to meet you”)
  • κατά πολύ (katá polý, “by far”)
  • πάρα πολύ (pára polý, “a lot, very much”)
  • λίγο πολύ (lígo polý, “more or less”)
  • πολύ περισσότερο (polý perissótero, “much more”)
  • το πολύ (to polý, “at the latest”)
  • το πολύ πολύ (to polý polý, “at most”)
