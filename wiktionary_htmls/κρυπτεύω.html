Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From κρυπτός (kruptós, “hidden”) +‎ -εύω (-eúō, “a suffix forming verbs of condition or activity”)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /kryp.těu̯.ɔː/ → /krypˈte.βo/ → /kripˈte.vo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /kryp.těu̯.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /krypˈte.wo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /krypˈte.βo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /krypˈte.vo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /kripˈte.vo/

Verb

[edit]

κρυπτεύω • (krupteúō)

  1. to conceal, hide
  2. (intransitive) hide oneself, lie concealed
  3. (passive voice) to be ensnared
