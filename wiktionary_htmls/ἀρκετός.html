Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἀρκέω (arkéō, “to be enough”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ar.ke.tós/ → /ar.ceˈtos/ → /ar.ceˈtos/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ar.ke.tós/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ar.keˈtos/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ar.ceˈtos/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ar.ceˈtos/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ar.ceˈtos/

Adjective

[edit]

ἀρκετός • (arketós) m (feminine ᾰ̓ρκετή, neuter ᾰ̓ρκετόν); first/second declension

  1. sufficient, enough
  2. (of persons) satisfactory

Usage notes

[edit]

This can be found in the construction: ἀρκετόν [ἐστι] + inf., "it is enough to..."
