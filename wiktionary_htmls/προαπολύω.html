Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From προ- (pro-, “before”) +‎ ᾰ̓πολῡ́ω (apolū́ō, “loose from”)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pro.a.po.ly̌ː.ɔː/ → /pro.a.poˈly.o/ → /pro.a.poˈli.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pro.a.po.ly̌ː.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /pro.a.poˈly.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /pro.a.poˈly.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /pro.a.poˈly.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /pro.a.poˈli.o/

Verb

[edit]

προᾰπολῡ́ω • (proapolū́ō)

  1. to dismiss before
  2. (Byzantine) to liberate from before, free from before
