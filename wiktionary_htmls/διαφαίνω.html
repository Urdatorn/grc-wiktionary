Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From δια- (dia-) +‎ φαίνω (phaínō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /di.a.pʰǎi̯.nɔː/ → /ði.aˈɸɛ.no/ → /ði.aˈfe.no/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /di.a.pʰǎi̯.nɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /di.aˈpʰɛ.no/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ði.aˈɸɛ.no/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ði.aˈfe.no/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ði.aˈfe.no/

Verb

[edit]

δῐᾰφαίνω • (diaphaínō)

  1. (active voice) to show through (transitive), shine through
  2. (passive voice) to appear or show through (intransitive)
  3. (passive voice) to glow, to be red hot
  4. (passive voice) to be proved, show itself, be conspicuous among others
