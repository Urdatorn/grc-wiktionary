Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ἐρέπτω (eréptō)

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Hellenic *erépʰō, from Proto-Indo-European *h₁rébʰ-e-ti, from Proto-Indo-European *h₁rebʰ- (“to cover, to roof; rib”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.ré.pʰɔː/ → /eˈre.ɸo/ → /eˈre.fo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.ré.pʰɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /eˈre.pʰo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /eˈre.ɸo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /eˈre.fo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /eˈre.fo/

Verb

[edit]

ἐρέφω • (eréphō)

  1. to cover with a roof
    • 414 BCE, Aristophanes, The Birds 1110
    • 446 BCE – 386 BCE, Aristophanes, Fragments 73
    • 343 BCE, Demosthenes, On the False Embassy 265
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 24.450
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 23.193
    • 170 CE – 250 CE, Philostratus, Life of Apollonius of Tyana 1.25
  2. to crown
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Olympian Ode 13.32
    • 406 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus at Colonus 473
  3. (middle voice) to crown oneself
    • 300 BCE – 250 BCE, Apollonius of Rhodes, Argonautica 2.159
    • 500 BCE – 400 BCE, Bacchylides, Collected Works 8.24
    • 500 BCE – 400 BCE, Bacchylides, Collected Works 12.70
    • 405 BCE, Euripides, The Bacchae 323
  4. to wreathe, cover (as with garlands)
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Olympian Ode 1.68
