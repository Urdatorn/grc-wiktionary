Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From κῆδος (kêdos, “care, concern, mourning”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /kɛ̌ː.dɔː/ → /ˈci.ðo/ → /ˈci.ðo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /kɛ̌ː.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈke̝.do/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈci.ðo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈci.ðo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈci.ðo/

Verb

[edit]

κήδω • (kḗdō)

  1. (transitive) to trouble, distress, vex
  2. (middle voice, passive voice) to be concerned for, care for [with genitive ‘someone’]
