Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ὦνος (ônos, “price”) +‎ -έομαι (-éomai).

The suppletive aorist ἐπρῐάμην (epriámēn) is from Proto-Indo-European *kʷreyh₂- (“to buy”). Cognates include Welsh prynu, Old Irish crenaid, Sanskrit क्रीणाति (krīṇā́ti) (root क्री (krī)), Old East Slavic крити (kriti).

Verb

[edit]

ὠνέομαι • (ōnéomai)

  1. to buy, purchase
    Synonym: πρίαμαι (príamai)
    Antonyms: πέρνημι (pérnēmi), πιπράσκω (pipráskō), πωλέω (pōléō)
    1. (in present and imperfect tenses) to offer to buy, deal for, bargain or bid for
      • 750 BCE – 650 BCE, Hesiod, Works and Days 339
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.1
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.69
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.165, (Compare 3.139, 6.121)
      • 375 BCE – 275 BCE, Alexis, Apeglaukomenos 2.10, (Compare The Heiress 1)
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 741B
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 5.6
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Protagoras 313D–E
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes 123.21
      • 50 CE – 150 CE, Achilles Tatius, Leucippe and Clitophon 5.17
    2. (with dative) to buy from
      • 446 BCE – 386 BCE, Aristophanes, The Acharnians 815
      • 421 BCE, Aristophanes, Peace 1261
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Hellenica 7.2.17
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 3.2.21
    3. (with genitive) to buy for such a price
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 5.6
      • 424 BCE, Euripides, Hecuba 360
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 7.6.24
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Politics 5.11.31
    4. (with dative) to buy with
      • 407 BCE, Euripides, Iphigenia in Aulis 1.170
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Memorabilia 2.10.4
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Agesilaus 1.18
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 2.3.27, (Compare 5.5.14)
    5. (participial substantive, ὁ ὠνούμενος (ho ōnoúmenos)) the buyer, purchaser, owner
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, On Horsemanship 3.2, (compare Demosthenes 309.15, Plutarch, Cato the Younger 36)
      • 388 BCE, Aristophanes, Plutus 7
      • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Moralia 2.242D
      • 361 BCE – 291 BCE, Dinarchus, Collected Works 109.30
      • 412 BCE, Euripides, Helen 902
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Hiero 9.11
      • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Moralia 2.457D
      • 445 BCE – 380 BCE, Lysias, Collected Works 180.15
      • 470 BCE, Aeschylus, The Suppliants 336
  2. to farm public taxes or tolls, or rather to bid for them
    • 440 BCE – 390 BCE, Andocides, Collected Works 17.28
    • 445 BCE – 380 BCE, Lysias, Collected Works 108.26
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Ways and Means 4.19
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes 435.7
  3. to buy off, avert by giving hush money
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes 96.7
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes 990.17
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes 987.7
    • 125 CE – 200 CE, Lucian, Hermotimus 81
    • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Sertorius 6
    • 170 CE – 240 CE, Herodian, History of the Empire from the Death of Marcus 6.7
  4. to buy a person, to bribe
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes 309.15
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes 122.21, (Compare Plutarch, Philopoemen 15.322):
      ὠνεῖται καὶ διαφθείρει τινάς
      ōneîtai kaì diaphtheírei tinás
