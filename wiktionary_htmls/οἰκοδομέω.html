Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From οἰκοδόμος (oikodómos, “builder, architect”) +‎ -έω (-éō, denominative verbal suffix).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /oi̯.ko.do.mé.ɔː/ → /y.ko.ðoˈme.o/ → /i.ko.ðoˈme.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /oi̯.ko.do.mé.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /y.ko.doˈme.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /y.ko.ðoˈme.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /y.ko.ðoˈme.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /i.ko.ðoˈme.o/

Verb

[edit]

οἰκοδομέω • (oikodoméō)

  1. to build a house, build
    • 380 BCE, Plato, Gorgias 514B
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.21
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.114
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.186
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.101
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, History of Animals 623B.27
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Charmides 161E
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Charmides 165D
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.121
    • 440 BCE – 390 BCE, Andocides, On the Peace 7
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 7.11
    • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 372A
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.126–127
  2. (figuratively) to build or found upon
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cyropaedia 8.7.15
    • 421 BCE, Aristophanes, Peace 749
  3. (figuratively) to build up, edify, embolden
    • 53 CE – 55 CE, Paul the Apostle, First Epistle to the Corinthians 8:1
    • 53 CE – 55 CE, Paul the Apostle, First Epistle to the Corinthians 10:23
    • New Testament, First Epistle to the Thessalonians 5:11
    • 53 CE – 55 CE, Paul the Apostle, First Epistle to the Corinthians 8:10
