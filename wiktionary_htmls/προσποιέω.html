Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

προσ- (pros-) +‎ ποιέω (poiéō)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pros.poi̯.é.ɔː/ → /pros.pyˈe.o/ → /pros.piˈe.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pros.poi̯.é.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /pros.pyˈe.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /pros.pyˈe.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /pros.pyˈe.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /pros.piˈe.o/

Verb

[edit]

προσποιέω • (prospoiéō)

  1. to make over to
  2. (middle voice) to attach to oneself, win over
  3. to appropriate, pretend to, lay claim to
  4. to pretend, feign, affect, simulate
