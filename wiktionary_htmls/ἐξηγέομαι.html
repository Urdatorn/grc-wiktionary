Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐκ- (ek-) +‎ ἡγέομαι (hēgéomai).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ek.sɛː.ɡé.o.mai̯/ → /ek.siˈʝe.o.mɛ/ → /ek.siˈʝe.o.me/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ek.sɛː.ɡé.o.mai̯/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ek.se̝ˈɡe.o.mɛ/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ek.siˈʝe.o.mɛ/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ek.siˈʝe.o.me/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ek.siˈʝe.o.me/

Verb

[edit]

ἐξηγέομαι • (exēgéomai)

  1. to lead (the way)
  2. to lead (an army) or govern
  3. to dictate
  4. to prescribe, order
  5. to expound, interpret
  6. to relate, tell at length, explain
