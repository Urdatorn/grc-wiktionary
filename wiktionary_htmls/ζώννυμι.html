Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *yeh₃s- +‎ -νῡμι (-nūmi), related to Lithuanian júosta (“girdle”), Latvian jôsta (“girdle”), Avestan 𐬫𐬁𐬵- (yāh-, “girdle”), 𐬫𐬁𐬯𐬙𐬀 (yāsta, “girdled”), Albanian gjesh (“to gird”). See also ζώνη (zṓnē).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /zdɔ̌ːn.nyː.mi/ → /ˈzon.ny.mi/ → /ˈzo.ni.mi/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /zdɔ̌ːn.nyː.mi/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈzon.ny.mi/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈzon.ny.mi/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈzon.ny.mi/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈzo.ni.mi/

Verb

[edit]

ζώννῡμῐ • (zṓnnūmi)

  1. to gird; especially to gird round the loins in wrestling
  2. (mediopassive)
    1. to gird oneself, especially of athletes
    2. (with dative or accusative) to gird oneself with something
    3. (generally) to gird up one's loins for battle
  3. (passive voice)
    1. to be fastened by means of girths
    2. to be formed in belts and seams
