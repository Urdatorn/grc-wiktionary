Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • φώνειμι (phṓneimi) — Aeolic

Etymology

[edit]

From φωνή (phōnḗ, “sound”) +‎ -έω (-éō, denominative verbal suffix).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pʰɔː.né.ɔː/ → /ɸoˈne.o/ → /foˈne.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pʰɔː.né.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /pʰoˈne.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ɸoˈne.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /foˈne.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /foˈne.o/

Verb

[edit]

φωνέω • (phōnéō)

  1. to produce a sound or tone
  2. to speak loud or clearly; to speak, give utterance, voice (of men)
  3. to call by name, call
  4. to speak, tell of
