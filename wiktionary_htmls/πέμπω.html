Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Unclear. Possibly Pre-Greek.

Pronunciation

[edit]

Verb

[edit]

πέμπω • (pémpō)

  1. to send, dispatch
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 3.116:
      Ἕκτωρ δὲ προτὶ ἄστυ δύω κήρυκας ἔπεμπε
      Héktōr dè protì ástu dúō kḗrukas épempe
      And Hector dispatched two heralds to the city.
    • 472 BCE, Aeschylus, The Persians 34
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Ajax 739
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 7.15
    • 480 BCE – 406 BCE, Euripides, Fragments 506
    • 413 BCE, Euripides, Iphigenia in Tauris 1308
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 1.129
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 1.9.25
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Epistles 310d
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Septuagint, 2 Esdras 4.14
    1. (with accusative of place)
      • 406 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus at Colonus 1770:
        Θήβας δ' ἡμᾶς / τὰς ὠγυγίους πέμψον
        Thḗbas d' hēmâs / tàs ōgugíous pémpson
        Dispatch us to Ogygian Thebes
      • 429 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus the King 761
    2. (with adverbs)
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 16.445:
        αἴ κε ζὼν πέμψῃς Σαρπηδόνα ὃνδε δόμονδε, (...)
        aí ke zṑn pémpsēis Sarpēdóna hònde dómonde, (...)
        If you send Sarpedon to his house alive, (...)
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 18.452
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 9.461
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 19.281
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 24.418
    3. (with infinitive of purpose)
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 16.575–576:
        οἱ δ’ ἅμ’ Ἀχιλλῆϊ ῥηξήνορι πέμπον ἕπεσθαι
        Ἴλιον εἰς εὔπωλον
        hoi d’ hám’ Akhillêï rhēxḗnori pémpon hépesthai
        Ílion eis eúpōlon
        They dispatched [him] alongside Achilles, breaker of ranks, to accompany [him]
        to Ilion, abounding in foals
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 4.8
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 14.396
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 24.419
      • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Electra 406
    4. to nominate
  2. to send forth; to dismiss
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 24.780
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 4.5
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 7.227
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 15.74
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 23.315
    • 429 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus the King 1518
    1. to discharge, shoot
      • 750 BCE – 650 BCE, Hesiod, Theogony 716
      • 470 BCE, Aeschylus, The Suppliants 1005
      • 346 CE – c. 414 CE, Eunapius, Lives of the Sophists 495B
    2. (of words) to utter
      • 467 BCE, Aeschylus, Seven Against Thebes 443
      • 409 BCE, Sophocles, Philoctetes 846
      • 409 BCE, Sophocles, Philoctetes 1445
  3. to conduct, escort
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 1.390
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Pythian Ode 4.203
    • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, The Eumenides 12
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Women of Trachis 571
    • 409 BCE, Sophocles, Philoctetes 133
    1. (with πομπήν (pompḗn)) to conduct or take part in (a procession)
      • 458 BCE, Euripides, Electra 434
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.49
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 5.56

      • 445 BCE – 380 BCE, Lysias, Against Agoratus 80
      • 391 BCE, Aristophanes, Assemblywomen 757

      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, First Philippic 26
      • 342 BCE – 290 BCE, Menander 494
      • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Aemilius Paulus 32
      • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Demetrius 12
      • 170 CE – 250 CE, Philostratus, Life of Apollonius of Tyana 4.22
  4. to send as a gift
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 4.623
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 16.83
    • 409 BCE, Sophocles, Philoctetes 1429

    • 409 BCE, Sophocles, Philoctetes 1162
  6. (middle voice) to send for
    • 424 BCE, Euripides, Hecuba 977
    • 406 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus at Colonus 602
  7. (middle voice) to send in one's service; to cause to be sent
    • 429 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus the King 556
    • 125 CE – 200 CE, Lucian, Toxaris 14

Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Ancient Greek πέμπω.

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): /ˈpem.bo/
  • Hyphenation: πέ‧μπω
  • Hyphenation: πέμ‧πω

Verb

[edit]

πέμπω • (pémpo) active (past έπεμψα, passive πέμπομαι)

  1. (formal) to send
    Synonym: στέλνω (stélno)

Usage notes

[edit]
  • Ancient verb, chiefly found in compounds.
