Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

ἐπι- (epi-) +‎ ἄγω (ágō)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.pá.ɡɔː/ → /eˈpa.ɣo/ → /eˈpa.ɣo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.pá.ɡɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /eˈpa.ɡo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /eˈpa.ɣo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /eˈpa.ɣo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /eˈpa.ɣo/

Verb

[edit]

ἐπάγω • (epágō)

  1. to bring on, bring to, lead to
  2. to apply
  3. to march against
  4. to advance
  5. (middle voice) to draw in to oneself
  6. (middle voice) to call for one’s benefit
  7. (middle voice) to bring to oneself, procure
