Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Contracted form of ἁπλόος (haplóos).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ha.plûːs/ → /aˈplus/ → /aˈplus/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ha.plûːs/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /(h)aˈplus/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /aˈplus/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /aˈplus/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /aˈplus/

Adjective

[edit]

ἁπλοῦς • (haploûs) m (feminine ἁπλῆ, neuter ἁπλοῦν); first/second declension

  1. Attic form of ἁπλόος (haplóos)
