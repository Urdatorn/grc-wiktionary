Ancient Greek

[edit]
FWOTD – 5 August 2023

Etymology

[edit]

Borrowed from Latin pāx! (“peace! quiet! enough talking!”)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pǎːks/ → /paks/ → /paks/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pǎːks/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /paks/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /paks/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /paks/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /paks/

Interjection

[edit]

πᾱ́ξ! • (pā́x!)

  1. enough!, oh!, bah!, good!
    • 342 BCE – 290 BCE, Menander, Epitrepontes 987:
      πάξ. μὴ βλέπ᾿ εἰς τ[ὴν ψάλτριαν
      Therefore, Halt! No more ogling of the harpist!
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Herodas, 7.114 :
      εἶ σίνος, θῶ μιν. πάξ·
      eî sínos, thô min. páx;
      Give me your foot; slip it in the shoe. Good!
    • 342 BCE – 291 BCE, Diphilus, fragment 96.2
