Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *sh₂ew-. Cognate with Hittite [script needed] (suhhai, “to pour out”) and Tocharian B [Term?] (suwaṃ, “to rain”). Possibly related to Ὑάς (Huás).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /hy̌ː.ɔː/ → /ˈy.o/ → /ˈi.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /hy̌ː.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈ(h)y.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈy.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈy.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈi.o/

Verb

[edit]

ῡ̔́ω • (hū́ō)

  1. to rain
    • 423 BCE, Aristophanes, The Clouds 368
    • 423 BCE, Aristophanes, The Clouds 1118
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Herodas, Collected Works 7.46
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.13
    • 750 BCE – 650 BCE, Hesiod, Works and Days 488
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 12.25
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 14.457
    • 161 CE – 180 CE, Marcus Aurelius, Meditations 5.7
    • 6th century BC, Theognis of Megara, Elegies 26
    1. (with accusative of place) to rain on
      • 300 BCE – 250 BCE, Apollonius of Rhodes, Argonautica 2.1115
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 4.151
      • 110 CE – 180 CE, Pausanias Periegeta, Graeciae Descriptio 2.29.7
    2. (with cognate accusative)
      • 423 BCE, Aristophanes, The Clouds 1280
      • 125 CE – 200 CE, Lucian, A True Story 2.14
      • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Olympian Ode 7.50
    3. (with dative of mode)
      • 250 CE – 350 CE, Athenaeus, Deipnosophistae 8.333a
  2. (passive voice) to be drenched with rain
    • 375 BCE – 275 BCE, Alexis 62.8
    • 519 BCE – 422 BCE, Cratinus, Collected Works 52
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.193
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.13
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 3.10
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 6.131
    • 371 BCE – 287 BCE, Theophrastus, Enquiry into Plants 8.11.4
  3. (passive voice) to fall as rain
    • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Moralia 2.912a
    • 64 BCE – 24 CE, Strabo, Geography 14.2.10
