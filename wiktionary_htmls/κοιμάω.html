Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *ḱey- (“to lie down”). Related to κεῖμαι (keîmai, “to lie outstretched”).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /koi̯.má.ɔː/ → /cyˈma.o/ → /ciˈma.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /koi̯.má.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /kyˈma.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /cyˈma.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /cyˈma.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ciˈma.o/

Verb

[edit]

κοιμάω • (koimáō)

  1. to put to sleep, lull
  2. (middle voice and passive voice) to sleep
