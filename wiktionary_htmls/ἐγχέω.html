Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ἐγχείω (enkheíō) — Epic

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐν (en, “in, into”) +‎ χέω (khéō, “to pour”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /eŋ.kʰé.ɔː/ → /eɲˈçe.o/ → /eɲˈçe.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /eŋ.kʰé.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /eŋˈkʰe.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /eɲˈçe.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /eɲˈçe.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /eɲˈçe.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἐγχέω • (enkhéō)

  1. to pour in; to fill the cup
    1. (of dry things) to pour in, to shoot in
      1. to thrust in
    2. (metaphoric) to infuse, to instill
  2. (of cups) to fill by pouring in
