Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From χέρσος (khérsos, “dry land”) +‎ -αῖος (-aîos, adjective suffix).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /kʰer.sâi̯.os/ → /çerˈsɛ.os/ → /çerˈse.os/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /kʰer.sâi̯.os/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /kʰerˈsɛ.os/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /çerˈsɛ.os/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /çerˈse.os/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /çerˈse.os/

Adjective

[edit]

χερσαῖος • (khersaîos) m (feminine χερσαίᾱ, neuter χερσαῖον); first/second declension
χερσαῖος • (khersaîos) m or f (neuter χερσαῖον); second declension

  1. from or of dry land, living or found on dry land
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 7.119
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 4.192
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.68
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.123
    • 360 BCE, Plato, Timaeus 40a
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, On Marvellous Things Heard 842b7
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, History of Animals 2.505b
    • 360 BCE, Plato, The Sophist 223b
    • Palatine Anthology 9.14
    1. (of landsmen)
      • 425 BCE, Euripides, Andromache 457
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 7.67
      • 100 BCE – 1 BCE, Asclepiodotus, Tactics 1.1
    2. inland
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 704b
    3. (of voyages) traveling by land
      • Palatine Anthology 11.42
      • Palatine Anthology 4.3b.46
      • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Moralia 2.750b
    4. (military, of ground forces)
      • 467 BCE, Aeschylus, Seven Against Thebes 64
      • 315 BCE – 240 BCE, Aratus, 919

Antonyms

[edit]
  • (antonym(s) of “from or of dry land”): λιμναῖος (limnaîos), θᾰλᾰ́σσῐος (thalássios)/θᾰλᾰ́ττῐος (thaláttios)

Noun

[edit]

χερσαῖος • (khersaîos) f (genitive χερσαίου); second declension

  1. (geography) peninsula
    Synonym: χερσόνησος (khersónēsos)
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Lycophron, Alexandra 534
