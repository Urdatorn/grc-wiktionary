Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • καυχέομαι (kaukhéomai)

Etymology

[edit]

Unknown, probably from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰewH-.[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /kau̯.kʰá.o.mai̯/ → /kaɸˈxa.o.mɛ/ → /kafˈxa.o.me/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /kau̯.kʰá.o.mai̯/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /kaʍˈkʰa.o.mɛ/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /kaɸˈxa.o.mɛ/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /kafˈxa.o.me/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /kafˈxa.o.me/

Verb

[edit]

καυχᾰ́ομαι • (kaukháomai)

  1. to speak loud, to boast, brag, vaunt
    • Etymologicum Magnum 527.1
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Olympian Ode 9.38
    • 519 BCE – 422 BCE, Cratinus, The Laconians 1
    • 342 BCE – 290 BCE, Menander, Monostichoi 616
    • 60 BCE – 7 BCE, Dionysius of Halicarnassus, Roman Antiquities 8.30
    • 64 BCE – 24 CE, Strabo, Geography 13.1.27
    • 55 CE – 56 CE, Paul the Apostle, Second Epistle to the Corinthians 9:2
    • 110 BCE – 35 BCE, Philodemus, On Arrogance 35
    • Palatine Anthology 12.234
