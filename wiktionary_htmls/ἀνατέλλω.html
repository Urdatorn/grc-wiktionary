Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἀνα- (ana-, “up”) +‎ τέλλω (téllō, “to perform, accomplish, rise”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /a.na.tél.lɔː/ → /a.naˈtel.lo/ → /a.naˈte.lo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /a.na.tél.lɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /a.naˈtel.lo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /a.naˈtel.lo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /a.naˈtel.lo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /a.naˈte.lo/

Verb

[edit]

ἀνᾰτέλλω • (anatéllō)

  1. to raise up
  2. to cause to grow
  3. to give birth to
  4. to bring to light
  5. to rise (of the sun and moon)
  6. to grow (of hair)
