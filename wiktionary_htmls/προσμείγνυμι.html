Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • προσμίσγω (prosmísgō) — poetic, Ionic

Etymology

[edit]

προσ- (pros-) +‎ μείγνῡμι (meígnūmi), alternative form of μίγνῡμι (mígnūmi).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /proz.měː.ɡnyː.mi/ → /prozˈmi.ɣny.mi/ → /prozˈmi.ɣni.mi/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /proz.měː.ɡnyː.mi/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /prozˈmi.ɡny.mi/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /prozˈmi.ɣny.mi/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /prozˈmi.ɣny.mi/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /prozˈmi.ɣni.mi/

Verb

[edit]

προσμείγνῡμῐ • (prosmeígnūmi)

  1. to mingle or join to
  2. to approach
  3. to go against, meet in battle, engage (with), come to close quarters with
  4. to come or go close up to
  5. to put to shore at, land in
