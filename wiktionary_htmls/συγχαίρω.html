Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ξυγχαίρω (xunkhaírō)

Etymology

[edit]

From συν- (sun-) +‎ χαίρω (khaírō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /syŋ.kʰǎi̯.rɔː/ → /syɲˈçɛ.ro/ → /siɲˈçe.ro/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /syŋ.kʰǎi̯.rɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /syŋˈkʰɛ.ro/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /syɲˈçɛ.ro/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /syɲˈçe.ro/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /siɲˈçe.ro/

Verb

[edit]

συγχαίρω • (sunkhaírō)

  1. to congratulate
  2. to rejoice together with

Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Learned borrowing from Ancient Greek συγχαίρω. Morphologically, from συγ- (συν-) +‎ χαίρω.

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): /siŋˈçero/
  • Hyphenation: συγ‧χαί‧ρω

Verb

[edit]

συγχαίρω • (synchaíro) (past συγχάρηκα/συνεχάρην, passive —)

  1. to congratulate
  2. (literally) to rejoice together with (rare)[1]
