Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Participle of ὄλλυμι (óllumi, “destroy; lose”) by metrical lengthening.

Adjective

[edit]

οὐλόμενος • (oulómenos) m (feminine οὐλομένη, neuter οὐλόμενον); first/second declension

  1. (Epic, poetic) one of or to whom the word ὄλοιτο (óloito, “may he/she/it perish”) may be used, accursed, destructive, ruined, wretched
