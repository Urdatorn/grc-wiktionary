Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

τύραννος (túrannos) +‎ -έω (-éō)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ty.ran.né.ɔː/ → /ty.ranˈne.o/ → /ti.raˈne.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ty.ran.né.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ty.ranˈne.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ty.ranˈne.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ty.ranˈne.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ti.raˈne.o/

Verb

[edit]

τῠραννέω • (turannéō)

  1. (intransitive) to be a monarch, absolute ruler (optionally with the people or region ruled in the genitive case)
  2. (transitive) to govern, rule over as monarch
