Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἄξιος (áxios, “worthy”) +‎ -όω (-óō, factitive verb–forming suffix).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ak.si.ó.ɔː/ → /ak.siˈo.o/ → /ak.siˈo.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ak.si.ó.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ak.siˈo.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ak.siˈo.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ak.siˈo.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ak.siˈo.o/

Verb

[edit]

ᾰ̓ξῐόω • (axióō) (Attic, Ionic)

  1. to think or deem worthy
    1. (transitive) to think [with accusative ‘someone’] worthy, either in a good or bad sense [with genitive ‘of something’]
      • 431 BCE, Euripides, Medea 962
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 3.2.7
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 3.145
      • 400 BCE – 387 BCE, Plato, Apology 38A
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 9.111
      • 424 BCE, Euripides, Hecuba 366
      • 480 BCE – 411 BCE, Antiphon of Rhamnus, Collected Works 122.23
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Phaedo 103E
      1. (transitive) to esteem, honor
        • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Ajax 1114
        • 430 BCE, Euripides, Herakles' Children 918
        • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, Agamemnon 903
        • 408 BCE, Euripides, Orestes 1210
      2. (intransitive) to value at a certain price
        • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 917D
    2. (transitive) to think [with accusative ‘someone’] worthy [with infinitive ‘to do or be’]
      • 438 BCE, Euripides, Alcestis 572
      • 424 BCE, Aristophanes, The Knights 182
      • 5th century BC, Pherecrates, Good-For-Nothings 7
      • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Nemean Ode 10.73
      • 525 BCE – 455 BCE, Aeschylus, Prometheus Bound 240
      • 366 BCE – 348 BCE, Plato, Theaetetus 161D
      1. (transitive) to think fit, expect, require, demand that; compare Latin postulō
        • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.162
        • 480 BCE – 411 BCE, Antiphon of Rhamnus, Collected Works 118.20
        • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 2.89
        • 440 BCE – 390 BCE, Andocides, Collected Works 18.36
    3. (intransitive) to think one has a right [with infinitive ‘to do’]
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 1.42
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 7.15
      • 445 BCE – 380 BCE, Lysias, Collected Works 164.32
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 4.86
      1. (intransitive) to think fit, expect, consent, resolve, deign, dare [with infinitive ‘to do’]
        • 429 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus the King 944
        • 472 BCE, Aeschylus, The Persians 335
        • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, Agamemnon 1661
        • 400 BCE – 387 BCE, Plato, Hippias Minor 364D
        • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes 547.9:
          οἶμαι πάντας...φέρειν ἀξιοῦν
          oîmai pántas...phérein axioûn
        • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.20
        • 525 BCE – 455 BCE, Aeschylus, Prometheus Bound 215
        • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 1.102
        • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Oeconomicus 21.4
        • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 3.66
        • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, Agamemnon 370
        • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, The Eumenides 425
        • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.199
        • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 7.!6
      2. (transitive) to think, deem, hold
        • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 6.87
        • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 1.54
    4. (intransitive, transitive) to make a claim
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 4.58
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Politics 3.17.6
      • 200 BCE – 118 BCE, Polybius, Histories 39.1.7
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Memorabilia 3.11
      1. to hold an opinion
        • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes 312.6:
          ἐγὼ μὲν οὖν οὑτωσὶ περὶ τῆς τύχης ἀξιῶ
          egṑ mèn oûn houtōsì perì tês túkhēs axiô
        • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes 460.28:
          ἐγὼ μὲν οὐκ ἀξιῶ
          egṑ mèn ouk axiô
      2. (philosophy) to lay down, hold, maintain
        • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Prior Analytics 1.17.5
        • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Prior Analytics 1.24.2
        • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 3.43
