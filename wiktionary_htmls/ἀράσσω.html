Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ἀρᾰ́ττω (aráttō) — Attic

Etymology

[edit]

Unknown, but Beekes suggests an onomatopoeic origin, perhaps similar to that of ἄρᾰβος (árabos, “gnashing, rattling”). Possibly related to ῥάσσω (rhássō, “to strike, dash”).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /a.rás.sɔː/ → /aˈras.so/ → /aˈra.so/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /a.rás.sɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /aˈras.so/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /aˈras.so/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /aˈras.so/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /aˈra.so/

Verb

[edit]

ἀρᾰ́σσω • (arássō)

  1. to smite, dash in pieces
  2. to knock at the door
  3. (of rattling, crashing) to beat, strike
