Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • πάττω (páttō) — Attic

Etymology

[edit]

    From Proto-Indo-European *kʷeh₁t- (“to shake”). Cognate with Latin quatiō (“to shake”), Proto-Celtic *kʷāti- (“chaff, husks”) (whence Old Irish cáith), and possibly Proto-Tocharian *kät- (“to strew”).[1]

    Pronunciation

    • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈpas.so/
    • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈpas.so/
    • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈpas.so/
    • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈpa.so/

    Verb

    [edit]

    πάσσω • (pássō)

    1. to sprinkle
      Synonyms: πᾰλῠ́νω (palúnō), ῥαίνω (rhaínō), ῥᾰντῐ́ζω (rhantízō)
