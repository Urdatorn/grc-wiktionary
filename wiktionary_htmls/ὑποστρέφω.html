Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ῠ̔πο- (hupo-, “under”) +‎ στρέφω (stréphō, “to turn”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /hy.pos.tré.pʰɔː/ → /y.posˈtre.ɸo/ → /i.posˈtre.fo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /hy.pos.tré.pʰɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /(h)y.posˈtre.pʰo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /y.posˈtre.ɸo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /y.posˈtre.fo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /i.posˈtre.fo/

Verb

[edit]

ῠ̔ποστρέφω • (hupostréphō)

  1. to turn round about or back, guide or bring back
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 5.581
    • 416 BCE, Euripides, Herakles 736
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Women of Trachis 220
    1. to roll up
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Problems 895B.10
    2. (in passive) to revolve beneath
      • 315 BCE – 240 BCE, Aratus, Phaenomena 73
      • 315 BCE – 240 BCE, Aratus, Phaenomena 512
  2. (intransitive) to turn about, turn short around
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 12.71
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 7.211
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 9.14
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 3.24
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 11.446
    • 438 BCE, Euripides, Alcestis 1019
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 6.6.38
    • 480 BCE – 411 BCE, Antiphon of Rhamnus, First Tetralogy 4.5
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 11.567
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 4.129
    • 429 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus the King 728
    1. to return
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 8.301
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 4.120–124
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 4.140
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 18.23
      • 460 BCE – 370 BCE, Hippocrates, Epidemics 1.3
    2. to turn away
      • 407 BCE, Euripides, Iphigenia in Aulis 363
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 2.1.18
    3. (participle as adverb) reversely
      • 414 BCE, Aristophanes, The Birds 1283
