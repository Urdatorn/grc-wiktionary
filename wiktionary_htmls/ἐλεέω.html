Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἔλεος (éleos, “mercy”) +‎ -έω (-éō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.le.é.ɔː/ → /e.leˈe.o/ → /e.leˈe.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.le.é.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /e.leˈe.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /e.leˈe.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /e.leˈe.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /e.leˈe.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἐλεέω • (eleéō)

  1. to have pity on, take pity on, show mercy to
    • 480 BCE – 411 BCE, Antiphon of Rhamnus, Against the Stepmother for Poisoning 27
    • 446 BCE – 386 BCE, Aristophanes, The Acharnians 706
    • 421 BCE, Aristophanes, Peace 400
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Aphobus 1 53
    • 4th century BC, Eubulus, Collected Works 1 D.
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 14.279
    • 400 BCE – 387 BCE, Plato, Apology 34c
    • 100 BCE – 1 BCE, Plato, Axiochus 368d
    • Module:Quotations:449: attempt to compare number with nil
    • 409 BCE, Sophocles, Philoctetes 501
    • 100 CE – 200 CE, Xenophon of Ephesus, Ephesian Tale 5.4
