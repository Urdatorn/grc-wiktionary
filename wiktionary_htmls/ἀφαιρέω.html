Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἀπο- (apo-, “away”) +‎ αἱρέω (hairéō, “take”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /a.pʰai̯.ré.ɔː/ → /a.ɸɛˈre.o/ → /a.feˈre.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /a.pʰai̯.ré.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /a.pʰɛˈre.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /a.ɸɛˈre.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /a.feˈre.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /a.feˈre.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἀφαιρέω • (aphairéō)

  1. to take away [with accusative ‘something’, along with genitive or dative ‘from someone’; or with genitive or accusative ‘something’]
    1. (mathematics) to subtract
  2. (middle voice)
    1. to deprive of [with accusative ‘something’]
    2. to prevent someone [with infinitive ‘from doing’]
    3. with εἰς ἐλευθερίᾱν (eis eleutheríān): to free someone
    4. to claim
  3. (passive voice) to be robbed; to have something taken, to be deprived of [with accusative ‘something’]
