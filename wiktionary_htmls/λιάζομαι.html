Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Unknown; Beekes considers a connection with Gothic 𐌰𐍆𐌻𐌹𐌽𐌽𐌰𐌽 (aflinnan) plausible.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /li.áz.do.mai̯/ → /liˈa.zo.mɛ/ → /liˈa.zo.me/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /li.áz.do.mai̯/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /liˈa.zo.mɛ/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /liˈa.zo.mɛ/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /liˈa.zo.me/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /liˈa.zo.me/

Verb

[edit]

λῐάζομαι • (liázomai)

  1. to recoil, drop, collapse, sink, slip away, be turned aside
  2. to part, e.g., a pair of doors
