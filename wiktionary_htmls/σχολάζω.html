Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From σχολή (skholḗ) +‎ -άζω (-ázō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /skʰo.láz.dɔː/ → /sxoˈla.zo/ → /sxoˈla.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /skʰo.láz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /skʰoˈla.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /sxoˈla.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /sxoˈla.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /sxoˈla.zo/

Verb

[edit]

σχολάζω • (skholázō)

  1. to be at leisure, have spare time
  2. to have opportunity for [with dative]
  3. to have respite from
  4. to be unoccupied (of a place)
