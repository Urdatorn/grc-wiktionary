Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ἐπορμάω (epormáō) — Ionic

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐπι- (epi-) +‎ ὁρμάω (hormáō)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.pʰor.má.ɔː/ → /e.ɸorˈma.o/ → /e.forˈma.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.pʰor.má.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /e.pʰorˈma.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /e.ɸorˈma.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /e.forˈma.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /e.forˈma.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἐφορμάω • (ephormáō)

  1. to stir up, rouse against
  2. to urge
  3. to rush upon, attack [with dative or accusative]
  4. (passive voice and middle voice) to be eager or desire [with infinitive]
  5. (intransitive, middle voice) to rush furiously on, spring forward
