Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ὑπο- (hupo-, “under”) +‎ ἀκούω (akoúō, “to listen”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /hy.pa.kǔː.ɔː/ → /y.paˈku.o/ → /i.paˈku.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /hy.pa.kǔː.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /(h)y.paˈku.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /y.paˈku.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /y.paˈku.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /i.paˈku.o/

Verb

[edit]

ῠ̔πᾰκούω • (hupakoúō)

  1. (absolute) to listen, hearken, give ear
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 8.4
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 14.485
    • 7th–6th centuries BC, Homeric Hymn to Aphrodite 180
    • 422 BCE, Aristophanes, The Wasps 318
    • 389 BCE – 314 BCE, Aeschines, Against Ctesiphon 56
    1. to make answer when called
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 4.283
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 10.83
      • 438 BCE, Euripides, Alcestis 400
      • 422 BCE, Aristophanes, The Wasps 273
      • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Theocritus, Collected Works 13.59
      • 440 BCE – 390 BCE, Andocides, On the Mysteries 112
      • 425 BCE, Aristophanes, Lysistrata 878
      • 423 BCE, Aristophanes, The Clouds 360
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Agesilaus 3.4
      • 389 BCE – 314 BCE, Aeschines, Against Timarchus 49
      • 343 BCE, Demosthenes, On the False Embassy 266
    2. (with the genitive of object) to listen or hearken to, give ear to, heed, attend to
      • 366 BCE – 348 BCE, Plato, Theaetetus 162D
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cyropaedia 8.1.20
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 708D
      • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 459C
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Politics 1333A.18
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 5.98
      • 250 CE – 350 CE, Athenaeus, Deipnosophistae 6.247D
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 3.148
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 4.119
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Protagoras 325A
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cyropaedia 2.2.3
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 1.29
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 774B
  2. (special senses)
    1. (of porters) to answer a knock at the door
      • 400 BCE – 387 BCE, Plato, Crito 43A
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Phaedo 59E
      • New Testament, Acts of the Apostles 12:13
      • 371 BCE – 287 BCE, Theophrastus, Characters 4.9
      • 371 BCE – 287 BCE, Theophrastus, Characters 28.3
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Symposium 1.11
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Evergus and Mnesibulus 35
    2. (of a judge) to listen to a complaint
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cyropaedia 8.1.18
      • 4th century BC, Isaeus, Nicostratus 28
      • 343 BCE, Demosthenes, On the False Embassy 257
      • 390 BCE – 322 BCE, Hyperides, In Defence of Euxenippus 2
    3. (of dependents, subjects) to obey, submit to
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 3.101
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 4.56
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 6.82
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.61
      • 90 CE – 168 CE, Ptolemy, Tetrabiblos 35
    4. to answer one's expectations, to succeed
      • 125 CE – 200 CE, Lucian, Icaromenippus 10
      • 64 BCE – 24 CE, Strabo, Geography 9.1.23
    5. (figuratively) to be subject to the sun's rays
      • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Olympian Ode 3.24
      • 371 BCE – 287 BCE, Theophrastus, On the Causes of Plants 1.15.1
      • 371 BCE – 287 BCE, Theophrastus, On the Causes of Plants 5.4.2
      • 129 CE – 216 CE, Galen, Collected Works 18.68
    6. (of ailments) to yield, give way to a remedy
      • 460 BCE – 370 BCE, Hippocrates, Epidemics 3.8
      • 129 CE – 216 CE, Galen, Collected Works 6.354
      • 460 BCE – 370 BCE, Hippocrates, Prorrhetics 2.39
      • 50 CE – 150 CE, Soranus, Collected Works 1.122
      • 129 CE – 216 CE, Galen, Collected Works 6.439
      • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Theseus 1
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Archytas, Collected Works 1
      • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Moralia 2.802B
      • 460 BCE – 370 BCE, Hippocrates, Epidemics 4.45
      • 50 CE – 150 CE, Soranus, Collected Works 2.62
    7. to concede a point in a dispute
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Topics 161B.15
  3. to understand under
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Philebus 31C
    • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Moralia 2.23C
    • 110 BCE – 35 BCE, Philodemus, On Poems 5.35
    1. (grammar) to understand a word omitted
      • 50 CE – 250 CE, Apollonius Dyscolus, On Syntax 22.21
      • 50 CE – 250 CE, Apollonius Dyscolus, On Pronouns 68.15
