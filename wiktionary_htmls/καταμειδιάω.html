Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From κᾰτᾰ- (kata-, “against”) +‎ μειδᾰ́ω (meidáō, “smile”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ka.ta.meː.di.á.ɔː/ → /ka.ta.mi.ðiˈa.o/ → /ka.ta.mi.ðiˈa.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ka.ta.meː.di.á.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ka.ta.mi.diˈa.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ka.ta.mi.ðiˈa.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ka.ta.mi.ðiˈa.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ka.ta.mi.ðiˈa.o/

Verb

[edit]

κᾰτᾰμειδῐᾰ́ω • (katameidiáō)

  1. to smile at, despise
    • 75 CE, Josephus, The Jewish War 3.7.33
