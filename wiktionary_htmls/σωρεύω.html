Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From σωρός (sōrós, “heap”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /sɔː.rěu̯.ɔː/ → /soˈre.βo/ → /soˈre.vo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /sɔː.rěu̯.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /soˈre.wo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /soˈre.βo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /soˈre.vo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /soˈre.vo/

Verb

[edit]

σωρεύω • (sōreúō)

  1. to heap one thing on another, pile up
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Rhetoric 2.15,2
  2. (with genitive) to heap with something
    • 200 BCE – 118 BCE, Polybius, The Histories 16.11,4
