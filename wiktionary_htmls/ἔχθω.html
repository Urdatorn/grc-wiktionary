Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From the same root as ἔχθος (ékhthos, “hate”). This etymology is incomplete. You can help Wiktionary by elaborating on the origins of this term.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ékʰ.tʰɔː/ → /ˈex.θo/ → /ˈex.θo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ékʰ.tʰɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈekʰ.tʰo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈex.θo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈex.θo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈex.θo/

Verb

[edit]

ἔχθω • (ékhthō)

  1. to hate
