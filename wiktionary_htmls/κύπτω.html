Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Related to, and perhaps derived from, κυφός (kuphós, “bent forward, hunchbacked”), which Beekes suspects is of Pre-Greek origin; see there for more.[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /kýp.tɔː/ → /ˈcyp.to/ → /ˈcip.to/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /kýp.tɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈkyp.to/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈcyp.to/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈcyp.to/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈcip.to/

Verb

[edit]

κύπτω • (kúptō)

  1. to bend forward, stoop down
  2. to hang one's head from shame
  3. to bow down under a burden
