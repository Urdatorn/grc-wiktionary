Ancient Greek

[edit]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /a.lé.wo.mai̯/ → /aˈle.o.mɛ/ → /aˈle.o.me/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /a.lé.wo.mai̯/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /aˈle.o.mɛ/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /aˈle.o.mɛ/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /aˈle.o.me/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /aˈle.o.me/

Etymology 1

[edit]

From the same root as the synonymous ἀλεείνω (aleeínō) and as ἀλέᾱ (aléā, “avoiding, escape”).

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ἀλεῦμαι (aleûmai), ἀλέυομαι (aléuomai)

Verb

[edit]

ἀλέομαι • (aléomai)

  1. to shun, avoid
  2. to flee from

Verb

[edit]

ἀλέομαι • (aléomai)

  1. first-person singular present mediopassive indicative of ἀλέω (aléō, “to grind, bruise”)
