Ancient Greek

  • IPA(key): /pêː.sis/ → /ˈpi.sis/ → /ˈpi.sis/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pêː.sis/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈpi.sis/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈpi.sis/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈpi.sis/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈpi.sis/

Etymology 1

[edit]

From πείθω (peíthō, “I persuade”) +‎ -σῐς (-sis). Compare the inherited formation, πῐ́στῐς (pístis).

Noun

[edit]

πεῖσῐς • (peîsis) f (genitive πείσεως); third declension

  1. persuasion

Etymology 2

[edit]

From πάσχω (páskhō), πείσομαι (peísomai, “I feel, suffer”) +‎ -σῐς (-sis).

Noun

[edit]

πεῖσῐς • (peîsis) f (genitive πείσεως); third declension

  1. affection, susceptibility
