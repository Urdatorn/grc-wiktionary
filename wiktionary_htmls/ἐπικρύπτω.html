Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐπι- (epi-, “on, upon”) +‎ κρύπτω (krúptō, “hide”)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.pi.krýp.tɔː/ → /e.piˈkryp.to/ → /e.piˈkrip.to/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.pi.krýp.tɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /e.piˈkryp.to/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /e.piˈkryp.to/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /e.piˈkryp.to/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /e.piˈkrip.to/

Verb

[edit]

ἐπῐκρύπτω • (epikrúptō)

  1. throw a cloak over, conceal, disguise, with concealment or secrecy, conceal a thing from one, disguise, conceal one's purpose
