Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • σμῖλα (smîla)

Etymology

[edit]

The word has been connected with Proto-Germanic *smiþaz (“smith”); however, this etymology is shaky. Perhaps from Proto-Indo-European *smī- (“to cut, hew”). Alternatively, a Pre-Greek etymology might be considered.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /zmǐː.lɛː/ → /ˈzmi.li/ → /ˈzmi.li/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /zmǐː.lɛː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈzmi.le̝/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈzmi.li/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈzmi.li/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈzmi.li/

Noun

[edit]

σμῑ́λη • (smī́lē) f (genitive σμῑ́λης); first declension

  1. A tool for cutting or carving: knife, chisel

Greek

[edit]

Noun

[edit]

σμίλη • (smíli) f (plural σμίλες)

  1. chisel
    Synonym: σκαρπέλο (skarpélo)

Declension

[edit]
Declension of σμίλη
singular plural
nominative σμίλη • σμίλες •
genitive σμίλης • σμίλων •
accusative σμίλη • σμίλες •
vocative σμίλη • σμίλες •
The genitive σμιλών (smilón) is less common.

Related terms
