Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From κῦμᾰ (kûma) +‎ -ῐον (-ion).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /kyː.má.ti.on/ → /cyˈma.ti.on/ → /ciˈma.ti.on/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /kyː.má.ti.on/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /kyˈma.ti.on/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /cyˈma.ti.on/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /cyˈma.ti.on/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ciˈma.ti.on/

Noun

[edit]

κῡμᾰ́τῐον • (kūmátion) n (genitive κῡμᾰτῐ́ου); second declension

  1. a small cyma
    • Inscriptiones Graecae, 160 37
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Septuagint, Exodus 25.11.24
    1. the volute of the Ionic capital
      • Vitruvius 4.1
