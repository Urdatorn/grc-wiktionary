Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Later form of δῐᾰχέω (diakhéō). (This etymology is missing or incomplete. Please add to it, or discuss it at the Etymology scriptorium.)

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ði.aˈçy.no/ → /ði.aˈçi.no/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /di.aˈkʰy.no/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ði.aˈçy.no/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ði.aˈçy.no/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ði.aˈçi.no/

Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • διαχέω (diachéo) (more formal but common)

Etymology

[edit]

From διαχέω (diachéo) with metaplasm according to the pattern χέω (khéō) > χύνω (khúnō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): /ðʝaˈçi.no/, /ði̯aˈçi.no/
  • Hyphenation: δια‧χύ‧νω
  • Old Hyphenation: δι‧α‧χύ‧νω

Verb

[edit]

διαχύνω • (diachýno) (past διέχυσα, passive διαχύνομαι, p‑past διαχύθηκα, ppp διαχυμένος)

  1. less formal variant of διαχέω (diachéo)
