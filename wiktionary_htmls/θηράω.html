Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From θήρ (thḗr, “beast of prey”) +‎ -άω (-áō), from Proto-Hellenic *kʷʰḗr, from Proto-Indo-European *ǵʰwer- (“wild”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /tʰɛː.rǎː.ɔː/ → /θiˈra.o/ → /θiˈra.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /tʰɛː.rǎː.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /tʰe̝ˈra.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /θiˈra.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /θiˈra.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /θiˈra.o/

Verb

[edit]

θηράω • (thēráō)

  1. to hunt
    Synonym: θηρεύω (thēreúō)
