Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἔρις (éris, “strife, quarrel”) +‎ -ῐ́ζω (-ízō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.ríz.dɔː/ → /eˈri.zo/ → /eˈri.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.ríz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /eˈri.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /eˈri.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /eˈri.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /eˈri.zo/

Verb

[edit]

ἐρίζω • (erízō)

  1. to struggle, quarrel, challenge
