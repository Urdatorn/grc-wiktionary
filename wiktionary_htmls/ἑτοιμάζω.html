Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἑτοῖμος (hetoîmos, “prepared”) +‎ -άζω (-ázō, denominative verb suffix).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /he.toi̯.máz.dɔː/ → /e.tyˈma.zo/ → /e.tiˈma.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /he.toi̯.máz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /(h)e.tyˈma.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /e.tyˈma.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /e.tyˈma.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /e.tiˈma.zo/

Verb

[edit]

ἑτοιμάζω • (hetoimázō)

  1. to get ready, prepare
    • 438 BCE, Euripides, Alcestis 364
    • 430 BCE, Euripides, Herakles' Children 472
    • 423 BCE, Euripides, The Suppliants 454
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 6.95
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 1.118
    • New Testament, Revelation 8:6
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Septuagint, Isaiah 40.3
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Women of Trachis 361
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 2.7
  2. (middle voice) to cause to be prepared
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 8.24
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 10.571
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 13.184
    1. to prepare for oneself
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Aristocrates 209
      • 341 BCE – 270 BCE, Epicurus, Vatican Sayings 30
      • Metrodorus, Fragments 53
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 1.58
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 4.77
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cyropaedia 3.3.5
    2. to prepare oneself, make oneself ready
      • 200 BCE – 118 BCE, Polybius, The Histories 3.105.11
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Apology 8
