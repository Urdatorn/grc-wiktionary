Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐκ- (ek-, “out”) +‎ λέγω (légō, “to choose”). Exact cognate in form and meaning to Latin ēligō (“to choose”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.klé.ɡɔː/ → /eˈkle.ɣo/ → /eˈkle.ɣo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.klé.ɡɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /eˈkle.ɡo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /eˈkle.ɣo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /eˈkle.ɣo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /eˈkle.ɣo/

Verb

[edit]

ἐκλέγω • (eklégō)

  2. to pick or single out, to choose
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 4.59
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Hellenica 1.6.19
    • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 535A
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 811A
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.199
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 3.38
    • 385 BCE – 380 BCE, Plato, Symposium 198D
    • 436 BCE – 338 BCE, Isocrates, Evagoras 58
    1. (in middle voice) to pull out one's gray hairs
      • 424 BCE, Aristophanes, The Knights 908
  3. to levy taxes or tribute
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 8.44
    • 440 BCE – 390 BCE, Andocides, On the Mysteries 92
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Timotheus 49
    • 371 BCE – 287 BCE, Theophrastus, Characters 6.4
    • 389 BCE – 314 BCE, Aeschines, Collected Works 3.113
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Hellenica 1.1.22
