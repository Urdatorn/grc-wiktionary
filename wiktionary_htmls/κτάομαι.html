Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • κτέομαι (ktéomai) — Ionic
  • πάομαι (páomai) — Doric, Aeolic, Boeotian, Arcadian, Cypriot

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *tk-éh₁- (“acquisition”), from Proto-Indo-European *tek- (“to take by the hand; to receive, obtain”).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ktá.o.mai̯/ → /ˈkta.o.mɛ/ → /ˈkta.o.me/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ktá.o.mai̯/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈkta.o.mɛ/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈkta.o.mɛ/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈkta.o.me/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈkta.o.me/

Verb

[edit]

κτᾰ́ομαι • (ktáomai)

  1. (transitive) to get, obtain, acquire, gain, win
  2. (transitive, of consequences) to bring on oneself, incur
  3. (transitive, perfect and pluperfect) to have acquired, have, own, possess
