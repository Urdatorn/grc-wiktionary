Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • σκεδάω (skedáō)

Etymology

[edit]

Apparently from Proto-Indo-European *(s)kd-n-eh₂ (“to split”); Beekes cites Avestan 𐬯𐬗𐬌𐬥𐬛𐬀𐬌𐬌𐬈𐬌𐬙𐬌 (scindaiieiti, “breaks, destroys”) as a particularly close cognate.[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ske.dán.nyː.mi/ → /sceˈðan.ny.mi/ → /sceˈða.ni.mi/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ske.dán.nyː.mi/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /skeˈdan.ny.mi/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /sceˈðan.ny.mi/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /sceˈðan.ny.mi/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /sceˈða.ni.mi/

Verb

[edit]

σκεδάννυμι • (skedánnumi)

  1. (active voice) to scatter, disperse
