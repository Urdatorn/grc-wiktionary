Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From σμάω (smáō, “to wipe, clean”), with a velar enlargement -χ (-kh).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /zmɛ̌ː.kʰɔː/ → /ˈzmi.xo/ → /ˈzmi.xo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /zmɛ̌ː.kʰɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈzme̝.kʰo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈzmi.xo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈzmi.xo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈzmi.xo/

Verb

[edit]

σμήχω • (smḗkhō)

  1. to wipe off by help of soap or unguent
  2. to clear off by help of lotions or salves
  3. to purge away
  4. to soap a person, wash him with soap or unguent
