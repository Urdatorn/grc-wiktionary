Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *slewk-, *slewg- (“to swallow”). Cognates are found in Celtic and Germanic, like in Welsh llyncu (“to swallow”) and in Middle High German slucken (“to gobble, swallow”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /lýz.dɔː/ → /ˈly.zo/ → /ˈli.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /lýz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈly.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈly.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈly.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈli.zo/

Verb

[edit]

λύζω • (lúzō)

  1. to hiccup
  2. to sob violently, from fear or cold
