Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From *ϝεμέω (*weméō), from Proto-Indo-European *wemh₁- (“to vomit”). Cognate with Latin vomō and Sanskrit वमति (vamati).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.mé.ɔː/ → /eˈme.o/ → /eˈme.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.mé.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /eˈme.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /eˈme.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /eˈme.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /eˈme.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἐμέω • (eméō)

  1. to vomit, throw up, be sick
    • 446 BCE – 386 BCE, Aristophanes, The Acharnians 587
    • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, The Eumenides 184
    • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, The Eumenides 730
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.133
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 7.88
    • 460 BCE – 370 BCE, Hippocrates, On Joints 40
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 15.11
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 4.8.20
