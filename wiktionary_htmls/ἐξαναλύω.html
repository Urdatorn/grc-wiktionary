Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐκ- (ek-, “off, away”) +‎ ᾰ̓νᾰλῡ́ω (analū́ō, “unsettle, unloose”)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ek.sa.na.ly̌ː.ɔː/ → /ek.sa.naˈly.o/ → /ek.sa.naˈli.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ek.sa.na.ly̌ː.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ek.sa.naˈly.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ek.sa.naˈly.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ek.sa.naˈly.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ek.sa.naˈli.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἐξᾰνᾰλῡ́ω • (exanalū́ō)

  1. to set quite free
  2. to resolve into its elements
