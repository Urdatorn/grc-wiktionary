Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐκ (ek, “out”) + χέω (khéō, “I pour”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ekʰ.kʰé.ɔː/ → /ekˈçe.o/ → /ekˈçe.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ekʰ.kʰé.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ekˈkʰe.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ekˈçe.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ekˈçe.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ekˈçe.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἐκχέω • (ekkhéō)

  1. to pour out
    1. to spill
    2. to utter (of words)
    3. to squander, waste
    4. to throw down
    5. to shed
  2. (passive voice) to pour out, stream forth
    1. to be cast away, to be forgotten
    2. to give oneself up to emotion, to be overjoyed
    3. to lie languidly
    4. to extend
