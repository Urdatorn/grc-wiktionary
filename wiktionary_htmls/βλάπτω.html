Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

If not Pre-Greek, perhaps from Proto-Indo-European *ml̥pyéti extended from *(s)mel- (“false, bad”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /bláp.tɔː/ → /ˈβlap.to/ → /ˈvlap.to/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /bláp.tɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈblap.to/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈβlap.to/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈvlap.to/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈvlap.to/

Verb

[edit]

βλάπτω • (bláptō)

  1. to disable, to hinder
  2. to harm, to hurt, to damage
    Synonym: σῑ́νομαι (sī́nomai)

Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • βλάφτω (vláfto) (familiar form)

Etymology

[edit]

Probably borrowed (in this form) from Ancient Greek βλάπτω (bláptō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): /ˈvla.pto/
  • Hyphenation: βλά‧πτω

Verb

[edit]

βλάπτω • (vlápto) (past έβλαψα, passive βλάπτομαι)

  1. (transitive) to damage, harm
  2. (transitive) to cause damage (especially to health)

Usage notes

[edit]
  • 3rd singular active present indicative: also used as a negative impersonal verb: δε(ν) βλάπτει (“it won't hurt...”)
  • See passive perfect participle βλαμμένος (vlamménos) for different meanings.
