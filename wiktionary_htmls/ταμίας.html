Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ταμίης (tamíēs) — Epic, Ionic

Etymology

[edit]

A nominal formation from τᾰμεῖν (tameîn, “to cut up, divide”), active infinitive aorist form of τέμνω (témnō, “to cut, hew”), though the exact morphological details are unclear. May be analyzed as τέμνω (témnō) +‎ -ῐ́ᾱς (-íās).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ta.mí.aːs/ → /taˈmi.as/ → /taˈmi.as/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ta.mí.aːs/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /taˈmi.as/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /taˈmi.as/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /taˈmi.as/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /taˈmi.as/

Noun

[edit]

τᾰμῐ́ᾱς • (tamíās) m (genitive τᾰμῐ́ου); first declension

  1. steward, dispenser

Greek

[edit]

Noun

[edit]

ταμίας • (tamías) m or f (plural ταμίες)

  1. cashier, teller, bursar, treasurer (sometimes a colloquial feminine genitive form)

Declension

[edit]
Declension of ταμίας
singular plural
nominative ταμίας • ταμίες •
genitive ταμία •,  ταμίας • ταμιών •
accusative ταμία • ταμίες •
vocative ταμία • ταμίες •
The second genitive singular is a colloquial feminine form.

Synonyms

[edit]
  • (bank clerk): τραπεζικός m (trapezikós)

Related terms

[edit]
  • see: ταμείο (tameío, “cashier's desk, checkout”)
