Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]
PIE word
*wek(ʷ)speros

From Proto-Hellenic *wésperos, from Proto-Indo-European *wek(ʷ)speros (“evening”). Cognates include Latin vesper, Old Armenian գիշեր (gišer), and Old Church Slavonic вєчєръ (večerŭ).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /hés.pe.ros/ → /ˈes.pe.ros/ → /ˈes.pe.ros/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /hés.pe.ros/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈ(h)es.pe.ros/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈes.pe.ros/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈes.pe.ros/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈes.pe.ros/
  • Hyphenation: ἕσ‧πε‧ρος

Adjective

[edit]

ἕσπερος • (hésperos) m or f (neuter ἕσπερον); second declension

  1. of or at evening
  2. western

Descendants

[edit]
  • → Albanian: dhespër (or from Latin vesper)

Noun

[edit]

ἕσπερος • (hésperos) m (genitive ἑσπέρου); second declension

  1. evening
  2. the evening star, Venus (shortened form of ἕσπερος ἀστήρ (hésperos astḗr))
