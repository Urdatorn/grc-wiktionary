Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἀπο- (apo-) +‎ στερέω (steréō)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /a.pos.te.ré.ɔː/ → /a.pos.teˈre.o/ → /a.pos.teˈre.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /a.pos.te.ré.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /a.pos.teˈre.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /a.pos.teˈre.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /a.pos.teˈre.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /a.pos.teˈre.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἀποστερέω • (aposteréō)

  1. to rob, despoil, bereave or defraud [with accusative ‘someone’, along with genitive or accusative ‘of something’]
  2. to defraud, cheat
  3. to detach, withdraw oneself
