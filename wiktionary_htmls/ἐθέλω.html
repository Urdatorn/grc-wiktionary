Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • θέλω (thélō)

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *h₁gʷʰel- (“to wish”). Cognates include Proto-Slavic *želěti (“to wish, want”) (whence Old Church Slavonic желѣти (želěti)).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.tʰé.lɔː/ → /eˈθe.lo/ → /eˈθe.lo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.tʰé.lɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /eˈtʰe.lo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /eˈθe.lo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /eˈθe.lo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /eˈθe.lo/

Verb

[edit]

ἐθέλω • (ethélō)

  1. usually weaker than βούλομαι (boúlomai), of consent rather than desire: (intransitive) to be willing, to wish [with infinitive ‘to do something’]

    • 428 BCE, Euripides, Hippolytus 1327
    • 70 CE – 110 CE, The Gospel of Matthew 7:12
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.3
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.71
    • 331 CE – 363 CE, Julian, Orations 1.132a
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 1.554
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 7.182
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 7.364
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 9.397
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 19.274
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 23.894
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 3.272
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 3.324
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 11.566
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 14.172
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 5.50
    1. (Koine) to want, wish
      • 70 CE – 110 CE, The Gospel of Matthew 12:38
      • 70 CE – 110 CE, The Gospel of Matthew 20:21
      • The Gospel of Mark 3:13
      • The Gospel of Mark 7:24
      • 50 CE – 100 CE, The Gospel of John 5:6
      • 50 CE – 100 CE, The Gospel of John 9:27
  2. (with negative) to not care to, be unable
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 13.106
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 17.702
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 21.366
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 8.223
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 8.316
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Phaedrus 230d
    • 7th–6th centuries BC, Homeric Hymn to Demeter 45
  3. (of inanimate objects)
    1. expressing future; to will, shall
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.109
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.11
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 7.49

    2. to be naturally disposed; to be wont, accustomed
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Meteorology 347a5
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Metaphysics 1013b27
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, History of Animals 575a28
