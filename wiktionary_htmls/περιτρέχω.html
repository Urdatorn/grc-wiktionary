Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From περῐ- (peri-) +‎ τρέχω (trékhō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pe.ri.tré.kʰɔː/ → /pe.riˈtre.xo/ → /pe.riˈtre.xo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pe.ri.tré.kʰɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /pe.riˈtre.kʰo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /pe.riˈtre.xo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /pe.riˈtre.xo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /pe.riˈtre.xo/

Verb

[edit]

περῐτρέχω • (peritrékhō)

  1. to run about everywhere, run round and round
    see quotation at περιδραμεῖται (peridrameîtai)
  2. to surround

Inflection

[edit]

This verb's missing forms are often suppleted with forms from περιδραμεῖν (peridrameîn) in the future, aorist, and perfect.
