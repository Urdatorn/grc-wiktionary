Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From κόρυς (kórus, “helmet”) +‎ -σσω (-ssō); more formally from */korútʰ-jɔː/ with */tʰj/ becoming /ss/ by regular sound change.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ko.rýs.sɔː/ → /koˈrys.so/ → /koˈri.so/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ko.rýs.sɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /koˈrys.so/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /koˈrys.so/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /koˈrys.so/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /koˈri.so/

Verb

[edit]

κορύσσω • (korússō) (chiefly Epic)

  1. to equip with a helmet
  2. to arm, equip (generally)
