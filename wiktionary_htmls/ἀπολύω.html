Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἀπο- (apo-, “from”) +‎ λῡ́ω (lū́ō, “to loosen”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /a.po.ly̌ː.ɔː/ → /a.poˈly.o/ → /a.poˈli.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /a.po.ly̌ː.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /a.poˈly.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /a.poˈly.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /a.poˈly.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /a.poˈli.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἀπολῡ́ω • (apolū́ō)

  1. to loose from

    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 12.420
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 3.392
    1. to set free from, release or relieve from

      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cyropaedia 8.3.47
      • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 365A
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Phaedo 64E
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Politics 1.9.8
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 1.70
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 7.56
      1. (law) to acquit
        • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 9.88
        • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 6.6.15
        • 422 BCE, Aristophanes, The Wasps 571
        • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.174
        • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 1.95
        • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 1.128
        • 422 BCE, Aristophanes, The Wasps 988–1000
        • 445 BCE – 380 BCE, Lysias, Collected Works 159.43
  2. (sole use in the Iliad) to let go free on receipt of ransom
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 1.95
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 24.115
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 22.50
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Hellenica 4.8.21
    1. to let go, let alone, leave
      • 460 BCE – 370 BCE, Hippocrates, The Coan Praenotions 210
  3. to discharge or disband an army, dismiss
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Hellenica 6.5.21, (compare Plato, Phaedo 67A)
    • 446 BCE – 386 BCE, Aristophanes, The Acharnians 1155
    1. to divorce a wife
      • New Testament, Matth. 1:19
      • 36 BCE – 30 BCE, Diodorus Siculus, Historical Library 12.18
    2. to do away with, remove
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Leptines 47, (compare Antiphon of Rhamnus, Collected Works 115.20)
    3. to discharge or pay a debt
      • 386 BCE – 367 BCE, Plato, Cratylus 417B
    4. to dismiss a charge
      • 390 BCE – 322 BCE, Hyperides, In Defence of Euxenippus 47
      • 480 BCE – 411 BCE, Antiphon of Rhamnus, Collected Works 115.20
  4. to sell
    • 480 BCE – 411 BCE, Antiphon of Rhamnus, Collected Works 131.39
    • 4th century BC, Isaeus, Collected Works 59.32
  5. (middle voice) to redeem
    • Opp. C. 3.128
    1. to do away with calumnies against one
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 8.87
      • 400 BCE – 387 BCE, Plato, Apology 37B
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 5.75
      • 352 BCE, Demosthenes, For the Megalopolitans 2

    2. to acquit of
      • 480 BCE – 411 BCE, Antiphon of Rhamnus, Collected Works 119.12
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 637C
      1. to release from
        • 386 BCE – 367 BCE, Plato, Menexenus 245A
    3. to depart
      • 442 BCE, Sophocles, Antigone 1314
  6. (passive voice) to be released, let off
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 4.84
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 2.8
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 1.70

    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Phaedo 113D:
      καὶ ἐκεῖ οἰκοῦσι τε καὶ καθαιρόμενοι τῶν τε ἀδικημάτων διδόντες δίκας ἀπολύονται

    1. to be separated, get clear, part
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 1.49
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Metaphysics 6.6.5
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Physics 1.2.6
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Generation of Animals 3.3.7
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, History of Animals 2.1.41
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, History of Animals 4.8.7
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, History of Animals 4.9.2
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Fragments 300
      1. to depart
        • 442 BCE, Sophocles, Antigone 1268
    2. (of a child) to be brought forth
      • 460 BCE – 370 BCE, Hippocrates, Collected Works 261.49
      • 460 BCE – 370 BCE, Hippocrates, Collected Works 1013E
    3. to be annulled
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Nicomachean Ethics 8.3.3

Synonyms

Related terms
