Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐκ- (ek-, “completely”) +‎ τειχῐ́ζω (teikhízō, “wall, fortify”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ek.teː.kʰíz.dɔː/ → /ek.tiˈçi.zo/ → /ek.tiˈçi.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ek.teː.kʰíz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ek.tiˈkʰi.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ek.tiˈçi.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ek.tiˈçi.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ek.tiˈçi.zo/

Verb

[edit]

ἐκτειχῐ́ζω • (ekteikhízō)

  1. to fortify completely, to build it from the ground
