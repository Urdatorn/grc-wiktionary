Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • βλῐ́ττω (blíttō) — Attic
  • βλίζω (blízō)

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Hellenic *mlíťťō, from Proto-Indo-European *mlít-ye-ti, from *mélit (“honey”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /blís.sɔː/ → /ˈβlis.so/ → /ˈvli.so/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /blís.sɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈblis.so/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈβlis.so/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈvlis.so/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈvli.so/

Verb

[edit]

βλῐ́σσω • (blíssō)

  1. to cut off combs of honey, take honey, squeeze honey
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Fragments 856
    • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 564E
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, History of Animals 5.22.9
    1. (figurative) to squeeze people
      • 424 BCE, Aristophanes, The Knights 794
      • 425 BCE, Aristophanes, Lysistrata 475
    2. (figurative) to rub, fondle
      • Erot., Fr. 16
