Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ὁρμή (hormḗ, “start, impetus, rush, attack, effort”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /hor.má.ɔː/ → /orˈma.o/ → /orˈma.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /hor.má.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /(h)orˈma.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /orˈma.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /orˈma.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /orˈma.o/

Verb

[edit]

ὁρμάω • (hormáō)

  1. (active voice) to set in motion, urge on, inspire
  2. (active voice, middle voice, passive voice) to start [with infinitive]
  3. to rush, hasten [with genitive or ἐπί (epí, + accusative) or εἰς (eis, + accusative) ‘at, against’]
