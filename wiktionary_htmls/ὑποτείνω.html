Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ῠ̔πο- (hupo-, “below”) +‎ τείνω (teínō, “to stretch, spread”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /hy.po.těː.nɔː/ → /y.poˈti.no/ → /i.poˈti.no/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /hy.po.těː.nɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /(h)y.poˈti.no/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /y.poˈti.no/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /y.poˈti.no/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /i.poˈti.no/

Verb

[edit]

ὑποτείνω • (hupoteínō)

  1. to stretch under, put under
  2. (geometry) to extend under, subtend
  3. to strain, pull hard
  4. to hold out hopes, offer
  5. to lay or put before one, present, suggest
