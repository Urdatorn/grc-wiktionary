Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From δῐᾰ- (dia-) +‎ τῐ́θημῐ (títhēmi, “put, place”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /di.a.tí.tʰɛː.mi/ → /ði.aˈti.θi.mi/ → /ði.aˈti.θi.mi/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /di.a.tí.tʰɛː.mi/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /di.aˈti.tʰe̝.mi/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ði.aˈti.θi.mi/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ði.aˈti.θi.mi/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ði.aˈti.θi.mi/

Verb

[edit]

δῐᾰτῐ́θημῐ • (diatíthēmi)

  1. (active voice) place separately, arrange each in their own places, distribute
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.132
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 7.39
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Memorabilia 2.1.27
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, History of Animals 8.4.3
    1. (with an adverb) to manage well or ill
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 6.15
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes 19.88
      1. (of persons) to handle or treat well or ill
        • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 3.155
        • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 6.57
        • 445 BCE – 380 BCE, Lysias, Collected Works 18.23
      2. to dispose one in such a manner
        • 436 BCE – 338 BCE, Isocrates, Collected Works 5.80
        • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes 18.168
        • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes 20.22
        • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Rhetoric 1.2.3
        • 366 BCE – 348 BCE, Plato, Theaetetus 151C
        • 436 BCE – 338 BCE, Isocrates, Collected Works 8.14
        • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Politics 5.2.4
        • 385 BCE – 380 BCE, Plato, Symposium 207C
    2. to set forth, recite
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Charmides 162D
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 658D
      1. to describe
        • 64 BCE – 24 CE, Strabo, Geography 1.1.16
  2. (middle voice) to arrange as one likes, to dispose of
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cyropaedia 5.2.7
    • 436 BCE – 338 BCE, Isocrates, Collected Works 12.140
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes 2.16
    • 125 CE – 200 CE, Lucian, How to Write History 51, (Compare On Salaried Posts in Great Houses 25)
    • 200 BCE – 118 BCE, Polybius, Histories 20.6.5
    • 200 BCE – 118 BCE, Polybius, Histories 16.1.2
    1. to dispose of one's property, devise it by will
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 922C
      • 4th century BC, Isaeus, Collected Works 3.68
      • 4th century BC, Isaeus, Collected Works 7.1
      • 445 BCE – 380 BCE, Lysias, Collected Works 19.39
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Politics 2.9.15
      • New Testament, Epistle to the Hebrews 9:16
    2. to set out for sale, dispose of merchandise
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.1.194
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 7.3.10
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Constitution of the Athenians 2.11
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 849D
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Memorabilia 1.6.13
    3. to arrange or settle mutually, make a covenant
      • 414 BCE, Aristophanes, The Birds 439
      • New Testament, Acts of the Apostles 3:25
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Memorabilia 2.6.23
    4. to compose, make
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 834A
    5. to set forth, recite
      • 200 BCE – 118 BCE, Polybius, Histories 3.108.2
      • 125 CE – 200 CE, Lucian, Hermotimus 1
