Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Likely from φρονέω (phronéō, “to think”) or φροντίς (phrontís, “thought”) with the verbal suffix -ίζω (-ízō). Related to φρήν (phrḗn).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pʰron.tíz.dɔː/ → /ɸronˈti.zo/ → /fronˈdi.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pʰron.tíz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /pʰronˈti.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ɸronˈti.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /fronˈti.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /fronˈdi.zo/

Verb

[edit]

φροντίζω • (phrontízō)

  1. (absolute) to consider, to take thought
  2. (with accusative) to consider, to ponder
  3. (with genitive) to take thought for something, give heed to something

Greek

Verb

[edit]

φροντίζω • (frontízo) (past φρόντισα, passive —)

  1. to take care of (a child, an obligation, etc.)
  2. to care for, attend to
