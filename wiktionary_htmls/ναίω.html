Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Hellenic *nayyō, possibly from Proto-Indo-European *nes- (“to join with, to conceal oneself”), but Beekes considers this uncertain. See νέομαι (néomai, “to go or come back”).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /nǎi̯.ɔː/ → /ˈnɛ.o/ → /ˈne.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /nǎi̯.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈnɛ.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈnɛ.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈne.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈne.o/

Verb

[edit]

ναίω • (naíō)

  1. (present and imperfect only) to dwell, abide

    • 750 BCE – 650 BCE, Hesiod, Works and Days 18

    • 429 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus the King 338
    • 429 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus the King 1105
    • 409 BCE, Sophocles, Philoctetes 1105
    1. (with accusative of place) to inhabit
      • 375 BCE – 275 BCE, Alexis 22
      • 300 BCE – 250 BCE, Apollonius of Rhodes, Argonautica 1.794
      • 405 BCE, Euripides, The Bacchae 719
      • 412 BCE, Euripides, Helen 1584
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 20.288
      • 750 BCE – 650 BCE, Hesiod, Works and Days 8
      • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Fragments 123.10
      • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Pythian Ode 7.5
      • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Electra 1375
      • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Fragments 1125
      • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Theocritus, Collected Works 16.88
    2. (of places) to lie, be situated
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 2.626
      • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Ajax 598
  2. (causal, with accusative of place) to give one to dwell in; to make habitable, build
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 4.174
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Paean 5.36
    • 7th–6th centuries BC, Homeric Hymn to Apollo 298
    1. (middle voice) to found
      • 300 BCE – 250 BCE, Apollonius of Rhodes, Argonautica 4.275
    2. (passive voice) to be situated
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 3.400
    3. (with accusative of person) to let one dwell, to settle them
      • 300 BCE – 250 BCE, Apollonius of Rhodes, Argonautica 4.567
      • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Pythian Ode 5.71
  3. (middle and passive in active sense) to settle
    • 300 BCE – 250 BCE, Apollonius of Rhodes, Argonautica 2.747
    • Dem.Bith. 6
    • 100 CE – 300 CE, Dionysius Periegetes, Periegesis 264
    • 100 CE – 300 CE, Dionysius Periegetes, Periegesis 349
    • 750 BCE – 650 BCE, Hesiod, Works and Days 639
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 14.119
    • 340 BCE – 285 BCE, Philitas, Collected Works 24
  4. Alternative form of νάω (náō)
