Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • προσδοκέω (prosdokéō) — Ionic

Etymology

[edit]

From πρός (prós, “to, towards, with”) +‎ δοκεύω (dokeúō, “to watch”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /proz.do.ká.ɔː/ → /proz.ðoˈka.o/ → /proz.ðoˈka.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /proz.do.ká.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /proz.doˈka.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /proz.ðoˈka.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /proz.ðoˈka.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /proz.ðoˈka.o/

Verb

[edit]

προσδοκᾰ́ω • (prosdokáō)

  1. to expect, to expect that...
    • 525 BCE – 455 BCE, Aeschylus, Prometheus Bound 930
    • 525 BCE – 455 BCE, Aeschylus, Prometheus Bound 988
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.42
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 7.156
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 7.235
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 699B
    • 405 BCE, Aristophanes, The Frogs 556
    • 386 BCE – 367 BCE, Plato, Cratylus 438E
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Constitution of Sparta 1.3
    • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, Agamemnon 675
    1. (with infinitive) to think, suppose
      • 438 BCE, Euripides, Alcestis 1091
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 803E
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 6.1.16
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, The Statesman 275A
    2. (with accusative of object) to expect, look for a thing
      • 525 BCE – 455 BCE, Aeschylus, Prometheus Bound 1026
      • 409 BCE, Sophocles, Philoctetes 784
      • 422 BCE, Aristophanes, The Wasps 56
      • 480 BCE – 411 BCE, Antiphon of Rhamnus, On the Murder of Herodes 19
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, On Horsemanship 8.14
      • 438 BCE, Euripides, Alcestis 363
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Hellenica 3.1.20
      • 366 BCE – 348 BCE, Plato, Theaetetus 170B
    3. (absolute)
      • 411 BCE, Aristophanes, Thesmophoriazusae 846
      • 400 BCE – 387 BCE, Plato, Apology 17C
      • 342 BCE – 290 BCE, Menander, The Harp-Player 7
    4. (passive voice)
      • 480 BCE – 406 BCE, Euripides, Fragments 550
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 966E
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, Letters 2.5
      • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Moralia 2.204D
    5. (passive voice)
      • 445 BCE – 380 BCE, Lysias, On the Property of Aristophanes 47–48
