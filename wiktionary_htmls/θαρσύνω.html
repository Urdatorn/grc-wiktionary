Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • θαρρῡ́νω (tharrhū́nō) — Attic

Etymology

[edit]

From θάρσος (thársos, “courage”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /tʰar.sy̌ː.nɔː/ → /θarˈsy.no/ → /θarˈsi.no/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /tʰar.sy̌ː.nɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /tʰarˈsy.no/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /θarˈsy.no/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /θarˈsy.no/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /θarˈsi.no/

Verb

[edit]

θαρσῡ́νω • (tharsū́nō)

  1. (transitive) to encourage, cheer
  2. (intransitive) to be of good courage
