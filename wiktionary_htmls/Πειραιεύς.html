Alternative forms

[edit]
  • Πειραεύς (Peiraeús)

Etymology

[edit]

Derived from πέρα (péra, “beyond”). The name roughly translates as "the place over the passage."

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /peː.rai̯.ěu̯s/ → /pi.rɛˈeɸs/ → /pi.reˈefs/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /peː.rai̯.ěu̯s/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /pi.rɛˈews/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /pi.rɛˈeɸs/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /pi.reˈefs/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /pi.reˈefs/

Proper noun

[edit]

Πειραιεύς • (Peiraieús) m (genitive Πειραιῶς); third declension

  1. Piraeus, Athens, Attica, Greece
