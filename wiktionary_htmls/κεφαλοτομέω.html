Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

κεφᾰλοτόμος (kephalotómos, “decapitation”) +‎ -έω (-éō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ke.pʰa.lo.to.mé.ɔː/ → /ce.ɸa.lo.toˈme.o/ → /ce.fa.lo.toˈme.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ke.pʰa.lo.to.mé.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ke.pʰa.lo.toˈme.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ce.ɸa.lo.toˈme.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ce.fa.lo.toˈme.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ce.fa.lo.toˈme.o/

Verb

[edit]

κεφᾰλοτομέω • (kephalotoméō)

  1. to decapitate, behead
