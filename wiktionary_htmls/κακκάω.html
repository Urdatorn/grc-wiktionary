Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Hellenic *kakkajō, from the Proto-Indo-European Lallwort root *kakka- (“to defecate”).[1]

Related to Latin cacō, Middle Irish caccaim, Old Armenian քակոր (kʻakor), Middle Armenian քաք (kʻakʻ), and German kacken.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /kak.ká.ɔː/ → /kaˈka.o/ → /kaˈka.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /kak.ká.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /kakˈka.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /kaˈka.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /kaˈka.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /kaˈka.o/

Verb

[edit]

κᾰκκᾰ́ω • (kakkáō)

  1. to defecate, shit
