Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *h₁éwidom, from *weyd- (“to see”). See εἴδομαι (eídomai).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /êː.don/ → /ˈi.ðon/ → /ˈi.ðon/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /êː.don/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈi.don/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈi.ðon/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈi.ðon/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈi.ðon/

Verb

[edit]

εἶδον • (eîdon)

  1. to see, behold, perceive
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 1.587
    • 408 BCE, Euripides, Orestes 1020
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 6.126
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 4.22
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 10.44
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 3.194
    • 525 BCE – 455 BCE, Aeschylus, Prometheus Bound 238
    • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 620A
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 10.195
    1. (strengthened) to look at, observe
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 4.476
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 4.412
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 22.450, (compare Plato, Gorgias 455A)
    2. to see a person, to meet, speak with them
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 4.125
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 2.4.5
    3. to see, experience, become acquainted with
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 5.221
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 3.233
      • 424 BCE, Euripides, Hecuba 56
      • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Fragments 835
      • 442 BCE, Sophocles, Antigone 1270
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 11.243
  2. to look at or towards
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 2.271
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 12.244
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 9.373
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 15.320
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 13.184
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 17.334
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 5.78
  3. to see mentally, to perceive
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 21.61
    • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 510E
    1. to examine, investigate
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Phaedo 70D
      • 366 BCE – 348 BCE, Plato, Theaetetus 192E

Usage notes

[edit]

All tenses besides the aorist are supplied by ὁράω (horáō) and ὄψομαι (ópsomai).
