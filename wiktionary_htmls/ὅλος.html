Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • οὖλος (oûlos) — Ionic, Homeric

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *solh₂wós, from *solh₂- (“whole”). Cognates include Sanskrit सर्व (sárva), Avestan 𐬵𐬀𐬎𐬭𐬬𐬀 (haurva), Latin salvus, sollus.[1] Unrelated false cognate of English whole.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /hó.los/ → /ˈo.los/ → /ˈo.los/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /hó.los/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈ(h)o.los/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈo.los/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈo.los/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈo.los/

Adjective

[edit]

ὅλος • (hólos) m (feminine ὅλη, neuter ὅλον); first/second declension

  1. whole, entire, perfect, complete
  2. absolute, utter
  3. generally, on the whole
  4. (with negative): at all
  5. actually
