Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • καταϝοικίδδω (katawoikíddō) — Cretan

Etymology

[edit]

From κάτοικος (kátoikos) +‎ -ίζω (-ízō) and/or κατα- (kata-) +‎ οἰκίζω (oikízō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ka.toi̯.kíz.dɔː/ → /ka.tyˈci.zo/ → /ka.tiˈci.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ka.toi̯.kíz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ka.tyˈki.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ka.tyˈci.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ka.tyˈci.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ka.tiˈci.zo/

Verb

[edit]

κᾰτοικίζω • (katoikízō)

  1. to settle, establish a colony
