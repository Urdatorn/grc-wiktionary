Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • τάμνω (támnō) — Ionic
  • τμήγω (tmḗgō) — epic

Etymology

[edit]

For earlier τάμνω (támnō), with ε from aorist ἔτεμον (étemon), from Proto-Indo-European *tm̥-né-h₁-ti, *tm̥-n-h₁-énti, nasal-infixed present (the present tense system includes a N progressive aspect marker) from *temh₁- (“to cut”).[1]

Cognate with Latin temnō, tondeō, tempus, templum.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /tém.nɔː/ → /ˈtem.no/ → /ˈtem.no/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /tém.nɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈtem.no/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈtem.no/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈtem.no/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈtem.no/

Verb

[edit]

τέμνω • (témnō)

  1. to cut, hew
  2. to maim, wound
  3. to butcher, sacrifice
  4. to cut down, reap
  5. to cut through, advance, drive through, plow through
