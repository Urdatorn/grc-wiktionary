Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐκ- (ek-, “out”) +‎ πῑ́νω (pī́nō, “to drink”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ek.pǐː.nɔː/ → /ekˈpi.no/ → /ekˈpi.no/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ek.pǐː.nɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ekˈpi.no/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ekˈpi.no/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ekˈpi.no/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ekˈpi.no/

Verb

[edit]

ἐκπῑ́νω • (ekpī́nō)

  1. to drink out or off of, quaff
    • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, The Libation Bearers 66
    • 480 BCE – 411 BCE, Antiphon of Rhamnus, Collected Works 1.20
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 4.199
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 9.353
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 10.237
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 22.56
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Electra 785
  2. to drain (of insects)
    • 423 BCE, Aristophanes, The Clouds 712
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Rhetoric 1393b31
  3. to drain (a cup) dry
    • 408 BCE – 334 BCE, Antiphanes Comicus, Collected Works 3
    • 5th century BC, Pherecrates, Fragmenta 143.9
    • 442 BCE, Sophocles, Antigone 532
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Fragments 483
