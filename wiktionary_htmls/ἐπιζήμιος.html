Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ἐπιζάμιος (epizámios) — Doric

Etymology

[edit]

ἐπι- (epi-, “loss, damage”) +‎ ζημίᾱ (zēmíā) +‎ -ιος (-ios)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.piz.dɛ̌ː.mi.os/ → /e.piˈzi.mi.os/ → /e.piˈzi.mi.os/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.piz.dɛ̌ː.mi.os/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /e.piˈze̝.mi.os/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /e.piˈzi.mi.os/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /e.piˈzi.mi.os/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /e.piˈzi.mi.os/

Adjective

[edit]

ἐπιζήμῐος • (epizḗmios) m or f (neuter ἐπιζήμῐον); second declension

  1. bringing loss, hurtful, damaging
  2. prejudicial
  3. penal
  4. liable to punishment
