Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ἐέλπω (eélpō)

Etymology

[edit]

From *ϝέλπω (*wélpō), perhaps from a Proto-Indo-European *welp- (“to hope, expect”), with Latin volup (“pleasurably”) as a cognate; see the Latin for more on the root.[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /él.pɔː/ → /ˈel.po/ → /ˈel.po/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /él.pɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈel.po/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈel.po/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈel.po/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈel.po/

Verb

[edit]

ἔλπω • (élpō)

  1. to cause to hope
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 2.91
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 13.380
  2. (middle voice)
    1. to hope, expect
      • 500 BCE – 400 BCE, Bacchylides, Fr. 12
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 13.609
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 15.539
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 15.701
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 17.404
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 17.495
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 3.275
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 9.419
      • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Nemean Ode 4.92
      • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Pythian Ode 4.243
    2. to expect anxiously, fear
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 9.113
    3. to deem, suppose
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.65
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 10.355
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 13.309
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 6.297
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 23.345
      • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Theocritus, Collected Works 7.31
      • Palatine Anthology 5.115
