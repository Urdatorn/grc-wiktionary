Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ὀϊζύς (oïzús, “misery”) +‎ -ρός (-rós).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /o.iz.dy.rós/ → /o.i.zyˈros/ → /o.i.ziˈros/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /o.iz.dy.rós/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /o.i.zyˈros/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /o.i.zyˈros/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /o.i.zyˈros/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /o.i.ziˈros/

Scanned with long υ in Epic poetry to avoid three light syllables in a row:

 
  • IPA(key): /o.iz.dyː.rós/ → /o.i.zyˈros/ → /o.i.ziˈros/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /o.iz.dyː.rós/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /o.i.zyˈros/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /o.i.zyˈros/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /o.i.zyˈros/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /o.i.ziˈros/

Adjective

[edit]

ὀϊζῠρός • (oïzurós) m (feminine ὀϊζῠρᾱ́, neuter ὀϊζῠρόν); first/second declension

  1. miserable
  2. causing misery
