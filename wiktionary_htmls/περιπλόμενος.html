Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

An aorist middle participle from περι- (peri-) +‎ πέλω (pélō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pe.ri.pló.me.nos/ → /pe.riˈplo.me.nos/ → /pe.riˈplo.me.nos/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pe.ri.pló.me.nos/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /pe.riˈplo.me.nos/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /pe.riˈplo.me.nos/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /pe.riˈplo.me.nos/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /pe.riˈplo.me.nos/

Participle

[edit]

περῐπλόμενος • (periplómenos) (Epic)

  1. moving or being around
  2. (of time) turning around
