Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ὀργή (orgḗ).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /or.ɡá.ɔː/ → /orˈɣa.o/ → /orˈɣa.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /or.ɡá.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /orˈɡa.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /orˈɣa.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /orˈɣa.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /orˈɣa.o/

Verb

[edit]

ὀργάω • (orgáō)

  1. to ripen
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 4.199
    • 460 BCE – 370 BCE, Hippocrates, Aphorisms 1.22
    • 460 BCE – 370 BCE, Hippocrates, On Fractures 28
    • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Moralia 2.647f
    • 371 BCE – 287 BCE, Theophrastus, On the Causes of Plants 1.6.2
    • 371 BCE – 287 BCE, Theophrastus, On the Causes of Plants 3.2.6
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Oeconomicus 19.19
  2. (of living things) to be sexually aroused or on heat
    • 414 BCE, Aristophanes, The Birds 462
    • 425 BCE, Aristophanes, Lysistrata 1113
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, History of Animals 500b.11
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, History of Animals 542a.32
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, History of Animals 560b.13
    • ante 177 CE, Pollux, Onomasticon 6.188
    • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Moralia 2.651c
  3. to be eager, ready, excited
    • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, The Libation Bearers 454
    • 525 BCE – 455 BCE, Aeschylus, Fragments 54A
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, History of Animals 613b.28
    • 519 BCE – 422 BCE, Cratinus, Collected Works 21D
    • 40 CE – 115 CE, Dio Chrysostom, Collected Works 36.26
    • Marcellin., Vit.Thuc. 54
    • 280 BCE – 220 BCE, Philo of Byzantium, Compendium of Mechanics 2.21
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Pythian Ode 6.50
    • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Alexander the Great 6
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 2.21
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 4.108
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 8.2

Usage notes

[edit]

Mostly used in present.
