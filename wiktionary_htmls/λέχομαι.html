Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *legʰ- (“to lie”).[1] Cognates include Old Church Slavonic лежати (ležati), Old Irish lige, and Old English licgan (English lie).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /lé.kʰo.mai̯/ → /ˈle.xo.mɛ/ → /ˈle.xo.me/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /lé.kʰo.mai̯/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈle.kʰo.mɛ/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈle.xo.mɛ/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈle.xo.me/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈle.xo.me/

Verb

[edit]

λέχομαι • (lékhomai)

  1. to lie down
