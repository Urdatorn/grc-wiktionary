Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From βουλή (boulḗ, “decision, counsel”) +‎ -εύω (-eúō, denominative suffix).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /buː.lěu̯.ɔː/ → /βuˈle.βo/ → /vuˈle.vo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /buː.lěu̯.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /buˈle.wo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /βuˈle.βo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /vuˈle.vo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /vuˈle.vo/

Verb

[edit]

βουλεύω • (bouleúō)

  1. to take counsel, deliberate, concert measures; (in past tenses) to determine or resolve after deliberation
