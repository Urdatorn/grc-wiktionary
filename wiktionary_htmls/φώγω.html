Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • φῴζω (phṓizō)
  • φωγνύω (phōgnúō)

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Hellenic *pʰṓgō, from Proto-Indo-European *bʰeh₃g- (“to bake”). Cognates include Phrygian βεκος (bekos /⁠begos⁠/) and Old English bacan (English bake).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pʰɔ̌ː.ɡɔː/ → /ˈɸo.ɣo/ → /ˈfo.ɣo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pʰɔ̌ː.ɡɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈpʰo.ɡo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈɸo.ɣo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈfo.ɣo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈfo.ɣo/

Verb

[edit]

φώγω • (phṓgō)

  1. to roast, toast, parch
