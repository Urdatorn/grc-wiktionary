Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From δια- (dia-) +‎ ἁρπάζω (harpázō)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /di.ar.páz.dɔː/ → /ði.arˈpa.zo/ → /ði.arˈpa.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /di.ar.páz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /di.arˈpa.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ði.arˈpa.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ði.arˈpa.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ði.arˈpa.zo/

Verb

[edit]

διαρπάζω • (diarpázō)

  1. to tear in pieces
  2. to efface
  3. to spoil, plunder
  4. to seize as plunder
