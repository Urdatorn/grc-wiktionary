Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἀνάγκη (anánkē, “force, necessity”) +‎ -άζω (-ázō, denominative verbal suffix).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /a.naŋ.káz.dɔː/ → /a.naŋˈɡa.zo/ → /a.naŋˈɡa.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /a.naŋ.káz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /a.naŋˈka.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /a.naŋˈɡa.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /a.naŋˈɡa.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /a.naŋˈɡa.zo/

Verb

[edit]

ᾰ̓νᾰγκᾰ́ζω • (anankázō)

  1. (transitive, control verb) to force [with accusative ‘someone’ and infinitive ‘to do something’]
    Synonym: βιάζω (biázō)
