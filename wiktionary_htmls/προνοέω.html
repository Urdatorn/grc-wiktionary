Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From πρόνοος (prónoos) +‎ -έω (-éō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pro.no.é.ɔː/ → /pro.noˈe.o/ → /pro.noˈe.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pro.no.é.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /pro.noˈe.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /pro.noˈe.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /pro.noˈe.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /pro.noˈe.o/

Verb

[edit]

προνοέω • (pronoéō)

  1. to perceive before, foresee
  2. to think of or plan beforehand, provide
  3. to be provident, take measures of precaution
  4. to provide for, take thought for [with genitive]
