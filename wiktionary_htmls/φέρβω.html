Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

According to Beekes, an agricultural term without etymology.[1] Alternatively from Proto-Indo-European *bʰergʷ-, if Latin forbea reflects a true Italic cognate rather than a borrowing from Ancient Greek φορβή (phorbḗ) or an unattested relative thereof.[2]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pʰér.bɔː/ → /ˈɸer.βo/ → /ˈfer.vo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pʰér.bɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈpʰer.bo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈɸer.βo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈfer.vo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈfer.vo/

Verb

[edit]

φέρβω • (phérbō)

  1. to feed, nourish
  2. to pasture, graze
  3. (passive voice) to be fed
  4. to eat, consume
  5. to enjoy, have
