Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From πόλις (pólis, “a city”) +‎ *φυλακέω (*phulakéō, “to guard”)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /po.li.o.pʰy.la.ké.ɔː/ → /po.li.o.ɸy.laˈce.o/ → /po.li.o.fi.laˈce.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /po.li.o.pʰy.la.ké.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /po.li.o.pʰy.laˈke.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /po.li.o.ɸy.laˈce.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /po.li.o.fy.laˈce.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /po.li.o.fi.laˈce.o/

Verb

[edit]

πολῐοφῠλᾰκέω • (poliophulakéō)

  1. to keep within the city
