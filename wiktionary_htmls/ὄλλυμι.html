Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ὀλλύω (ollúō)

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *h₃elh₁- (“destroy”) with the suffix -νῡμῐ (-nūmi) and assimilation of *ln to ll (*όλ-νῠ-μι). See Latin ab-oleō, which may be related.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ól.lyː.mi/ → /ˈol.ly.mi/ → /ˈo.li.mi/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ól.lyː.mi/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈol.ly.mi/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈol.ly.mi/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈol.ly.mi/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈo.li.mi/

Verb

[edit]

ὄλλῡμῐ • (óllūmi)

  1. to destroy, make an end of
  2. to lose
  3. (middle, passive, and perfect) to perish, come to an end
  4. (middle, passive, and perfect) to be ruined, undone
