Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From εἰς (eis, “into”) +‎ ἄγω (ágō, “to lead”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /eː.sá.ɡɔː/ → /iˈsa.ɣo/ → /iˈsa.ɣo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /eː.sá.ɡɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /iˈsa.ɡo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /iˈsa.ɣo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /iˈsa.ɣo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /iˈsa.ɣo/

Verb

[edit]

εἰσάγω • (eiságō)

  1. to lead in or into (esp. into one’s dwelling)
  2. to bring in, bring forward (esp. as a law-term, e.g. "bring forward a case")
  3. to introduce to a subject, instruct
  4. (intransitive) to enter
