Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

ἀνα- (ana-) +‎ διδάσκω (didáskō)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /a.na.di.dás.kɔː/ → /a.na.ðiˈðas.ko/ → /a.na.ðiˈðas.ko/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /a.na.di.dás.kɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /a.na.diˈdas.ko/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /a.na.ðiˈðas.ko/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /a.na.ðiˈðas.ko/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /a.na.ðiˈðas.ko/

Verb

[edit]

ἀνᾰδῐδάσκω • (anadidáskō)

  1. to teach otherwise or better
  2. (passive voice) to be better instructed, change one's mind
  3. to show clear, expound, interpret
