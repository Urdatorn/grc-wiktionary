Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἀπό (apó, “from, away from”) + κρίνω (krínō, “to separate, distinguish, judge”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /a.po.krǐː.nɔː/ → /a.poˈkri.no/ → /a.poˈkri.no/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /a.po.krǐː.nɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /a.poˈkri.no/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /a.poˈkri.no/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /a.poˈkri.no/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /a.poˈkri.no/

Verb

[edit]

ἀποκρῑ́νω • (apokrī́nō)

  1. to separate, set apart
    • 5th century BC, Pherecrates, Deserters 1
    • 175 CE – 235 CE, Claudius Aelianus, Various History 12.8
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, The Statesman 302C
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 5.12
    • 680 BCE – 645 BCE, Archilochus, Collected Works 82
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.60
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.36
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 1.3
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 4.72
    • 460 BCE – 370 BCE, Hippocrates, Prognostics 45
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, History of Animals 6.3.3
    • 460 BCE – 370 BCE, Hippocrates, On Ancient Medicine 13
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 2.49
    1. to distinguish
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.194
      • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 407C
  2. to choose
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 6.130, (compare Plato, Laws 946A)
    • 429 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus the King 640
  3. to reject on inquiry
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 751D
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 936A
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Politics 5.12.2
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 966D
  4. (in middle voice) to answer, give answer, reply
    • 405 BCE, Euripides, The Bacchae 1272
    • 407 BCE, Euripides, Iphigenia in Aulis 1354
    • 423 BCE, Aristophanes, The Clouds 1245
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 5.42
    • 390 BCE, Plato, Hippias Major 287A
    • 422 BCE, Aristophanes, The Wasps 964
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 1.90
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 3.61, (compare Plato, Crito 49A)
    • 388 BCE, Aristophanes, Plutus 17
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 8.71
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 658C
    • 380 BCE, Plato, Gorgias 453D, (compare Plato, Meno ,
      380 BCE, Plato, Euthydemus
      )
    1. to answer charges, defend oneself
      • 446 BCE – 386 BCE, Aristophanes, The Acharnians 632
      • 480 BCE – 411 BCE, Antiphon of Rhamnus, Collected Works 143.30, (compare 119.32)
    2. to meet events, circumstances
      • 108 CE, Arrian, Discourses of Epictetus 2.16.2
    3. (passive voice) to be given as an answer (+ dative of agent)
      • 366 BCE – 348 BCE, Plato, Theaetetus 187B:
        κινδυνεύει δὲ ἡ ἀληθὴς δόξα ἐπιστήμη εἶναι, καί μοι τοῦτο ἀποκεκρίσθω.
        kinduneúei dè hē alēthḕs dóxa epistḗmē eînai, kaí moi toûto apokekrísthō.
        But I venture to assert that knowledge is true opinion, and let that be my answer.
