Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From φιλόσοφος (philósophos) +‎ -έω (-éō)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pʰi.lo.so.pʰé.ɔː/ → /ɸi.lo.soˈɸe.o/ → /fi.lo.soˈfe.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pʰi.lo.so.pʰé.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /pʰi.lo.soˈpʰe.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ɸi.lo.soˈɸe.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /fi.lo.soˈfe.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /fi.lo.soˈfe.o/

Verb

[edit]

φῐλοσοφέω • (philosophéō)

  1. (intransitive) to love or pursue knowledge, philosophize
  2. (intransitive) to teach philosophy
  3. (transitive) to discuss philosophically, investigate, study
