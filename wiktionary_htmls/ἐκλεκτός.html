Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐκλέγω (eklégō, “to pick out”) +‎ -τός (-tós, adjectival suffix).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.klek.tós/ → /e.klekˈtos/ → /e.klekˈtos/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.klek.tós/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /e.klekˈtos/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /e.klekˈtos/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /e.klekˈtos/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /e.klekˈtos/

Adjective

[edit]

ἐκλεκτός • (eklektós) m (feminine ἐκλεκτή, neuter ἐκλεκτόν); first/second declension

  1. picked out, select
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 6.100
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 938B
  2. (in New Testament and ecclesiastical writings, in the plural) the elect
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Septuagint, Isaiah 43.20
    • The Gospel of Mark 13:20
