Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From χρόνος (khrónos, “time”) +‎ -ίζω (-ízō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /kʰro.níz.dɔː/ → /xroˈni.zo/ → /xroˈni.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /kʰro.níz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /kʰroˈni.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /xroˈni.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /xroˈni.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /xroˈni.zo/

Verb

[edit]

χρονῐ́ζω • (khronízō)

  1. to spend time
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 3.61
    1. to last long, continue
      • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, Agamemnon 847
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, History of Animals 3.22.3
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, History of Animals 4.10.4
    2. to persevere
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Phaedrus 255B
    3. to take time, tarry, linger, delay, be slow
      • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, Agamemnon 1356
      • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, The Libation Bearers 64
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 6.49
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 8.16
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Rhetoric 2.3.12
      • 200 BCE – 118 BCE, Polybius, Histories 33.16.6
      • 70 CE – 110 CE, The Gospel of Matthew 24:48
    4. (of ailments) to be/become chronic
      • 460 BCE – 370 BCE, Hippocrates, Aphorisms 1248
    5. (of wine) to be/become old, to have age
      • 250 CE – 350 CE, Athenaeus, Deipnosophistae 33A
  2. (passive voice) to be prolonged or protracted
    • 467 BCE, Aeschylus, Seven Against Thebes 54, (Compare The Libation Bearers 957)
    • 440 BCE – 390 BCE, Andocides, Collected Works 27.1
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Nicomachean Ethics 9.5.3
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Problems 13.1
    1. to grow up
      • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, Agamemnon 727

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • χρονιάζω (chroniázo)

Verb

[edit]

χρονίζω • (chronízo) (past χρόνισα)

  1. to take a long time, drag
  2. to endure, last a long time
  3. to delay, drag
  4. to age one year
