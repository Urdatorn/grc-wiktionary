Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ποθήκω (pothḗkō) — Doric
  • ποθᾱ́κω (pothā́kō) — Hyperdoric

Etymology

[edit]

From προσ- (pros-) +‎ ἥκω (hḗkō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pro.sɛ̌ː.kɔː/ → /proˈsi.ko/ → /proˈsi.ko/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pro.sɛ̌ː.kɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /proˈse̝.ko/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /proˈsi.ko/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /proˈsi.ko/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /proˈsi.ko/

Verb

[edit]

προσήκω • (prosḗkō)

  1. to have arrived at a place, have come, be near at hand, be present
  2. to belong to [with dative]
  3. (also impersonal) to concern [with dative]
  4. to be related [with dative]
  5. (impersonal) to be fitting, to suit [with dative or accusative]
