Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Causative of πληθύω (plēthúō), from πληθύς (plēthús, “throng”), from πίμπλημῐ (pímplēmi, “I fill”).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /plɛː.tʰy̌ː.nɔː/ → /pliˈθy.no/ → /pliˈθi.no/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /plɛː.tʰy̌ː.nɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ple̝ˈtʰy.no/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /pliˈθy.no/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /pliˈθy.no/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /pliˈθi.no/

Verb

[edit]

πληθῡ́νω • (plēthū́nō)

  1. to increase, multiply
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, History of Animals 587b.20
    • 55 CE – 56 CE, Paul the Apostle, Second Epistle to the Corinthians 9:10
    • New Testament, Epistle to the Hebrews 6:14
    • 280 BCE – 220 BCE, Philo of Byzantium, Compendium of Mechanics 1.496
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Septuagint, Genesis 17.2
  2. to make multiple
    • 458 CE – 538 CE, Damascius, Difficulties and Solutions of First Principles 99
    • 331 CE – 363 CE, Julian, Orations 4.143b
    • 331 CE – 363 CE, Julian, Orations 7.222a
  3. to use the plural
    • 50 CE – 250 CE, Apollonius Dyscolus, On Syntax 31.25
  4. (passive voice) to be in the majority
    • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, Agamemnon 1370
    • 470 BCE, Aeschylus, The Suppliants 604
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Septuagint, Genesis 18.20
