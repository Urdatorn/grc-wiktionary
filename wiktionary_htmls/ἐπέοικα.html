Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐπι- (epi-) +‎ ἔοικα (éoika).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.pé.oi̯.ka/ → /eˈpe.y.ka/ → /eˈpe.i.ka/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.pé.oi̯.ka/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /eˈpe.y.ka/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /eˈpe.y.ka/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /eˈpe.y.ka/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /eˈpe.i.ka/

Verb

[edit]

ἐπέοικᾰ • (epéoika) (chiefly Epic)

  1. to be like, to suit [with dative]
  2. (chiefly impersonal) to be fit, proper, seemly [with dative or accusative]
