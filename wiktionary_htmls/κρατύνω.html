Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • καρτῡ́νω (kartū́nō) — Epic

Etymology

[edit]

From κράτος (krátos) +‎ -ῡ́νω (-ū́nō)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /kra.ty̌ː.nɔː/ → /kraˈty.no/ → /kraˈti.no/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /kra.ty̌ː.nɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /kraˈty.no/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /kraˈty.no/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /kraˈty.no/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /kraˈti.no/

Verb

[edit]

κρᾰτῡ́νω • (kratū́nō)

  1. (transitive) to strengthen
  2. (passive voice) to become strong
  3. (transitive) to harden
  4. to rule, govern [with genitive or accusative]
  5. to become master, get possession [with genitive ‘of’]
  6. (transitive) to hold, exercise (power)
  7. (transitive) to ply or throw (a missile) stoutly
