Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐκ- (ek-, “out, away, off”) +‎ λᾰ́μπω (lámpō, “to give light, shine”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.klám.pɔː/ → /eˈklam.po/ → /eˈklam.bo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.klám.pɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /eˈklam.po/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /eˈklam.po/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /eˈklam.po/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /eˈklam.bo/

Verb

[edit]

ἐκλᾰ́μπω • (eklámpō)

  1. to shine or beam forth; (of a fever) to burst forth violently; (of sound) to be clearly heard
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 6.82
    • 460 BCE – 370 BCE, Hippocrates, On Ancient Medicine 16
    • 200 BCE – 118 BCE, Polybius, The Histories 15.31.1
    1. to be distinguished
      • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Cicero 2
  2. to flash forth
    • 95 CE – 165 CE, Appian, Syrian Wars 56
  3. (astrology) Synonym of δῐαυγᾰ́ζω (diaugázō)
