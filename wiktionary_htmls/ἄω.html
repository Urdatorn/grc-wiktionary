Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology 1

[edit]

Related to ἄημι (áēmi).

Verb

[edit]

ἄω • (áō)

  1. to blow
    • 300 BCE – 250 BCE, Apollonius of Rhodes, Argonautica 1.605
    • 300 BCE – 250 BCE, Apollonius of Rhodes, Argonautica 2.1228
Usage notes
[edit]

Only found in the imperfect ἄεν (áen).

References
[edit]

ἄω • (áō)

  1. Alternative form of ἀάω (aáō, “to hurt”)

Etymology 4

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *seh₂- (“to satiate, satisfy”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ǎː.ɔː/ → /ˈa.o/ → /ˈa.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ǎː.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈa.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈa.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈa.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈa.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἄω • (áō)

  1. to satiate (with genitive and accusative)
    αἵματος ἆσαι Ἄρηα (Iliad 5.289 and other passages)
    to sate Ares with blood
    λιλαιόμενα χροὸς ἆσαι (Iliad 11.574 and other passages)
    anxious to sate themselves with flesh (said of spears)
  2. (intransitive) to take one's fill
