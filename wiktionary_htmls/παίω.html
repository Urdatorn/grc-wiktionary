Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • πήω (pḗō) — Boeotian

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *pēu-, *pyu-, *pū- (“to hit; to cut”). Cognates include Latin paviō.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pǎi̯.ɔː/ → /ˈpɛ.o/ → /ˈpe.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pǎi̯.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈpɛ.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈpɛ.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈpe.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈpe.o/

    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cyropaedia 7.3.6

      • 408 BCE, Euripides, Orestes 1472
