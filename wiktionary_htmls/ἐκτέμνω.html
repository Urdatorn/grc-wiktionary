Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ἐκτᾰ́μνω (ektámnō) — Homeric, Ionic

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐκ- (ek-, “out”) +‎ τέμνω (témnō, “to cut”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ek.tém.nɔː/ → /ekˈtem.no/ → /ekˈtem.no/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ek.tém.nɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ekˈtem.no/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ekˈtem.no/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ekˈtem.no/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ekˈtem.no/

Verb

[edit]

ἐκτέμνω • (ektémnō)

  1. to cut out
    1. to cut down trees, fell, cut to shape
    2. to cut away sinews and the like
  2. to castrate
  3. to divide, zone
  4. to be disarmed and deceived
