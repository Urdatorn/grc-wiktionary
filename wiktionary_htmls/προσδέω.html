Ancient Greek

[edit]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /proz.dé.ɔː/ → /prozˈðe.o/ → /prozˈðe.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /proz.dé.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /prozˈde.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /prozˈðe.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /prozˈðe.o/

Etymology 1

[edit]

From προσ- (pros-) +‎ δέω (déō, “to bind”)

Verb

[edit]

προσδέω • (prosdéō)

  1. (transitive) to bind on, attach

Etymology 2

[edit]

From προσ- (pros-) +‎ δέω (déō, “to lack”)

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ποτιδεύομαι (potideúomai) — Doric

Verb

[edit]

προσδέω • (prosdéō)

  1. (active voice)
    1. to need besides [with genitive]
  2. (middle voice)
    1. to be in want of, stand in need of, require besides [with genitive]
    2. to desire also [with infinitive]
    3. to beg or ask [with genitive ‘of’]
    4. to entreat [with accusative and infinitive ‘someone to do something’]
