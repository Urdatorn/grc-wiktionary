Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From κατα- (kata-, intensifying prefix) +‎ λῡ́ω (lū́ō, “loosen”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ka.ta.ly̌ː.ɔː/ → /ka.taˈly.o/ → /ka.taˈli.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ka.ta.ly̌ː.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ka.taˈly.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ka.taˈly.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ka.taˈly.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ka.taˈli.o/

Verb

[edit]

κᾰτᾰλῡ́ω • (katalū́ō)

  1. to loosen, unbind
  2. to unyoke, unharness
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 4.28:
      ἄλλ' εἴπ', ἤ σφωϊν καταλύσομεν ὠκέας ἵππους
      áll' eíp', ḗ sphōïn katalúsomen ōkéas híppous
      but, tell us, shall we unharness their fast horses?
  3. to take down something hung or someone hanged
    • 460 BCE – 370 BCE, Hippocrates, Aphorisms :
      τῶν ἀπαγχομένων καὶ καταλυομένων, μηδέπω δὲ τεθνηκότων ...
      tôn apankhoménōn kaì kataluoménōn, mēdépō dè tethnēkótōn ...
      about those who were hanged and taken down but have not died
  4. to destroy
    • 436 BCE – 338 BCE, Isocrates, On the Team of Horses 40.8–9:
      καὶ τὴν ὑμετέραν καταλῦσαι δύναμιν
      kaì tḕn humetéran katalûsai dúnamin
      to destroy your (city's) might
  5. to abolish, do away with
    • 436 BCE – 338 BCE, Isocrates, Against Lochites 10.2–4:
      αὐτοὶ γὰρ ἡμεῖς δίς ἤδη τὴν δημοκρατίαν ἐπείδομεν καταλυθεῖσαν καὶ δίς τῆς ἐλευθερίας ἀπεστερήθημεν
      autoì gàr hēmeîs dís ḗdē tḕn dēmokratían epeídomen katalutheîsan kaì dís tês eleutherías apesterḗthēmen
      twice we ourselves have already seen our democracy abolished and twice we have lost our freedom
  6. to deprive someone of their authority
    • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Timoleon 39.5:
      τοὺς τυράννους καταλύσας, καὶ τοὺς βαρβάρους καταπολεμήσας
      toùs turánnous katalúsas, kaì toùs barbárous katapolemḗsas
      having overthrown the tyrants and fought the barbarians
  7. to put an end to
    καταλῦσαι τὸν βίον ― katalûsai tòn bíon ― to die (literally, “to put an end to life”)
  8. to stay somewhere for one night, seek shelter
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 1.136):
      (Θεμιστοκλῆς) ἀναγκάζεται κατά τι ἄπορον παρὰ Ἄδμητον τὸν Μολοσσῶν βασιλέα ὄντα αὐτῷ οὐ φίλον καταλῦσαι
      (Themistoklês) anankázetai katá ti áporon parà Ádmēton tòn Molossôn basiléa ónta autôi ou phílon katalûsai
      (Themistocles) being in a difficult position had to seek shelter from Admetos, the king of Molossi, though the latter was not a friend of his

Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Learned borrowing from Ancient Greek καταλύω (katalúō). Morphologically, from κατα- (“fully”) +‎ λύω (“unbind, loosen”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): /kataˈlio/
  • Hyphenation: κα‧τα‧λύ‧ω

Verb

[edit]

καταλύω • (katalýo) (past κατέλυσα, passive καταλύομαι)

  1. to abolish, break down
  2. to stay (usually for one night)
  3. (chemistry) to catalyze
  4. (religion) to break one's fast (resume normal diet after fasting)
