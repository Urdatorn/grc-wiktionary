Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From παρα- (para-) +‎ σκευάζω (skeuázō, “to make, prepare”) or παρασκευή (paraskeuḗ) +‎ -άζω (-ázō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pa.ras.keu̯.áz.dɔː/ → /pa.ras.ceˈβa.zo/ → /pa.ras.ceˈva.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pa.ras.keu̯.áz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /pa.ras.keˈwa.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /pa.ras.ceˈβa.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /pa.ras.ceˈva.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /pa.ras.ceˈva.zo/

Verb

[edit]

πᾰρᾰσκευᾰ́ζω • (paraskeuázō)

  1. to prepare, make ready
  2. to procure
  3. to make

Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Ancient Greek παρασκευάζω (paraskeuázō). By surface analysis, παρα- (para-) +‎ σκευάζω (skevázo).

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): /pa.ɾa.sceˈva.zo/
  • Hyphenation: πα‧ρα‧σκευ‧ά‧ζω

Verb

[edit]

παρασκευάζω • (paraskevázo) (past παρασκεύασα, passive παρασκευάζομαι)

  1. to prepare
  2. to make, concoct
  3. to cook
