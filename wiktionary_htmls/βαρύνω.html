Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From βᾰρῠ́ς (barús, “heavy, weighty”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ba.rý.nɔː/ → /βaˈry.no/ → /vaˈri.no/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ba.rý.nɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /baˈry.no/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /βaˈry.no/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /vaˈry.no/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /vaˈri.no/

Verb

[edit]

βᾰρῠ́νω • (barúnō)

  1. (active voice) to weigh down, oppress, depress
  2. (passive voice) to be heavy, weary, oppressed
  3. (figuratively) to oppress, weary, afflict
    Synonym: θλῑ́βω (thlī́bō)
  4. to mark with the grave accent
