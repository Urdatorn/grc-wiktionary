Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἀπο- (apo-, “away, off”) +‎ κεφαλή (kephalḗ, “head”) +‎ -ίζω (-ízō, denominative verb suffix).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /a.po.ke.pʰa.líz.dɔː/ → /a.po.ce.ɸaˈli.zo/ → /a.po.ce.faˈli.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /a.po.ke.pʰa.líz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /a.po.ke.pʰaˈli.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /a.po.ce.ɸaˈli.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /a.po.ce.faˈli.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /a.po.ce.faˈli.zo/

Verb

[edit]

ἀποκεφαλίζω • (apokephalízō)

  1. to decapitate, behead
