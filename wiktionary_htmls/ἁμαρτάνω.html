Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Although there are no known cognates, Beekes nonetheless reconstructs Proto-Indo-European *h₂mert- (“miss, fail”) as the ancestor of this word.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ha.mar.tá.nɔː/ → /a.marˈta.no/ → /a.marˈta.no/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ha.mar.tá.nɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /(h)a.marˈta.no/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /a.marˈta.no/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /a.marˈta.no/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /a.marˈta.no/

Verb

[edit]

ᾰ̔μᾰρτᾰ́νω • (hamartánō)

  1. (transitive, intransitive) to miss, miss the mark, especially of a thrown spear [with genitive ‘something’]
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 5.287
    • 525 BCE – 455 BCE, Aeschylus, Fragments 179, (Compare Aeschylus, Agamemnon 1194)
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 10.372
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Ajax 155
    • 388 BCE, Aristophanes, Plutus 961
    • 480 BCE – 411 BCE, Antiphon of Rhamnus, Collected Works 124.26
    1. (transitive, intransitive) to fail at, miss one's point, go wrong in [with genitive ‘something’]
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 21.155
      • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, Agamemnon 1194
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 7.292

      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.71
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 7.139
    2. (transitive) to be deprived of, to lose [with genitive ‘something’]
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 9.512
      • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, Agamemnon 535
      • 438 BCE, Euripides, Alcestis 879
      • 409 BCE, Sophocles, Philoctetes 231
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 9.7
      • 440 BCE – 390 BCE, Andocides, Collected Works 4.2
    3. (rarely transitive) to fail to do, neglect [with genitive ‘something’]
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 24.68
      • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, Agamemnon 213
  2. (intransitive) to fail, do wrong, err, sin
