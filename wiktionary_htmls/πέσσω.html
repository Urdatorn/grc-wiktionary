Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • πέττω (péttō) — Attic
  • πέπτω (péptō) — Attic

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Hellenic *péťťō, from Proto-Indo-European *pékʷ-ye-, from *pekʷ-. The alternative present stem πέπτω (péptō) apparently comes from analogy with the root-stem πεπ- (pep-) < *pekʷ-. Cognates include Latin coquō (whence English cook), Sanskrit पचति (pacati), Avestan 𐬞𐬀𐬗𐬀𐬌𐬙𐬌 (pacaiti), and Albanian pjek.

Verb

[edit]

πέσσω • (péssō)

  1. to soften, ripen
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Generation of Animals 780b.10
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Problems 944a.13
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 7.119
  2. to boil, cook, bake
    • 405 BCE, Aristophanes, The Frogs 505
    • 421 BCE, Aristophanes, Peace 869
    • 388 BCE, Aristophanes, Plutus 1136
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.160
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.37
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 8.137
    • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 372b
  3. to digest
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Generation of Animals 718b.21
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Generation of Animals 762a.15
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Meteorology 381b.12
    • 350 BCE, Aristotle, Parts of Animals 677b.31
    • 460 BCE – 370 BCE, Hippocrates, On Ancient Medicine 11
    • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Eumenes 11
  4. (figuratively) to brood
    • 300 BCE – 250 BCE, Apollonius of Rhodes, Argonautica 1.283
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 4.513
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 8.513
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 9.565
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 24.617
    • 340 BCE – 285 BCE, Philitas, Collected Works 1
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Pythian Ode 4.186
    1. (of diseases)
      • 460 BCE – 370 BCE, Hippocrates, On Regimen in Acute Diseases 42
