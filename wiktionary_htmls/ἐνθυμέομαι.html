Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐν- (en-) +‎ θῡμός (thūmós) +‎ -έομαι (-éomai)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /en.tʰyː.mé.o.mai̯/ → /en.θyˈme.o.mɛ/ → /en.θiˈme.o.me/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /en.tʰyː.mé.o.mai̯/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /en.tʰyˈme.o.mɛ/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /en.θyˈme.o.mɛ/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /en.θyˈme.o.me/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /en.θiˈme.o.me/

Verb

[edit]

ἐνθῡμέομαι • (enthūméomai)

  1. to lay to heart, consider well, reflect on, ponder
  2. to take to heart, be hurt or angry at
  3. to think out, form a plan
  4. to infer, conclude
