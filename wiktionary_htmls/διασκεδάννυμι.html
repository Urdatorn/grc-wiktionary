Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From δια- (dia-) +‎ σκεδάννῡμι (skedánnūmi).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /di.as.ke.dán.nyː.mi/ → /ði.as.ceˈðan.ny.mi/ → /ði.as.ceˈða.ni.mi/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /di.as.ke.dán.nyː.mi/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /di.as.keˈdan.ny.mi/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ði.as.ceˈðan.ny.mi/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ði.as.ceˈðan.ny.mi/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ði.as.ceˈða.ni.mi/

Verb

[edit]

δῐασκεδάννῡμι • (diaskedánnūmi)

  1. to scatter abroad, disperse, disband
