Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From the root of πολύς (polús, “much, many”) +‎ -ιστος (-istos, superlative suffix).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /plêːs.tos/ → /ˈplis.tos/ → /ˈplis.tos/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /plêːs.tos/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈplis.tos/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈplis.tos/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈplis.tos/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈplis.tos/

Adjective

[edit]

πλεῖστος • (pleîstos)

  1. (of number, also of size, extent, strength, etc.) most, very much
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 15.616
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 16.377
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 4.697
    • 750 BCE – 650 BCE, Hesiod, Fr. 33.1
    • 409 BCE, Sophocles, Philoctetes 478
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Protagoras 342A
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Hellenica 7.1.23
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 7.220
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 5.126
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 7.36
    1. (with the article, like οἱ πολλοί) the greatest number, the greatest part of..
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 4.90
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 718A
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 1.5
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 7.3
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 4.34
      • 423 BCE, Euripides, The Suppliants 408
  2. (adverbial, like μάλιστα) most
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 19.287
    • 750 BCE – 650 BCE, Hesiod, Theogony 231
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 2.2.12
    • 409 BCE, Sophocles, Philoctetes 631
    • 406 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus at Colonus 743
    • 438 BCE, Euripides, Alcestis 790
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Pythian Ode 9.97
    • 406 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus at Colonus 720
    • 390 BCE, Plato, Hippias Major 281B
    1. (with the article) for the most part
      • 422 BCE, Aristophanes, The Wasps 260
      • 366 BCE – 348 BCE, Plato, Critias 118C
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, History of Animals 563A.31
      • 129 CE – 216 CE, Galen, Collected Works 17.855
      • 460 BCE – 370 BCE, Hippocrates, Epidemics 6.1.10
  3. (with prepositions)
    1. (διά) furthest off
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 4.115
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 6.11
    2. (εἰς) most
      • 406 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus at Colonus 739
    3. (ἐπί) over the greatest distance, to the greatest extent
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 6.127
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 1.138
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 1.2
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 4.14
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 720D
    4. (κατά)
      • 200 BCE – 118 BCE, Polybius, The Histories 11.4.7
    5. (περί)

Related terms

[edit]
  • πᾰ́μπλειστος (pámpleistos, “in large quantity, number”)
  • πλειστᾰ́κις (pleistákis, “mostly, very often”, adverb)
  • πλείσταρχος (pleístarkhos, “holding widest sway”)
  • πλειστᾰχόθεν (pleistakhóthen, “from most or many places”, adverb)
  • πλειστήρης (pleistḗrēs, “manifold, all the whole”)
  • πλειστηριᾰ́ζω (pleistēriázō, “raise the price”)
  • πλειστηριᾰσμός (pleistēriasmós, “increase of price”) (Hellenistic Koine)
  • πλειστηρῐ́ζομαι (pleistērízomai, “accuse as chief cause”)
  • πλειστοβολέω (pleistoboléō, “throw highest at dice”)
  • πλειστοβολίνδα f (pleistobolínda, “dice-playing”)
  • πλειστόβολος (pleistóbolos, “throwing high”)
  • πλειστοβόλος (pleistobólos, “throwing the most”)
  • πλειστοδυναμέω (pleistodunaméō, “be dominant, prevail”)
  • πλειστοφόρος (pleistophóros, “bearing most”)
  • πλειστογονέω (pleistogonéō, “produce many offsprings”)
  • πλειστολόγως (pleistológōs, “in various ways”, adverb)
  • πλειστολόχειᾰ f (pleistolókheia, “small birthwort, Aristolochia Plistolochia”)
  • πλειστόμβροτος (pleistómbrotos, “crowded with people”)
  • πλειστονῑ́κης (pleistonī́kēs, “victor in many contests”)
  • Πλειστός m (Pleistós, “the river Plistus”)
  • πλειστοτόκος (pleistotókos, “producing most offspring”)
