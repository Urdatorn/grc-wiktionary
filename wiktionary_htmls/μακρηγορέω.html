Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From μακρήγορος (makrḗgoros, “speaking at great length”), from μακρός (makrós, “long”) + ἀγορεύω (agoreúō, “to speak”), + -έω (-éō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ma.krɛː.ɡo.ré.ɔː/ → /ma.kri.ɣoˈre.o/ → /ma.kri.ɣoˈre.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ma.krɛː.ɡo.ré.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ma.kre̝.ɡoˈre.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ma.kri.ɣoˈre.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ma.kri.ɣoˈre.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ma.kri.ɣoˈre.o/

Verb

[edit]

μακρηγορέω • (makrēgoréō)

  1. to speak at great length
