Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Impersonal forms of δέω (déō, “to bind”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /dêː/ → /ði/ → /ði/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /dêː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /di/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ði/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ði/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ði/

Verb

[edit]

δεῖ • (deî)

  1. (with personal accusative and infinitive) it behoves one to, it is necessary to, one must
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 9.337
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Olympian Ode 6.48
    • 343 BCE, Demosthenes, On the False Embassy 235
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Ajax 556
    • 428 BCE, Euripides, Hippolytus
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 3.4.35
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Oeconomicus 7.20
    • 484 BCE – 425 BCE, Herodotus, 1 8
    • 406 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus at Colonus 570
    1. (with impersonal accusative and infinitive)
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, 5 26
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.161
      • 429 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus the King 825
    2. (when used in absolute an infinitive may be supplied)
      • 406 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus at Colonus 1442
      • Philetaerus Comicus, Ἀταλ. 1
  2. (with impersonal genitive) there is need of, there is wanting
    • 525 BCE – 455 BCE, Aeschylus, Prometheus Bound 870
    • 470 BCE, Aeschylus, The Suppliants 407
    1. (with a personal dative)
      • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, Agamemnon 848
      • 431 BCE, Euripides, Medea 565
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 1.71
    2. (with personal accusative)
      • 525 BCE – 455 BCE, Aeschylus, Prometheus Bound 86
      • c. 440 BCE, Euripides, Rhesus 837
    3. (nominative, rare)
      • 423 BCE, Euripides, The Suppliants 594
      • 480 BCE – 411 BCE, Antiphon of Rhamnus, Collected Works 142.43
  3. (neuter participle, absolute use) it being needful, fitting
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Protagoras 355D
    • 361 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Midias 90
    • 423 BCE, Aristophanes, The Clouds 989
    • 484 BCE – 425 BCE, Herodotus, 3 65
    • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Fabius Maximus 9
    • 200 BCE – 118 BCE, Polybius, The Histories 2.37.5

Inflection

[edit]
  • Present indicative: δεῖ (deî)
  • Present subjunctive: δέῃ (déēi), δῇ (dêi)
  • Present optative: δέοι (déoi)
  • Present infinitive: δεῖν (deîn)
  • Present participle: δέον (déon), δεῖν (deîn)
  • Imperfect: ἔδει (édei), ἔδεε (édee) (Ionic)
  • Future: δεήσει (deḗsei)
  • Aorist: ἐδέησε (edéēse)
