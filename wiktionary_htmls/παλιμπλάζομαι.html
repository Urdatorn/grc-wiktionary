Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From πάλιν (pálin) +‎ πλάζω (plázō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pa.lím.plaz.do.mai̯/ → /paˈlim.pla.zo.mɛ/ → /paˈlim.bla.zo.me/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pa.lím.plaz.do.mai̯/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /paˈlim.pla.zo.mɛ/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /paˈlim.pla.zo.mɛ/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /paˈlim.pla.zo.me/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /paˈlim.bla.zo.me/

Verb

[edit]

πᾰλῐ́μπλᾰζομαι • (palímplazomai)

  1. (Epic) to be driven or turned back

Usage notes

[edit]

Only attested as (various forms of) an aorist passive participle παλιμπλαγχθείς (palimplankhtheís).
