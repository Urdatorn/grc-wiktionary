Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐνέχω (enékhō, “to hold within”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /é.no.kʰos/ → /ˈe.no.xos/ → /ˈe.no.xos/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /é.no.kʰos/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈe.no.kʰos/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈe.no.xos/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈe.no.xos/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈe.no.xos/

Adjective

[edit]

ἔνοχος • (énokhos) m or f (neuter ἔνοχον); second declension

  1. held in, bound by
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Metaphysics 1009B.17
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Politics 1337B.17
    • 350 BCE – 200 BCE, Apollodorus of Carystus, The Laconian Woman 1
  2. (law) liable to, subject to
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 869B
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Memorabilia 1.2.64
    • 70 CE – 110 CE, The Gospel of Matthew 5:22
    • 480 BCE – 411 BCE, Antiphon of Rhamnus, Third Tetralogy 1.6
    • 445 BCE – 380 BCE, Lysias, Against Alcibiades 1 9
    • 343 BCE, Demosthenes, On the False Embassy 201
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, On the Trierarchic Crown 4
    • 436 BCE – 338 BCE, Isocrates, On the Peace 7
    • 389 BCE – 314 BCE, Aeschines, On the Embassy 146
    • 389 BCE – 314 BCE, Aeschines, Against Timarchus 185
    1. liable
      • 366 BCE – 348 BCE, Plato, Theaetetus 148B
      • 480 BCE – 411 BCE, Antiphon of Rhamnus, On the Choreutes 46
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 914E
      • 445 BCE – 380 BCE, Lysias, Against Alcibiades 1 5
      • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Septuagint, 2 Maccabees 13.6
      • 36 BCE – 30 BCE, Diodorus Siculus, Historical Library 27.4
      • 70 CE – 110 CE, The Gospel of Matthew 26:66
    2. (rare, with preposition)
      • 440 BCE – 390 BCE, Andocides, On the Mysteries 79
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Rhetoric 1384B.2
    3. (absolute) guilty
      • 480 BCE – 411 BCE, Antiphon of Rhamnus, Against the Stepmother for Poisoning 11
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Politics 1269A.3
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Rhetoric 1380A.3
      • 480 BCE – 411 BCE, Antiphon of Rhamnus, Third Tetralogy 1.1
      • 480 BCE – 411 BCE, Antiphon of Rhamnus, On the Choreutes 17
      • 360 BCE, Plato, The Sophist 261A
