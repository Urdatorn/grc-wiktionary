Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology 1

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *kelh₂- (“to beat, break”). Cognate with Latin percellō.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /klá.ɔː/ → /ˈkla.o/ → /ˈkla.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /klá.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈkla.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈkla.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈkla.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈkla.o/

Verb

[edit]

κλᾰ́ω • (kláō)

  1. to break, to break off, to break into pieces
  2. (of geometric lines, passive voice) to be broken or deflected
  3. to break, to weaken, to frustrate

Etymology 2

[edit]

Attic form of κλαίω (klaíō, “weep”). Root of κλαϝ- (klaw-).
