Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ᾰ̓νᾰ- (ana-, “up”) +‎ ῐ̔́στημῐ (hístēmi, “to stand”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /a.nís.tɛː.mi/ → /aˈnis.ti.mi/ → /aˈnis.ti.mi/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /a.nís.tɛː.mi/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /aˈnis.te̝.mi/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /aˈnis.ti.mi/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /aˈnis.ti.mi/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /aˈnis.ti.mi/

Verb

[edit]

ᾰ̓νῐ́στημῐ • (anístēmi)

  1. active of the present, imperfect, future, and first aorist, and the perfect active ᾰ̓νέστᾰκᾰ (anéstaka)
    1. (transitive) to make to stand up, raise up
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 24.515, (compare Odyssey 14.319)
      • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Ajax 788
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Protagoras 317E
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Memorabilia 1.4.11

        • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 10.32
        • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 24.551

        • 446 BCE – 411 BCE, Eupolis, Incert. 8
        • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Women of Trachis 979

        • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 24.551, (compare Aeschylus, Agamemnon 1361)
        • 406 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus at Colonus 276
        • 389 BCE – 314 BCE, Aeschines, Collected Works 6.28

        • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.102

      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Hellenica 2.4.42

        • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 9.73

      3. (transitive) to make to ascend
        • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Moralia 2.784
      4. (of sportsmen, transitive) to put up game, to spring
        • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 1.5.3
