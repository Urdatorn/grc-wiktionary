Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ἐρέττω (eréttō) — Attic

Etymology

[edit]

Denominative verb, from ἐρέτης (erétēs, “rower”) +‎ -σσω (-ssō).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.rés.sɔː/ → /eˈres.so/ → /eˈre.so/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.rés.sɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /eˈres.so/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /eˈres.so/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /eˈres.so/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /eˈre.so/

Verb

[edit]

ἐρέσσω • (eréssō)

  1. to row, paddle
  2. to speed by rowing
  3. to put in quick motion, ply
  4. (passive voice, of a bow) to be plied, handled
  5. to row through, traverse
