Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐκ- (ek-, “out”) +‎ ἀφανίζω (aphanízō, “to do away with, destroy”), the latter from ἀφανής (aphanḗs, “unseen”) +‎ -ίζω (-ízō, “verb-forming suffix”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ek.sa.pʰa.níz.dɔː/ → /ek.sa.ɸaˈni.zo/ → /ek.sa.faˈni.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ek.sa.pʰa.níz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ek.sa.pʰaˈni.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ek.sa.ɸaˈni.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ek.sa.faˈni.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ek.sa.faˈni.zo/

Verb

[edit]

ἐξᾰφᾰνίζω • (exaphanízō)

  1. to destroy utterly
  2. (passive voice) to disappear utterly
