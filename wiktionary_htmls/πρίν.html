Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • πρείν (preín)

Etymology

[edit]

Appears to be related to πρό (pró), Latin prior, priscus and Lithuanian prie, but the exact formation is uncertain. Perhaps from an old locative *pr-i of Proto-Indo-European *pró (“forward”).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /prín/ → /prin/ → /prin/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /prín/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /prin/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /prin/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /prin/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /prin/
