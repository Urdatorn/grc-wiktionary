Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ὄσδω (ósdō) — Doric

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Hellenic *óďďō, from Proto-Indo-European *h₃ed-ye-, from *h₃ed-. Cognates include Latin odor and Lithuanian úosti.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /óz.dɔː/ → /ˈo.zo/ → /ˈo.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /óz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈo.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈo.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈo.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈo.zo/

Verb

[edit]

ὄζω • (ózō)

  1. to smell (i.e. exude a scent) [with genitive]
    • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, Agamemnon 1310
    • 446 BCE – 386 BCE, Aristophanes, The Acharnians 192
    • 446 BCE – 386 BCE, Aristophanes, The Acharnians 852
    • 391 BCE, Aristophanes, Assemblywomen 524
    • 424 BCE, Aristophanes, The Knights 892
    • 425 BCE, Aristophanes, Lysistrata 616
    • 423 BCE, Aristophanes, The Clouds 50
    • 423 BCE, Aristophanes, The Clouds 398
    • 423 BCE, Aristophanes, The Clouds 1007
    • 422 BCE, Aristophanes, The Wasps 38
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 5.60
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 9.210
    • 5th century BC, Pherecrates, Fragmenta 67
    • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Alexander the Great 20
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Symposium 2.4
  2. (impersonal) there is a smell
    • 421 BCE, Aristophanes, Peace 529
    • 388 BCE, Aristophanes, Plutus 1020
    • 422 BCE, Aristophanes, The Wasps 1059
    • 519 BCE – 422 BCE, Cratinus, Jun. 1
    • 500 BCE – 350 BCE, Hermippus, Collected Works 82.8
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 3.23
    • 445 BCE – 380 BCE, Lysias, Against Andocides 1
    • 5th century BC, Pherecrates, Fragmenta 25
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cynegeticus 5.1
