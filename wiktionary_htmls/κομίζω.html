Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From κομέω (koméō) +‎ -ίζω (-ízō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ko.míz.dɔː/ → /koˈmi.zo/ → /koˈmi.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ko.míz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /koˈmi.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /koˈmi.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /koˈmi.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /koˈmi.zo/

Verb

[edit]

κομίζω • (komízō)

  1. to take care of
  2. to carry, bring

Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Ancient Greek κομίζω (komízō, “to carry, bring, convey”).

Verb

[edit]

κομίζω • (komízo) (past κόμισα)

  1. to bring, carry
    κομίζω γλαύκα εις Αθήνας ― komízo gláfka eis Athínas ― carry coals to Newcastle (literally, “bring owls to Athens”)
