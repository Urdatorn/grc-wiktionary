Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • πεύθομαι (peúthomai) — Poetic

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *bʰewdʰ-. Cognates include Sanskrit बोधति (bodhati), Gothic 𐌰𐌽𐌰𐌱𐌹𐌿𐌳𐌰𐌽 (anabiudan), and Old English bēodan (English bede, bid).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pyn.tʰá.no.mai̯/ → /pynˈθa.no.mɛ/ → /pinˈθa.no.me/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pyn.tʰá.no.mai̯/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /pynˈtʰa.no.mɛ/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /pynˈθa.no.mɛ/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /pynˈθa.no.me/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /pinˈθa.no.me/

Verb

[edit]

πῠνθάνομαι • (punthánomai)

  1. to learn
    1. to learn (accusative) from (genitive)
      • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, Agamemnon 599
      • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, The Libation Bearers 737
      • 405 BCE, Aristophanes, The Frogs 1417
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.91
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 17.408
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 10.537
      • 406 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus at Colonus 1266
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 1.22
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cyropaedia 4.1.3
    2. (with accusative) to hear or learn
      • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, The Libation Bearers 765
      • 480 BCE – 411 BCE, Antiphon of Rhamnus, On the Murder of Herodes 25
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.22
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 2.119
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 2.411
      • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Pythian Ode 7.7
    3. (with genitive) to hear about, ask about
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 15.224
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 1.281
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 2.256
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 635b
      • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Electra 35
    4. (with participle) to learn that
      • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, The Libation Bearers 763
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 9.58
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 1.257
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 17.377
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 19.322
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 4.732
      • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Ajax 692
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 4.6
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 7.31
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 1.7.16
    5. (with interrogative clause) to ask
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 3.68
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laches 196c
      • 360 BCE, Plato, The Sophist 216d
      • 200 BCE – 118 BCE, Polybius, The Histories 3.107.6
      • 406 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus at Colonus 11
      • 406 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus at Colonus 993
