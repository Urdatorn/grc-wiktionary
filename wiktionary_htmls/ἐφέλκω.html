Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ἐπέλκω (epélkō) — Ionic

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐπι- (epi-) +‎ ἕλκω (hélkō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.pʰél.kɔː/ → /eˈɸel.ko/ → /eˈfel.ko/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.pʰél.kɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /eˈpʰel.ko/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /eˈɸel.ko/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /eˈfel.ko/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /eˈfel.ko/

Verb

[edit]

ἐφέλκω • (ephélkō)

  1. to draw on, drag or trail after, lead
  2. to bring on, bring in its train
  3. to drink off
  4. (middle voice) to draw to oneself, attract
  5. (middle voice) to draw or pull over
  6. (middle voice) to bring on consequences
  7. (middle voice) to assume
