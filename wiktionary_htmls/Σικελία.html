Etymology

[edit]

From Σῐ́κελος (Síkelos, “Sicel”) +‎ -ῐ́ᾱ (-íā).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /si.ke.lí.aː/ → /si.ceˈli.a/ → /si.ceˈli.a/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /si.ke.lí.aː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /si.keˈli.a/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /si.ceˈli.a/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /si.ceˈli.a/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /si.ceˈli.a/

Proper noun

[edit]

Σῐκελῐ́ᾱ • (Sikelíā) f (genitive Σῐκελῐ́ᾱς); first declension

  1. Sicily (an island and former kingdom in Europe)

Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Ancient Greek Σικελία (Sikelía).

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): /si.ceˈli.a/

Proper noun

[edit]

Σικελία • (Sikelía) f

  1. Sicily (The largest island in the Mediterranean Sea, an autonomous region of Italy, close to Africa and separated from Tunisia and Libya by the Strait of Sicily)
