Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • πηγνύω (pēgnúō)
  • πήσσω (pḗssō)

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *peh₂ǵ- (“to coagulate, become fixed”). Cognates include Latin pangō and Old English fōn (English fang).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pɛ̌ː.ɡnyː.mi/ → /ˈpi.ɣny.mi/ → /ˈpi.ɣni.mi/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pɛ̌ː.ɡnyː.mi/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈpe̝.ɡny.mi/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈpi.ɣny.mi/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈpi.ɣny.mi/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈpi.ɣni.mi/
