Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From συν- (sun-, “together, with”) +‎ διαλῡ́ω (dialū́ō, “dissolve”)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /syn.di.a.ly̌ː.ɔː/ → /syn.di.aˈly.o/ → /sin.di.aˈli.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /syn.di.a.ly̌ː.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /syn.di.aˈly.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /syn.di.aˈly.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /syn.di.aˈly.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /sin.di.aˈli.o/

Verb

[edit]

συνδῐᾰλῡ́ω • (sundialū́ō)

  1. to help in putting an end to something
  2. (mediopassive) to help to pay
  3. (passive voice) to be dissipated, melt away with, to be abolished at the same time
