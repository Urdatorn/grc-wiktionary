Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From μετα- (meta-) +‎ λαμβάνω (lambánō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /me.ta.lam.bá.nɔː/ → /me.ta.lamˈba.no/ → /me.ta.lamˈba.no/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /me.ta.lam.bá.nɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /me.ta.lamˈba.no/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /me.ta.lamˈba.no/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /me.ta.lamˈba.no/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /me.ta.lamˈba.no/

Verb

[edit]

μετᾰλαμβάνω • (metalambánō)

  1. to have or get a share of, to partake of [with genitive]
  2. (middle voice) to get possession of, lay claim to
  3. (Koine) to eat or take (food)
  4. to share (someone's) society [with genitive]
  5. to take after, succeed to
  6. to take in exchange, substitute
  7. to interchange
