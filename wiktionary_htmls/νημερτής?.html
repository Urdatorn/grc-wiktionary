Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • νᾱμερτής (nāmertḗs) — Choral Doric

Etymology

[edit]

From νη- (nē-) +‎ , variant of ἀ- (a-, “un-”) + the root of ἁμαρτάνω (hamartánō, “to err”) + -ής (-ḗs, adjective suffix).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /nɛː.mer.tɛ̌ːs/ → /ni.merˈtis/ → /ni.merˈtis/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /nɛː.mer.tɛ̌ːs/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ne̝.merˈte̝s/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ni.merˈtis/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ni.merˈtis/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ni.merˈtis/

Adjective

[edit]

νημερτής • (nēmertḗs) m or f (neuter νημερτές); third declension (Epic)

  1. Not making mistakes: unerring, infallible, sure
