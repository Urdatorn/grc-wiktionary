Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From κᾰτᾰ- (kata-, “against”) +‎ ἠχέω (ēkhéō, “sound”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ka.tɛː.kʰé.ɔː/ → /ka.tiˈçe.o/ → /ka.tiˈçe.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ka.tɛː.kʰé.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ka.te̝ˈkʰe.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ka.tiˈçe.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ka.tiˈçe.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ka.tiˈçe.o/

Verb

[edit]

κᾰτηχέω • (katēkhéō)

  1. to resound
    1. to sound amiss
  2. to teach by word of mouth
    1. to teach, instruct
    2. (in Christian writers) to instruct in the elements of religion, catechize
