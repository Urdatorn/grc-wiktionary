Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From νεώτερος (neṓteros, “newer”) +‎ -ίζω (-ízō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ne.ɔː.te.ríz.dɔː/ → /ne.o.teˈri.zo/ → /ne.o.teˈri.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ne.ɔː.te.ríz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ne.o.teˈri.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ne.o.teˈri.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ne.o.teˈri.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ne.o.teˈri.zo/

Verb

[edit]

νεωτερίζω • (neōterízō)

  1. (intransitive) to attempt anything new, make a violent change
  2. (intransitive) to attempt political changes, make innovations or revolutionary movements
  3. (transitive) to revolutionize
