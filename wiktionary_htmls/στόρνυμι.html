Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • στορνύω (stornúō), στρώννῡμι (strṓnnūmi), στρωννύω (strōnnúō), στορεννύω (storennúō), στορέννυμι (storénnumi)

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Hellenic *stórnūmi, from Proto-Indo-European *sterh₃- (“to spread, extend”).[1] Likely related to Swedish strö (“spread, sprinkle”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /stór.nyː.mi/ → /ˈstor.ny.mi/ → /ˈstor.ni.mi/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /stór.nyː.mi/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈstor.ny.mi/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈstor.ny.mi/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈstor.ny.mi/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈstor.ni.mi/

Verb

[edit]

στόρνῡμι • (stórnūmi)

  1. to smooth out, spread
