Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ἐκπλήττω (ekplḗttō) — Attic

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐκ- (ek-, “out of”) +‎ πλήσσω (plḗssō, “to strike, to smite”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ek.plɛ̌ːs.sɔː/ → /ekˈplis.so/ → /ekˈpli.so/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ek.plɛ̌ːs.sɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ekˈple̝s.so/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ekˈplis.so/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ekˈplis.so/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ekˈpli.so/

Verb

[edit]

ἐκπλήσσω • (ekplḗssō)

  1. to strike out of, to drive away from, to expel
    1. to drive out of one's senses by a sudden shock, to amaze, to astound
      1. (generally, of any sudden, overpowering passion) to be struck with desire
      2. to frighten
