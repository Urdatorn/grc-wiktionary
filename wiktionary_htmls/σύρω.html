Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *twer- (“to twirl, swirl”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /sy̌ː.rɔː/ → /ˈsy.ro/ → /ˈsi.ro/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /sy̌ː.rɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈsy.ro/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈsy.ro/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈsy.ro/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈsi.ro/

Verb

[edit]

σῡ́ρω • (sū́rō)

  1. to draw, drag, or trail along
  2. to drag by force, hale
  3. to sweep or carry away

Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Ancient Greek σῡ́ρω (sū́rō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): /ˈsi.ro/
  • Hyphenation: σύ‧ρω

Verb

[edit]

σύρω • (sýro) (past έσυρα, passive σύρομαι)

  1. (formal) to drag, Alternative form of σέρνω (sérno)
  2. to draw a line
