Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *h₃n̥-né-h₂-ti, from the root *h₃neh₂- (“to enjoy”). Cognate with Proto-Germanic *unnaną.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /o.ní.nɛː.mi/ → /oˈni.ni.mi/ → /oˈni.ni.mi/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /o.ní.nɛː.mi/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /oˈni.ne̝.mi/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /oˈni.ni.mi/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /oˈni.ni.mi/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /oˈni.ni.mi/

Verb

[edit]

ὀνί̆νημῐ • (onínēmi)

  1. to help, to support, to be useful
  2. to please, to delight
