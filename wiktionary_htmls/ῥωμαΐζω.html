Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Ῥώμη (Rhṓmē, “Rome”) +‎ -ίζω (-ízō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /r̥ɔː.maː.íz.dɔː/ → /ro.maˈi.zo/ → /ro.maˈi.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /r̥ɔː.maː.íz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ro.maˈi.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ro.maˈi.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ro.maˈi.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ro.maˈi.zo/

Verb

[edit]

ῥωμαΐζω • (rhōmaḯzō)

  1. to speak or write Latin
    • 95 CE – 165 CE, Appian, Roman History 7.41
  2. to be of the Roman party
    • 95 CE – 165 CE, Appian, Roman History 8.68
  3. to Romanize
  4. to behave like a Roman
