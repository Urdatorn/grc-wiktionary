Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Latin Februārius.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ɸe.βruˈa.ri.os/ → /fe.vruˈa.ri.os/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /pʰe.bruˈa.ri.os/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ɸe.βruˈa.ri.os/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /fe.vruˈa.ri.os/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /fe.vruˈa.ri.os/

Adjective

[edit]

Φεβρουάριος • (Phebrouários) m (feminine Φεβρουαρίᾱ, neuter Φεβρουάριον); first/second declension

  1. of or pertaining to February
  2. (nominalized, masculine) February

Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • Φλεβάρης m (Fleváris) — colloquial or less formal, literary

Etymology

[edit]

Learned borrowing from Koine Greek Φεβρουάριος (Phebrouários), from Latin Februārius. Doublet of Φλεβάρης (Fleváris).

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): /fe.vɾuˈa.ɾi.os/
  • Hyphenation: Φε‧βρου‧ά‧ρι‧ος

Proper noun

[edit]

Φεβρουάριος • (Fevrouários) m (plural Φεβρουάριοι)

  1. (calendar) February
