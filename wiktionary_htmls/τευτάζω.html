Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • τευτᾰ́σσω (teutássō)

Etymology

[edit]

From ταὐτός (tautós, “the same”) +‎ -άζω (-ázō, suffix to form a verb), with a change of vocals.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /teu̯.táz.dɔː/ → /teɸˈta.zo/ → /tefˈta.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /teu̯.táz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /tewˈta.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /teɸˈta.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /tefˈta.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /tefˈta.zo/

Verb

[edit]

τευτᾰ́ζω • (teutázō)

  1. to be employed upon, engaged in, concerned with a thing
  2. to be busy, bustling
  3. to bid or order one repeatedly to do a thing
