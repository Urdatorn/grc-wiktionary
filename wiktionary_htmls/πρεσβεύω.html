Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

πρέσβυς (présbus) +‎ -εύω (-eúō)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /prez.běu̯.ɔː/ → /prezˈβe.βo/ → /prezˈve.vo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /prez.běu̯.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /prezˈbe.wo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /prezˈβe.βo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /prezˈve.vo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /prezˈve.vo/

Verb

[edit]

πρεσβεύω • (presbeúō)

  1. (intransitive) to be the elder or eldest
  2. to take the first place, be best
  3. to rank before, take precedence of [with genitive]
  4. to rule over [with genitive]
  5. (transitive) to place (someone or something) as eldest or first, to put first in rank
  6. to pay honor or worship
  7. (passive voice) to hold the first place
  8. to serve an ambassador
  9. to negotiate (e.g. peace) as an ambassador
  10. (middle voice) to send ambassadors
