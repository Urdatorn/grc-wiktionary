Etymology

[edit]

From Old Median *Huvaxšarah.[1][2]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ky.ak.sá.rɛːs/ → /cy.akˈsa.ris/ → /ci.akˈsa.ris/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ky.ak.sá.rɛːs/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ky.akˈsa.re̝s/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /cy.akˈsa.ris/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /cy.akˈsa.ris/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ci.akˈsa.ris/

Proper noun

[edit]

Κυαξάρης • (Kuaxárēs) m (genitive Κυαξάρου); first declension
Κυαξάρης • (Kuaxárēs) m (genitive Κυαξάρους); third declension

  1. Cyaxares
