Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • μᾱνύω (mānúō) — Doric

Etymology

[edit]

Perhaps from Proto-Hellenic *mānúō, from Proto-Indo-European *(s)meh₂néwti, from *(s)meh₂- (“to beckon”) or Pre-Greek.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /mɛː.ný.ɔː/ → /miˈny.o/ → /miˈni.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /mɛː.ný.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /me̝ˈny.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /miˈny.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /miˈny.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /miˈni.o/

Verb

[edit]

μηνύω • (mēnúō)

  1. to show, to inform
  2. to denounce

Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Ancient Greek μηνύω.

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): /miˈni.o/
  • Hyphenation: μη‧νύ‧ω

Verb

[edit]

μηνύω • (minýo) (past μήνυσα, passive μηνύομαι)

  1. (transitive) to sue; file a lawsuit against somebody
    Είπαν ότι θα τον μηνύσουν. ― Eípan óti tha ton minýsoun. ― They said they were going sue him.
