Ancient Greek

[edit]
 Zoe on Wikipedia

Etymology

[edit]

Already in the Septuagint translation. From ζωή (zōḗ, “life”), fashioned as a semantic loan from Hebrew חַוָּה (ḥawwā, “Eve”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /zdɔː.ɛ̌ː/ → /zoˈi/ → /zoˈi/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /zdɔː.ɛ̌ː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /zoˈe̝/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /zoˈi/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /zoˈi/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /zoˈi/

Proper noun

[edit]

Ζωή • (Zōḗ) f (genitive Ζωῆς); first declension (Koine)

  1. a female given name, Zoe or Zoë, meaning “life”
