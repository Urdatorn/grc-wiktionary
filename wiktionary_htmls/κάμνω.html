Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *ḱemh₂- (“exert oneself, get tired”). Cognate with Sanskrit शम् (śam, “to toil, arrange”), Hindi शांति (śānti, “peace and quiet”), as well as perhaps Middle Irish cuma (“trouble”) and cumal (“female slave”).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /kám.nɔː/ → /ˈkam.no/ → /ˈkam.no/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /kám.nɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈkam.no/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈkam.no/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈkam.no/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈkam.no/

Verb

[edit]

κᾰ́μνω • (kámnō)

  1. to exert oneself, labour, work hard
  2. to be weary
  3. to be sick, ill
  4. to suffer
