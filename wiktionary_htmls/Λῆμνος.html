Alternative forms

[edit]
  • Λᾶμνος (Lâmnos) — Doric

Etymology

[edit]

From Phoenician 𐤋𐤁𐤍 (lbn), from Proto-Semitic *laban- (“white”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /lɛ̂ːm.nos/ → /ˈlim.nos/ → /ˈlim.nos/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /lɛ̂ːm.nos/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈle̝m.nos/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈlim.nos/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈlim.nos/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈlim.nos/

Proper noun

[edit]

Λῆμνος • (Lêmnos) f (genitive Λήμνου); second declension

  1. Lemnos
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 1.593
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 8.283
    • 300 BCE – 250 BCE, Apollonius of Rhodes, Argonautica 1.851
