Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

ἀνα- (ana-) +‎ σκάπτω (skáptō) The present tense system includes a T progressive aspect marker.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /a.nas.káp.tɔː/ → /a.nasˈkap.to/ → /a.nasˈkap.to/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /a.nas.káp.tɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /a.nasˈkap.to/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /a.nasˈkap.to/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /a.nasˈkap.to/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /a.nasˈkap.to/

Verb

[edit]

ᾰ̓νᾰσκᾰ́πτω • (anaskáptō)

  1. to dig up
  2. to extirpate (of plants)
  3. to raze to the ground (of buildings)
