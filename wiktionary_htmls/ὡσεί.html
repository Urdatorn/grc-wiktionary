Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ὡς (hōs, “as”) + εἰ (ei, “if”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /hɔː.sěː/ → /oˈsi/ → /oˈsi/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /hɔː.sěː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /(h)oˈsi/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /oˈsi/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /oˈsi/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /oˈsi/

Adverb

[edit]

ὡσεί • (hōseí)

  1. as if, as though
    1. (with optative)
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 2.780
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 11.389
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 9.314
    2. (ὡσεί τε, with subjunctive)
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 9.481
    3. (ὡς εἰ or ὡς εἴ τε, with indicative)
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 13.492
      • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Olympian Ode 7.1
  2. (in mere comparisons, as if, like, just)
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 16.59
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 7.36
    • 750 BCE – 650 BCE, Hesiod, The Shield of Heracles 290
    • 500 BCE – 400 BCE, Bacchylides, Collected Works 11.1
    • 442 BCE, Sophocles, Antigone 653
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Electra 234
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 5.374
    • 750 BCE – 650 BCE, Hesiod, The Shield of Heracles 194
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 11.474
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 14.254
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Pythian Ode 4.112
    • 750 BCE – 650 BCE, Hesiod, The Shield of Heracles 189
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Theocritus, Collected Works 25.163
    • 7th–6th centuries BC, Homeric Hymn to Demeter 215
  3. (like ὡς, with numerals or measures of time and space) about
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 7.109
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Hellenica 1.2.9
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Septuagint, Genesis 24.55
    • 70 CE – 110 CE, The Gospel of Matthew 14:21
