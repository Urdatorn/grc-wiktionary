Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ὑποκρίνομαι (hupokrínomai, “to play a part on stage”) +‎ -τής (-tḗs).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /hy.po.kri.tɛ̌ːs/ → /y.po.kriˈtis/ → /i.po.kriˈtis/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /hy.po.kri.tɛ̌ːs/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /(h)y.po.kriˈte̝s/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /y.po.kriˈtis/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /y.po.kriˈtis/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /i.po.kriˈtis/

Noun

[edit]

ῠ̔ποκρῐτής • (hupokritḗs) m (genitive ῠ̔ποκρῐτοῦ); first declension

  1. one who answers: interpreter, expounder
  2. (Attic) stage actor
    1. (figurative) pretender, dissembler, hypocrite
