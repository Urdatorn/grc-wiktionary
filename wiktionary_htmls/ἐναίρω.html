Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ἐνναίρω (ennaírō)

Etymology

[edit]

From ἔναρα (énara) with a *y progressive aspect marker.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.nǎi̯.rɔː/ → /eˈnɛ.ro/ → /eˈne.ro/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.nǎi̯.rɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /eˈnɛ.ro/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /eˈnɛ.ro/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /eˈne.ro/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /eˈne.ro/

Verb

[edit]

ἐναίρω • (enaírō)

  1. to kill, slay (especially in battle)
    Synonyms: καίνω (kaínō), κτείνω (kteínō)
  2. to destroy
