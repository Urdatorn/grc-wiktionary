Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἄση (ásē, “surfeit, nausea”) +‎ -ᾰ́ω (-áō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /a.sá.ɔː/ → /aˈsa.o/ → /aˈsa.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /a.sá.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /aˈsa.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /aˈsa.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /aˈsa.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /aˈsa.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἀσᾰ́ω • (asáō)

  1. to glut oneself, take a surfeit
  2. to feel loathing or nausea, caused by surfeit
  3. (figuratively) to be disgusted or vexed at a thing
