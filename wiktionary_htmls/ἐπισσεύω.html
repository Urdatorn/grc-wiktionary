Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ἐπισεύω (episeúō) — rare; only post-Epic

Etymology

[edit]

ἐπι- (epi-) +‎ σεύω (seúō)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.pis.sěu̯.ɔː/ → /e.pisˈse.βo/ → /e.piˈse.vo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.pis.sěu̯.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /e.pisˈse.wo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /e.pisˈse.βo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /e.pisˈse.vo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /e.piˈse.vo/

Verb

[edit]

ἐπισσεύω • (episseúō) (chiefly Epic)

  1. to set upon, incite against, send
  2. (in middle) to rush on or at, hasten on, speed to (especially in hostile sense)
