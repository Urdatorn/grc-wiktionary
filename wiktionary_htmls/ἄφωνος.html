Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἀ- (a-, not) +‎ φωνή (phōnḗ, “voice”) +‎ -ος (-os).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /á.pʰɔː.nos/ → /ˈa.ɸo.nos/ → /ˈa.fo.nos/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /á.pʰɔː.nos/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈa.pʰo.nos/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈa.ɸo.nos/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈa.fo.nos/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈa.fo.nos/

Adjective

[edit]

ᾰ̓́φωνος • (áphōnos) m or f (neuter ᾰ̓́φωνον); second declension

  1. voiceless, dumb
    Synonyms: ἐνεός (eneós), κωφός (kōphós), μυνδός (mundós), μύρκος (múrkos)
  2. (of children) unable to speak
  3. intestate
  4. (ἄφωνα (γράμματα, στοιχεῖα)) the consonants, particularly the mutes
