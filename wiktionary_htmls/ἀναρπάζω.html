Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἀνα- (ana-) +‎ ἁρπάζω (harpázō)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /a.nar.páz.dɔː/ → /a.narˈpa.zo/ → /a.narˈpa.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /a.nar.páz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /a.narˈpa.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /a.narˈpa.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /a.narˈpa.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /a.narˈpa.zo/

Verb

[edit]

ἀναρπάζω • (anarpázō)

  1. to snatch up
  2. to snatch away, carry off, steal
  3. to rescue
  4. to take by storm, plunder
