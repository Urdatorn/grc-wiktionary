Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐπανα- (epana-, “re-”) +‎ ἵημι (híēmi, “throw”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.pa.nǐː.ɛː.mi/ → /e.paˈni.i.mi/ → /e.paˈni.i.mi/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.pa.nǐː.ɛː.mi/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /e.paˈni.e̝.mi/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /e.paˈni.i.mi/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /e.paˈni.i.mi/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /e.paˈni.i.mi/

Verb

[edit]

ἐπᾰνῑ́ημῐ • (epanī́ēmi)

  1. to let loose at
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 5.405
  2. (construed with the accusative) to let go, to give up
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes 2.30
  3. to dismiss, to remit
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes 18.177
    • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Lycurgus 22
  4. (construed with the genitive) to release from
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cynegeticus 7.1
  5. (transitive) to relax
    • 320 CE – 403 CE, Oribasius, Collected Works 6.38.5
    • 40 CE – 90 CE, Dioscorides, Eup. 2.56
  6. (intransitive) to relax, to leave off, to slacken, to become easier
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Phaedrus 266a
    • 460 BCE – 370 BCE, Hippocrates, Epidemics 3.17
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cynegeticus 4.5
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes 32.25
