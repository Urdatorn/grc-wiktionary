Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From προ- (pro-) +‎ ἐπιβουλεύω (epibouleúō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pro.e.pi.buː.lěu̯.ɔː/ → /pro.e.pi.βuˈle.βo/ → /pro.e.pi.vuˈle.vo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pro.e.pi.buː.lěu̯.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /pro.e.pi.buˈle.wo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /pro.e.pi.βuˈle.βo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /pro.e.pi.vuˈle.vo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /pro.e.pi.vuˈle.vo/

Verb

[edit]

προεπῐβουλεύω • (proepibouleúō)

  1. to plot against beforehand
