Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἀγρεύς (agreús, “hunter”); equivalent to ἄγρα (ágra, “chase, prey”) +‎ -εύω (-eúō, verb-forming suffix).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /a.ɡrěu̯.ɔː/ → /aˈɣre.βo/ → /aˈɣre.vo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /a.ɡrěu̯.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /aˈɡre.wo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /aˈɣre.βo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /aˈɣre.vo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /aˈɣre.vo/

Verb

[edit]

ἀγρεύω • (agreúō) (Attic, Ionic, Koine)

  1. (transitive) to take by hunting or fishing, catch, take
    • 405 BCE, Euripides, The Bacchae 1204
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.95
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cynegeticus 12.6
    • 405 BCE, Euripides, The Bacchae 434
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Fragments 554
    • 413 BCE, Euripides, Iphigenia in Tauris 1163
    • 425 BCE, Euripides, Andromache 842
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 5.3.8
    • Palatine Anthology 9.94
    • Palatine Anthology 12.113
    1. (transitive, figuratively) to hunt after, thirst for
      • 405 BCE, Euripides, The Bacchae 138
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Fragments 675.11
      • Palatine Anthology 7.196
      • The Gospel of Mark 12:13
