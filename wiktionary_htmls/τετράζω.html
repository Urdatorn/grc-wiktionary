Ancient Greek

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /te.tráz.dɔː/ → /teˈtra.zo/ → /teˈtra.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /te.tráz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /teˈtra.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /teˈtra.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /teˈtra.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /teˈtra.zo/

Etymology 1

[edit]

From τέτρᾰξ (tétrax, “hazel grouse”) +‎ -άζω (-ázō, suffix to form a verb).

Verb

[edit]

τετρᾰ́ζω • (tetrázō)

  1. to cackle like the hazel grouse, on laying an egg

Etymology 2

[edit]

From τετρᾰ́ς (tetrás, “space of four days”) +‎ -άζω (-ázō, suffix to form a verb).

Verb

[edit]

τετρᾰ́ζω • (tetrázō)

  1. to observe a four-day cycle
