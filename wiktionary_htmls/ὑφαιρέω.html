Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ὑπο- (hupo-) +‎ αἱρέω (hairéō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /hy.pʰai̯.ré.ɔː/ → /y.ɸɛˈre.o/ → /i.feˈre.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /hy.pʰai̯.ré.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /(h)y.pʰɛˈre.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /y.ɸɛˈre.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /y.feˈre.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /i.feˈre.o/

Verb

[edit]

ὑφαιρέω • (huphairéō)

  1. to seize underneath or inwardly
  2. to draw or take away from under
  3. to take away underhand, filch away, purloin
  4. (middle voice) to rob (somebody of something)
