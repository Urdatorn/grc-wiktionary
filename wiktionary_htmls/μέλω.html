Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Uncertain. Suggested connection to μέλλω (méllō, “to intend, to mean to”), but Beekes doesn't find this convincing.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /mé.lɔː/ → /ˈme.lo/ → /ˈme.lo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /mé.lɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈme.lo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈme.lo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈme.lo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈme.lo/

Verb

[edit]

μέλω • (mélō)

  1. (active voice and middle voice)
    1. (with passive meaning) to be an object of care or interest [with dative ‘to someone’]
    2. (with active meaning) to care for, be interested in [with genitive ‘someone, something’]
  2. (impersonal, active voice) to be a matter of concern [with genitive ‘something of concern’ and dative ‘to someone’]
