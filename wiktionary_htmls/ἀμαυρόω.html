Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

ἀμαυρός (amaurós, “dim; feeble”) +‎ -όω (-óō)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /a.mau̯.ró.ɔː/ → /a.maˈβro.o/ → /a.maˈvro.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /a.mau̯.ró.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /a.mawˈro.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /a.maˈβro.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /a.maˈvro.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /a.maˈvro.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἀμαυρόω • (amauróō)

  1. to darken
  2. to weaken
  3. (in passive) to grow dark, dim, or pale
