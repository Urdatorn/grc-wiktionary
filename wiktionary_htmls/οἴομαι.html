Ancient Greek

FWOTD – 30 September 2014

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ὀΐομαι (oḯomai) — Homeric
  • οἶμαι (oîmai) — Attic

Etymology

[edit]

For earlier ὀϝίομαι (owíomai), from Proto-Indo-European *h₂ew- (“perceive”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ǒi̯.o.mai̯/ → /ˈy.o.mɛ/ → /ˈi.o.me/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ǒi̯.o.mai̯/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈy.o.mɛ/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈy.o.mɛ/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈy.o.me/

Verb

[edit]

οἴομαι • (oíomai)

  1. to anticipate or to conclude likely with less than complete evidence: to conjecture, to guess or to surmise, in a variety of significations
    1. to consider certain or highly probable: to expect, to look to, to look for
    2. to have a presentiment of something: to forebode, to foretell, to predict, to presage
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 13.283
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 10.248
    3. to look for a contingency: to hope, to fear
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 24.401
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 9.339
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 10.380
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 19.390
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 14.298
    4. to express full persuasion, either modestly or ironically: to think, to suppose
      Synonym: προσδοκᾰ́ω (prosdokáō)
    5. (when the event rests with oneself): to have in mind as a goal or motive for action: to aim, to mean, to intend, to purport, to purpose, to propose
    6. to conceive, to deem, to imagine, to suspect (of an opinion or judgment)
      Synonym: σκέπτομαι (sképtomai)
  2. (construction)

Usage notes

[edit]

Homer has forms with diaeresis, and several forms of the present middle often have long ῑ for the sake of meter; otherwise ὀίομαι (oíomai), for instance, would begin in short–short–short, which is unmetrical.
