Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Hellenic *lípos, from i-Proto-Indo-European *leyp- (“fat substance”).

Stem λῐπ- (lip-), λῐπο- (lipo-), λῐπᾰρ- (lipar-, “fat”). Different from λῐπ- (lip-, “wanting”) of the aorist ἔλῐπον (élipon) of the verb λείπω (leípō, “leave; lack”). Different from λῑπᾰρ- (līpar-) from λίπτω (líptō, “desire”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /lí.pos/ → /ˈli.pos/ → /ˈli.pos/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /lí.pos/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈli.pos/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈli.pos/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈli.pos/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈli.pos/

Noun

[edit]

λίπος • (lípos) n (genitive λίπους); third declension

  1. animal fat, lard, tallow

Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Ancient Greek λίπος. For sense substance with fat, Semantic loan from French graisse or English fat. See stem λιπ- for sense: “fat”. Different from λιπ- for sense: “wanting” (as in ελλιπής).

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): /ˈli.pos/
  • Hyphenation: λί‧πος

Noun

[edit]

λίπος • (lípos) n (plural λίπη)

  1. fat, tallow
