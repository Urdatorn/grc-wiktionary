Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *meh₂d-. Cognates include Sanskrit मदति (madati), ममत्ति (mamatti), Latin madeō, and Old Irish maidim. Compare Serbo-Croatian modar (“blue”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ma.dá.ɔː/ → /maˈða.o/ → /maˈða.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ma.dá.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /maˈda.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /maˈða.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /maˈða.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /maˈða.o/

Verb

[edit]

μᾰδάω • (madáō)

  1. to be moist, wet
  2. to melt away
  3. (of hair) to fall off
  4. to be bald
