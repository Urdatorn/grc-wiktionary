Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From συν- (sun-, “with, together”) +‎ πίνω (pínō, “to drink”). Compare Ancient Greek ἀναπίνω (anapínō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /sym.pǐː.nɔː/ → /symˈpi.no/ → /simˈbi.no/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /sym.pǐː.nɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /symˈpi.no/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /symˈpi.no/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /symˈpi.no/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /simˈbi.no/

Verb

[edit]

σῠμπῑ́νω • (sumpī́nō)

  1. to drink together
    • 389 BCE – 314 BCE, Aeschines, On the Embassy 52
    • 620 BCE – 500 BCE, Alcaeus of Mytilene, Collected Works 54
    • 446 BCE – 386 BCE, Aristophanes, The Acharnians 277
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.121
    • 5th century BC, Pherecrates, Fragmenta 153.5
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Minos 320a
    • 385 BCE – 380 BCE, Plato, Symposium 213a
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cyropaedia 5.2.28
  2. to absorb
    • 280 BCE – 220 BCE, Philo of Byzantium, Belopoeica 61.40
  3. to soak
    • 40 CE – 90 CE, Dioscorides, Materia medica 1.25
    • 625 CE – 690 CE, Paul of Aegina, Medical Compendium in Seven Books 3.2.6
