Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

μετα- (meta-) +‎ ἀνίστημι (anístēmi), itself from ἀνα- (ana-) +‎ ἵστημι (hístēmi).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /me.ta.nís.tɛː.mi/ → /me.taˈnis.ti.mi/ → /me.taˈnis.ti.mi/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /me.ta.nís.tɛː.mi/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /me.taˈnis.te̝.mi/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /me.taˈnis.ti.mi/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /me.taˈnis.ti.mi/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /me.taˈnis.ti.mi/

Verb

[edit]

μετᾰνίστημῐ • (metanístēmi)

  1. to remove from one's country
  2. (generally) to remove
  3. (in passive) to migrate
