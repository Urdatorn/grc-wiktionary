Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • κάρκαρος (kárkaros)

Etymology

[edit]

Unknown, probably onomatopoeic, from an earlier reduplicated stem *kʰar-kʰar-. The variation may also indicate Pre-Greek origin.[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /kár.kʰa.ros/ → /ˈkar.xa.ros/ → /ˈkar.xa.ros/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /kár.kʰa.ros/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈkar.kʰa.ros/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈkar.xa.ros/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈkar.xa.ros/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈkar.xa.ros/

Adjective

[edit]

κάρχᾰρος • (kárkharos) m or f (neuter κάρχᾰρον); second declension or
κάρχᾰρος • (kárkharos) m (feminine καρχάρᾱ, neuter κάρχᾰρον); first/second declension

  1. sharp-pointed, jagged, with sharp or jagged teeth
  2. (figurative) sharp, biting (of language)
