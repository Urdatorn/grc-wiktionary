Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Probably Pre-Greek. Has been compared to βαίνω (baínō); Beekes finds this unconvincing.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /bas.táz.dɔː/ → /βasˈta.zo/ → /vasˈta.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /bas.táz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /basˈta.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /βasˈta.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /vasˈta.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /vasˈta.zo/

Verb

[edit]

βαστάζω • (bastázō)

  1. to lift, lift up, raise
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 11.594:
      λᾶαν βαστάζοντα πελώριον ἀμφοτέρῃσιν.
      lâan bastázonta pelṓrion amphotérēisin.
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 21.405
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Ajax 827
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Electra 1470
    1. (figuratively) to lift up, exalt, ennoble
      • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Olympian Ode 12.19
      • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Isthmian Ode 3.8
  2. to bear, carry
    • 525 BCE – 455 BCE, Aeschylus, Prometheus Bound 1019
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Electra 1129
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Electra 1216
    • 500 BCE – 350 BCE, Hermippus, Collected Works 46.2
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Theocritus, Collected Works 16.78
    1. to hold in one's hands
      • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Electra 905
      • 409 BCE, Sophocles, Philoctetes 657
      • 409 BCE, Sophocles, Philoctetes 1127
      • 341 BCE – 270 BCE, Epicurus, Letters 2.35
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Rhetoric 1413B.12
    2. to have in mind, consider, weigh, make proof of
      • 525 BCE – 455 BCE, Aeschylus, Prometheus Bound 888
      • 411 BCE, Aristophanes, Thesmophoriazusae 437
      • 446 BCE – 411 BCE, Eupolis, Dippers 6
  3. to carry off, take away
    • 50 CE – 100 CE, The Gospel of John 20:15
    • 200 BCE – 118 BCE, Polybius, The Histories 32.15.4
    • 93 CE – 94 CE, Josephus, Antiquities of the Jews 1.19.9
    • 3rd century CE, Diogenes Laertius, Lives and Opinions of Eminent Philosophers 4.59
    • 125 CE – 200 CE, Lucian, The Ass 16
    • 50 CE – 100 CE, The Gospel of John 12:6
    • 250 CE – 350 CE, Athenaeus, Deipnosophistae 2.46F
  4. to handle, touch
    • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, Agamemnon 35
    • 406 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus at Colonus 1105

Verb

[edit]

βαστάζω • (vastázo) (past βάσταξα/βάστηξα, passive βαστάζομαι, p‑past βαστάχτηκα, ppp βασταγμένος)

  1. to carry, support (bring something as a load)
  2. (figuratively) to bear, withstand (loss, etc)
