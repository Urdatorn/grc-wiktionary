Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From μνήμων (mnḗmōn, “mindful”) +‎ -εύω (-eúō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /mnɛː.mo.něu̯.ɔː/ → /mni.moˈne.βo/ → /mni.moˈne.vo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /mnɛː.mo.něu̯.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /mne̝.moˈne.wo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /mni.moˈne.βo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /mni.moˈne.vo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /mni.moˈne.vo/

Verb

[edit]

μνημονεύω • (mnēmoneúō)

  1. (active voice)
    1. to call to mind, remember, think of
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Phaedo 103B
      • 37 CE – 100 CE, Josephus, Against Apion 1.1
      • 436 BCE – 338 BCE, Isocrates, Panathenaicus 128
      • 366 BCE – 348 BCE, Plato, Critias 117E
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.36
      • 472 BCE, Aeschylus, The Persians 783
      • 409 BCE, Sophocles, Philoctetes 121
      • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Otho 1
      • 391 BCE, Aristophanes, Assemblywomen 264
      • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 480A
      • 349 BCE, Demosthenes, First Olynthiac 11
      • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Septuagint, 2 Maccabees 10.6
      1. (absolute)
        • 519 BCE – 422 BCE, Cratinus, Collected Works 122
        • 380 BCE, Plato, Gorgias 499E
        • 342 BCE – 290 BCE, Menander, The Girl who has her hair cropped 142
        • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, On Memory 453A.6
    2. to call to another's mind, mention, say
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 646B
      • Phalaris, Letters 2
      • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Themistocles 32
      • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Septuagint, 1 Maccabees 12.11
    3. to serve as μνήμων (mnḗmōn)
      • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Septuagint, 1 Maccabees 11.3
  2. (passive voice) to be remembered, had in memory
    • 430 BCE, Euripides, Herakles' Children 334
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 1.23
    • 436 BCE – 338 BCE, Isocrates, Panathenaicus 128
    • 330 BCE, Demosthenes, On the Crown 231
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Memorabilia 4.8.2
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 2.47
    • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 600A
