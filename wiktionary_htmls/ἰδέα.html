Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • εἰδέα (eidéa) (Koine)
  • ἰδέη (idéē) (Ionian)

Etymology

[edit]

Maybe from Proto-Hellenic *widéhā, from Proto-Indo-European *widéseh₂, feminine derivation from *wéydos (whence εἶδος (eîdos)), from *weyd- (“to see”).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /i.dé.aː/ → /iˈðe.a/ → /iˈðe.a/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /i.dé.aː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /iˈde.a/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /iˈðe.a/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /iˈðe.a/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /iˈðe.a/

Noun

[edit]

ἰδέᾱ • (idéā) f (genitive ἰδέᾱς); first declension

  1. form, shape
  2. look, appearance, semblance
  3. type, sort, class
  4. style
  5. notion, idea
