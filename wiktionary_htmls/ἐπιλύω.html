Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐπι- (epi-, “upon, on”) +‎ λῡ́ω (lū́ō, “loosen”)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.pi.ly̌ː.ɔː/ → /e.piˈly.o/ → /e.piˈli.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.pi.ly̌ː.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /e.piˈly.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /e.piˈly.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /e.piˈly.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /e.piˈli.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἐπῐλῡ́ω • (epilū́ō)

  1. (generally) to set free, release, loose, untie
  2. to solve, explain
  3. to confute an accusation
  4. (middle voice) to manumit
  5. to release, discharge a debtor, pay, discharge a debt
  6. (future tense, middle voice, with passive meaning) to lose strength, give in
