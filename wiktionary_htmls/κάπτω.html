Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Hellenic *kapťō, from Proto-Indo-European *kap-yé-ti, from *kap- (“seize, grab”). Exact cognate of Latin capiō, (English have).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /káp.tɔː/ → /ˈkap.to/ → /ˈkap.to/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /káp.tɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈkap.to/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈkap.to/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈkap.to/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈkap.to/

Verb

[edit]

κᾰ́πτω • (káptō)

  1. to gulp down
    Synonym: λᾰ́πτω (láptō)
    • 414 BCE, Aristophanes, The Birds 245
    • Nicocles 140D
    • Xenarchus, Πορφύρα 3
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, History of Animals 8.3.10
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, History of Animals 8.6.1
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, History of Animals 9.35
    • 4th century BC, Eubulus, Antiope 2.7
