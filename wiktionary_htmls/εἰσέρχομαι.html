Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From εἰσ- (eis-, “into”) +‎ ἔρχομαι (érkhomai, “to go”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /eː.sér.kʰo.mai̯/ → /iˈser.xo.mɛ/ → /iˈser.xo.me/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /eː.sér.kʰo.mai̯/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /iˈser.kʰo.mɛ/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /iˈser.xo.mɛ/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /iˈser.xo.me/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /iˈser.xo.me/

Verb

[edit]

εἰσέρχομαι • (eisérkhomai)

  1. to go into, enter, invade
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 3.184
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 22.56
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 2.321
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Hellenica 5.4.28
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 5.36
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 7.1.27
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cyropaedia 1.5.1
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cyropaedia 3.3.13
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 7.3.21
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Ways and Means 5.12
    1. (of the chorus or of actors) to come upon the stage, to enter
      • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 580B
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 6.1.9
      • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Electra 700
    2. (as an Attic law term, of the accuser) to come into court
      • 380 BCE, Plato, Gorgias 522B
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Neaera 1
      • 330 BCE, Demosthenes, On the Crown 278
      1. (of the parties, with accusative) to enter upon the charge
        • 330 BCE, Demosthenes, On the Crown 105
        • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Aphobus 2 27
      2. (of the accused) to come before the court
        • 400 BCE – 387 BCE, Plato, Apology 29C
        • 330 BCE, Demosthenes, On the Crown 103
      3. (of the cause) to be brought in
        • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Lacritus 49
    3. to enter on an office
      • 480 BCE – 411 BCE, Antiphon of Rhamnus, Collected Works 146.25
      • 155 CE – 235 CE, Cassius Dio, 41 39
    4. (figuratively)
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 17.157
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 15.407
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 6.125
      • 472 BCE, Aeschylus, The Persians 845
      • 407 BCE, Euripides, Iphigenia in Aulis 1411
      • 407 BCE, Euripides, Iphigenia in Aulis 57
      • 406 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus at Colonus 372
      • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Fragments 678
      • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 330D
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Lys. 218C
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.86
      1. (impersonal, with infinitive)
        • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 3.42
        • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 7.46
        • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 835D
        • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 8.137
        • 525 BCE – 455 BCE, Aeschylus, Prometheus Bound 1002
