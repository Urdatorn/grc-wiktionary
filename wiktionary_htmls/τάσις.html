Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Hellenic *tə́tsis, from the oblique stem of Proto-Indo-European *tén-tis, from *ten- (“stretch”). Equivalent to τείνω (teínō, “I stretch”) +‎ -σῐς (-sis).

Pronunciation 1

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /tá.sis/ → /ˈta.sis/ → /ˈta.sis/

  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈta.sis/

Noun

[edit]

τᾰ́σῐς • (tásis) f (genitive τᾰ́σεως); third declension

  1. stretching, tension
  2. extension
  3. (in music) pitch of the voice
  4. intensity, force
