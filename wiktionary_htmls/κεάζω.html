Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *ḱes- (“to cut up, to slaughter”); compare κείω (keíō, “to cleave”), Latin castrō (“to castrate”), Sanskrit शस्त्र (śastra, “sword, dagger”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ke.áz.dɔː/ → /ceˈa.zo/ → /ceˈa.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ke.áz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /keˈa.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ceˈa.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ceˈa.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ceˈa.zo/

Verb

[edit]

κεᾰ́ζω • (keázō)

  1. to split, cleave wood
  2. (of lightening) to shiver
  3. to pound, rub to pieces
