Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *seh₁y-. Cognates include Old Church Slavonic сито (sito), сѣꙗти (sějati), Lithuanian sijóti, and Old Norse sald.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ɛː.tʰé.ɔː/ → /iˈθe.o/ → /iˈθe.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ɛː.tʰé.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /e̝ˈtʰe.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /iˈθe.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /iˈθe.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /iˈθe.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἠθέω • (ēthéō)

  1. to sift, strain
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Herodas, Collected Works 3.33
    • 200 BCE – 100 BCE, Nicander, Alexipharmaca 324
    • 386 BCE – 367 BCE, Plato, Cratylus 402d
    • 360 BCE, Plato, Timaeus 59b
