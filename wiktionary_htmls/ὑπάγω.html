Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ὑπο- (hupo-, “beneath”) +‎ ἄγω (ágō, “to lead”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /hy.pá.ɡɔː/ → /yˈpa.ɣo/ → /iˈpa.ɣo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /hy.pá.ɡɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /(h)yˈpa.ɡo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /yˈpa.ɣo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /yˈpa.ɣo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /iˈpa.ɣo/

Verb

[edit]

ῠ̔πᾰ́γω • (hupágō)

  1. (transitive)
    1. to lead or bring under
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 16.148
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 23.261
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 6.73
      • 442 BCE, Sophocles, Antigone 353
      1. to bring under one's power
        • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 8.106
        • 125 CE – 200 CE, Lucian, pro Merc. Cond. 3
        • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 7.46
        • 125 CE – 200 CE, Lucian, Dialogues of the Gods 18.1
    2. to bring a person before the judgment seat, to accuse, impeach
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 9.93
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 6.82
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Hellenica 2.3.28
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 3.70
      • 445 BCE – 380 BCE, Lysias, Collected Works 105.4
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Hellenica 2.3.33
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Hellenica 2.3.12
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Hellenica 5.4.24
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 6.136
      • 458 BCE, Euripides, Electra 1155
      • 125 CE – 200 CE, Lucian, The Runaways 11
    3. to lead slowly on, to lead on by degrees, by deceit
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cynegeticus 5.15
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 9.94
      • 480 BCE – 406 BCE, Euripides, Cyclops 505
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cyropaedia 1.6.37
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cyropaedia 3.2.8
      • 399 BCE, Plato, Euthyphro 14C
      • 412 BCE, Euripides, Helen 826
      • 445 BCE – 380 BCE, Lysias, Collected Works 105.4
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, History of Animals 9.8.6
      • 425 BCE, Euripides, Andromache 428
      • 436 BCE – 338 BCE, Isocrates, Collected Works 100D
      • 341 BCE, Demosthenes, On the Chersonese 62
      • 425 BCE, Euripides, Andromache 906
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 2.1.18
      • 436 BCE – 338 BCE, Isocrates, Collected Works 82B
      • 346 BCE, Demosthenes, On the Peace 10
      • 389 BCE – 314 BCE, Aeschines, Collected Works 25.23
      • 330 BCE, Demosthenes, On the Crown 188
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Conon 19
    4. to take away from under, withdraw
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 11.163
      • 450 BCE – 350 BCE, Archippus, Hercules Getting Married 3
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 2.76
      1. to draw off
        • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 4.127
      2. to purge the bowels
        • 1 CE – 100 CE, Aretaeus, De Curatione Acutorum Morborum 1.10
  2. (intransitive)
    1. to go slowly away, draw off, withdraw, retire
      • 6th century BC, Theognis of Megara, Elegies 921
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 4.120–122
      • 414 BCE, Aristophanes, The Birds 1017
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 4.126
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, History of Animals 9.44.3
    2. to go slowly forwards, draw on
      • 480 BCE – 406 BCE, Euripides, Cyclops 53
      • 423 BCE, Aristophanes, The Clouds 1298
      • 405 BCE, Aristophanes, The Frogs 174
      • 446 BCE – 411 BCE, Eupolis, Dippers 2
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 3.4.48
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 4.2.16
    3. (medicine, of the bowels) to be open
      • 460 BCE – 370 BCE, Hippocrates, Collected Works 396.27
    4. to sink down, squat
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, History of Animals 5.2.6
