Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From κατα- (kata-, “downwards”) +‎ φρονέω (phronéō, “to think, to be arrogant”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ka.ta.pʰro.né.ɔː/ → /ka.ta.ɸroˈne.o/ → /ka.ta.froˈne.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ka.ta.pʰro.né.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ka.ta.pʰroˈne.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ka.ta.ɸroˈne.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ka.ta.froˈne.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ka.ta.froˈne.o/

Verb

[edit]

κᾰταφρονέω • (kataphronéō)

  1. to look down on, think slightly of
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 4.134
    • 405 BCE, Euripides, The Bacchae 199
    • 445 BCE – 380 BCE, Lysias, Collected Works 12.78
    • 400 BCE – 387 BCE, Plato, Apology 28C
    • 380 BCE, Plato, Gorgias 512C
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cynegeticus 1.18
    • 408 BCE – 334 BCE, Antiphanes Comicus, Unknown Work 43
    • 342 BCE – 290 BCE, Menander, The Helmsmen 10
    • 342 BCE – 290 BCE, Menander, The Woman From Boeotia 1
    1. (with accusative) to regard slightly, despise
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 8.10
      • 405 BCE, Euripides, The Bacchae 503
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 6.34
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 8.8
      • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 556D
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Hellenica 7.4.30
    2. to be disdainful, deal contemptuously
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 2.11
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Rhetoric 1378B.15
      • 60 BCE – 7 BCE, Dionysius of Halicarnassus, Roman Antiquities 5.44
      • 405 BCE, Euripides, The Bacchae 199
    3. (with infinitive) to think contemptuously that..., to presume
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.66
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 3.83
  2. (with accusative, only in Ionic writers) to fix one's thoughts upon, aim at
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.59
    • 408 BCE – 334 BCE, Antiphanes Comicus, Archon 1.5
  3. to come to one's senses
    • 460 BCE – 370 BCE, Hippocrates, On the Sacred Disease 15
    • 460 BCE – 370 BCE, Hippocrates, On the Nature of the Woman 3
