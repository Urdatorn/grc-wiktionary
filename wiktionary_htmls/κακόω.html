Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From κακός (kakós) +‎ -όω (-óō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ka.kó.ɔː/ → /kaˈko.o/ → /kaˈko.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ka.kó.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /kaˈko.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /kaˈko.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /kaˈko.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /kaˈko.o/

Verb

[edit]

κᾰκόω • (kakóō)

  1. to treat ill, maltreat, afflict, distress, vex
  2. to hurt, harm
  3. (passive voice) to suffer ill, be in poor condition
  4. to befoul, spoil, ruin
