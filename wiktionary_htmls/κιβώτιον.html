Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From κῑβωτός (kībōtós, “wooden chest”) +‎ -ιον (-ion, diminutive suffix).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /kiː.bɔ̌ː.ti.on/ → /ciˈβo.ti.on/ → /ciˈvo.ti.on/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /kiː.bɔ̌ː.ti.on/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /kiˈbo.ti.on/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ciˈβo.ti.on/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ciˈvo.ti.on/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ciˈvo.ti.on/

Noun

[edit]

κῑβώτῐον • (kībṓtion) n (genitive κῑβωτῐ́ου); second declension

  1. box for drawing lots
    Synonym: σενδούκη (sendoúkē)
  2. voting urn
