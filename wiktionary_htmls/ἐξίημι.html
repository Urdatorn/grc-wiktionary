Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐξ- (ex-) +‎ ἵημι (híēmi).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ek.sǐː.ɛː.mi/ → /ekˈsi.i.mi/ → /ekˈsi.i.mi/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ek.sǐː.ɛː.mi/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ekˈsi.e̝.mi/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ekˈsi.i.mi/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ekˈsi.i.mi/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ekˈsi.i.mi/

Verb

[edit]

ἐξῑ́ημῐ • (exī́ēmi)

  1. to send out, let (someone) go out, dismiss
  2. to take out
  3. to discharge themselves (of rivers)
  4. (middle voice) to get rid of
  5. to divorce
