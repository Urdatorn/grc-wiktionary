Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐνεργής (energḗs, “active, effective”) +‎ -έω (-éō, denominative verbal suffix).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.ner.ɡé.ɔː/ → /e.nerˈʝe.o/ → /e.nerˈʝe.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.ner.ɡé.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /e.nerˈɡe.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /e.nerˈʝe.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /e.nerˈʝe.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /e.nerˈʝe.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἐνεργέω • (energéō)

  1. to be in action or activity, to operate
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Rhetoric 1411B.26
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Nicomachean Ethics 1101A.15
    • 204 CE – 270 CE, Plotinus, Collected Works 6.6.16
    • New Testament, Epistle to the Galatians 2:8
    • 70 CE – 110 CE, The Gospel of Matthew 14:2
    • New Testament, Epistle to the Romans 7:5
  2. (transitive) to effect, execute
    • 200 BCE – 118 BCE, Polybius, The Histories 18.14.8
    • 200 BCE – 118 BCE, Polybius, The Histories 7.5.8
    • 100 BCE – 1 BCE, Athenaeus Mechanicus, On Machines 14.2
    • 200 CE – 300 CE, Aristides Quintilianus, On Music 2.9
    • 200 BCE – 118 BCE, Polybius, The Histories 1.13.5
    • 36 BCE – 30 BCE, Diodorus Siculus, Historical Library 20.95
    • 200 BCE – 118 BCE, Polybius, The Histories 9.12.7
    • 161 CE – 180 CE, Marcus Aurelius, Meditations 4.2
  3. (euphemistically) to have sex
    • 400 BCE – 300 BCE, Diocles of Carystus, Collected Works 141
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Theocritus, Collected Works 4.61
    • Alciphron, Collected Works 3.55
    • Alciphron, Collected Works 1.39
