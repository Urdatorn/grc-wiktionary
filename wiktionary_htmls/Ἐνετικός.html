Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • Ἑνετικός (Henetikós)

Etymology

[edit]

From Ἐνετοί (Enetoí, “Eneti; Veneti; Venetian”) +‎ -ικός (-ikós, “-ic: forming adjectives”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.ne.tiˈkos/ → /e.ne.tiˈkos/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /e.ne.tiˈkos/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /e.ne.tiˈkos/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /e.ne.tiˈkos/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /e.ne.tiˈkos/

Adjective

[edit]

Ἐνετῐκός • (Enetikós) m (feminine Ἐνετῐκή, neuter Ἐνετῐκόν); first/second declension (Koine)

  1. (historical) Enetic, of or related to the Eneti of Paphlagonia
  2. (historical) Enetic, of or related to the Eneti of Illyria
  3. (historical) Venetic, of or related to the Veneti of northeastern Italy
  4. (Koine, Byzantine) Venetian, of or related to Venice
