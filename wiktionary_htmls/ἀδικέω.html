Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἄδικος (ádikos, “wrong, unjust”) +‎ -έω (-éō, stative suffix).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /a.di.ké.ɔː/ → /a.ðiˈce.o/ → /a.ðiˈce.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /a.di.ké.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /a.diˈke.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /a.ðiˈce.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /a.ðiˈce.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /a.ðiˈce.o/

Verb

[edit]

ᾰ̓δῐκέω • (adikéō)

  1. (intransitive) to be in the wrong, to have sinned, to have broken the law
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Rhetoric 1368B.6
    • 442 BCE, Sophocles, Antigone 1059
    • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, The Eumenides 85
    • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, The Eumenides 749
    • 400 BCE – 387 BCE, Plato, Apology 19B
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Memorabilia 1.1.1
    • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 344C
    • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 409A
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Rhetoric 1389B.7
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 3.145
    • 385 BCE – 380 BCE, Plato, Symposium 188A
    • 361 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Midias 175
    • 423 BCE, Aristophanes, The Clouds 25
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Nicomachean Ethics 1123B.32
  2. (transitive) to do one wrong, to wrong one, to injure [with accusative ‘someone’], to break the law
    • 680 BCE – 645 BCE, Archilochus, Supplement 2.13
    • 630 BCE – 570 BCE, Sappho, Collected Works 1.20
    • 540 BCE – 450 BCE, Epicharmus, Collected Works 286
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.112
    • 342 BCE – 290 BCE, Menander, The Farmer 30
    • 388 BCE, Aristophanes, Plutus 460
    • 361 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Midias 129
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Leptines 124
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 854E
    • 406 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus at Colonus 174
    • 431 BCE, Euripides, Medea 265
    • 389 BCE – 314 BCE, Aeschines, Against Ctesiphon 84
    • 385 BCE – 380 BCE, Plato, Symposium 196B
    • 480 BCE – 411 BCE, Antiphon of Rhamnus, Third Tetralogy 4.9
    • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 359A
    • New Testament, Second Epistle of Peter 2:13
