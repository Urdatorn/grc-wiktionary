Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • αἴτημι (aítēmi) — Aeolic

Etymology

[edit]

A denominative of an unattested *αἶτος (*aîtos, “something given: allotment, share”), from Proto-Indo-European *h₂ey- (“to give, take”), whence also αἴνυμαι (aínumai), αἶσα (aîsa), αἴτιος (aítios), διαιτάω (diaitáō).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ai̯.té.ɔː/ → /ɛˈte.o/ → /eˈte.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ai̯.té.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ɛˈte.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ɛˈte.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /eˈte.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /eˈte.o/

Verb

[edit]

αἰτέω • (aitéō)

  1. (usually transitive) to ask for, crave, demand, beg [with accusative ‘something’; or with accusative ‘something’ and accusative ‘from someone’]; or with object omitted
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 5.358
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 17.365
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 10.17
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 20.74
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 5.17
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 22.295
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 2.387
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 3.1
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 8.114
    • 6th century BC, Theognis of Megara, Elegies 556
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 1.3.16
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 18.49
    • 470 BCE, Aeschylus, The Suppliants 340
    1. (transitive) to ask for [with accusative ‘someone’ and infinitive ‘to do something’]
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 3.173
      • 406 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus at Colonus 1334
      • 442 BCE, Sophocles, Antigone 65
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Eryxias 398E
    2. (logic, transitive) to postulate, assume
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Prior Analytics 1.24.2
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Topics 8.13.2
  2. (middle voice, transitive) to ask for oneself, for one's own use or purpose, to claim
    • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, The Libation Bearers 480
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.90
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 9.34
    • 525 BCE – 455 BCE, Aeschylus, Prometheus Bound 822
    • 480 BCE – 411 BCE, Antiphon of Rhamnus, Collected Works 112.41
    • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, The Libation Bearers 480
    • 442 BCE, Sophocles, Antigone 778
    • 445 BCE – 380 BCE, Lysias, Collected Works 154.24
    • 342 BCE – 290 BCE, Menander, Hymnis 5
    • 445 BCE – 380 BCE, Lysias, Collected Works 141.35
  3. (passive voice, of persons) to have a thing begged of one
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 8.111
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 2.97
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Theocritus, Collected Works 14.63
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Isthmian Ode 8.10
    1. (of things) to be asked for
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 8.112
      • 445 BCE – 380 BCE, Lysias, Collected Works 169.17
