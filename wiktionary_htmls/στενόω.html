Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From στενός (stenós, “narrow”) +‎ -όω (-óō, “to make someone do or be something”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ste.nó.ɔː/ → /steˈno.o/ → /steˈno.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ste.nó.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /steˈno.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /steˈno.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /steˈno.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /steˈno.o/

Verb

[edit]

στενόω • (stenóō)

  1. to straiten, confine, contract
