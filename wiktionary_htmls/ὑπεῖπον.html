Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ὑπο- (hupo-) +‎ εἶπον (eîpon).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /hy.pêː.pon/ → /yˈpi.pon/ → /iˈpi.pon/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /hy.pêː.pon/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /(h)yˈpi.pon/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /yˈpi.pon/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /yˈpi.pon/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /iˈpi.pon/

Verb

[edit]

ὑπεῖπον • (hupeîpon)

  1. to say as a foundation or preface, to premise, suggest
  2. to subjoin, add
  3. to suggest an explanation, explain, interpret
