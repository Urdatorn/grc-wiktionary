Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Onomatopoeic of the cicada's chirping noises, like τιτίζω (titízō).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /tét.tiːks/ → /ˈtet.tiks/ → /ˈte.tiks/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /tét.tiːks/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈtet.tiks/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈtet.tiks/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈtet.tiks/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈte.tiks/

Noun

[edit]

τέττῑξ • (téttīx) m (genitive τέττῑγος or τέττῑκος); third declension

  1. cicada (Cicada plebeia or allied species; a winged insect fond of basking on trees, when the male makes a chirping or clicking noise by means of certain drums or “tymbals” underneath its wings)
    Synonym: λᾱκέτᾱς (lākétās)
