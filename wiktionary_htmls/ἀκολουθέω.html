Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἀκόλουθος (akólouthos, “following”) +‎ -έω (-éō, verbal suffix).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /a.ko.luː.tʰé.ɔː/ → /a.ko.luˈθe.o/ → /a.ko.luˈθe.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /a.ko.luː.tʰé.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /a.ko.luˈtʰe.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /a.ko.luˈθe.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /a.ko.luˈθe.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /a.ko.luˈθe.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἀκολουθέω • (akolouthéō)

  1. (transitive, intransitive) to follow, go after, go with [with dative or (rarely) accusative ‘someone’; or with σύν (sún) ‘dat’; or with μετά (metá) ‘gen’; or with ἐπί (epí) ‘acc’]
    • 388 BCE, Aristophanes, Plutus 19
    • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 474C
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laches 187E
    • 445 BCE – 380 BCE, Lysias, Collected Works 193.18
    • 436 BCE – 338 BCE, Isocrates, Collected Works 299C
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 7.5.3
    • 388 BCE, Aristophanes, Plutus 13
    • 342 BCE – 290 BCE, Menander, Unknown Work 32
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 2.98
    • 342 BCE – 290 BCE, Menander, The Flatterer 3
  2. (figuratively)
    1. (transitive) to follow one in a thing, let oneself be led by [with dative or (rarely) accusative ‘someone or something’]
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 3.38
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, First Philippic 39
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Timocrates 95
      • 440 BCE – 390 BCE, Andocides, Collected Works 31.55
    2. (transitive) to follow the thread of a discourse
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Phaedo 107B
    3. (of things, transitive) to follow, be consequent on
      • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 332D
      • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 400E
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, History of Animals 2.1.3
    4. (intransitive) it follows
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Categories 12.2
