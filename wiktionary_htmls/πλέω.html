Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • πλώω (plṓō) — Ionic

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Hellenic *pléwō, from Proto-Indo-European *plew- (“to sail, flow”).[1]

In the present and imperfect, the semivowel *ϝ is lost between vowels, but before a consonant is kept as υ, the second element of a diphthong. Cognate with English float.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /plé.ɔː/ → /ˈple.o/ → /ˈple.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /plé.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈple.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈple.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈple.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈple.o/

Verb

[edit]

πλέω • (pléō)

  1. to sail (in a boat)
  2. to float

Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Ancient Greek πλέω.

Compare Mariupol Greek плэ́ву (plévu).

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): /ˈple.o/
  • Hyphenation: πλέ‧ω

Verb

[edit]

πλέω • (pléo) (past έπλευσα, passive —)

  1. to sail (in a boat)
  2. to float
  3. (figuratively) to have something in abundance, in expressions like:
    πλέω σε πελάγη ευτυχίας ― pléo se pelági eftychías ― I sail in seas of happiness, I am happy
    πλέω στο χρήμα ― pléo sto chríma ― I sail in money, I am rich
    πλέω στο αίμα ― pléo sto aíma ― I sail in blood, I bleed heavily
  4. (figuratively) to be too large (of shoes, clothing, etc)
