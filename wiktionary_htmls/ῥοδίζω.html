Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

ῥόδον (rhódon, “rose”) +‎ -ῐ́ζω (-ízō, denominative verb suffix).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /r̥o.díz.dɔː/ → /roˈði.zo/ → /roˈði.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /r̥o.díz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /roˈdi.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /roˈði.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /roˈði.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /roˈði.zo/

Verb

[edit]

ῥοδῐ́ζω • (rhodízō)

  1. to have a colour or scent like a rose
  2. (transitive) to scent with rose
  3. (transitive) to decorate a grave with roses
