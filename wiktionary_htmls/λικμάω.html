Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *neyk-. Cognates include Lithuanian niekoti, Latvian niekāt, and Welsh nithio.

This etymology is incomplete. You can help Wiktionary by elaborating on the origins of this term. How did the initial n change to l?

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /lik.má.ɔː/ → /likˈma.o/ → /likˈma.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /lik.má.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /likˈma.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /likˈma.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /likˈma.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /likˈma.o/

Verb

[edit]

λικμάω • (likmáō)

  1. to winnow
  2. to scatter, crush, destroy
