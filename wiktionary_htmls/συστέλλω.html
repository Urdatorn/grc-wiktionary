Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From συν- (sun-, “with, together”) +‎ στέλλω (stéllō, “to dispatch, to send”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /sys.tél.lɔː/ → /sysˈtel.lo/ → /sisˈte.lo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /sys.tél.lɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /sysˈtel.lo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /sysˈtel.lo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /sysˈtel.lo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /sisˈte.lo/

Verb

[edit]

συστέλλω • (sustéllō)

  1. to draw together: shorten sail; to draw in, contract
    1. to contract, reduce
      1. to deprive of all the food and drink
    2. to humble, abase
    3. (phonology) to lower, make mean; to pronounce (a syllable) short
  2. to wrap closely up, shroud
    1. to cloak
