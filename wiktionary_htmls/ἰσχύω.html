Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἰσχῡ́ς (iskhū́s, “strength”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /is.kʰy̌ː.ɔː/ → /isˈçy.o/ → /isˈçi.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /is.kʰy̌ː.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /isˈkʰy.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /isˈçy.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /isˈçy.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /isˈçi.o/
later
 
  • IPA(key): /is.kʰý.ɔː/ → /isˈçy.o/ → /isˈçi.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /is.kʰý.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /isˈkʰy.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /isˈçy.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /isˈçy.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /isˈçi.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἰσχῡ́ω or ἰσχῠ́ω • (iskhū́ō or iskhúō)

  1. to be physically strong
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Women of Trachis 234
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cyropaedia 6.1.24
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Ajax 502
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Memorabilia 2.7.7
    • 480 BCE – 411 BCE, Antiphon of Rhamnus, On the Murder of Herodes 93
    • 422 BCE, Aristophanes, The Wasps 357
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Hellenica 6.4.18
  2. to be strong, mighty, powerful, to prevail
    • 525 BCE – 455 BCE, Aeschylus, Prometheus Bound 510
    • 424 BCE, Euripides, Hecuba 1188
    • 414 BCE, Aristophanes, The Birds 1607
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Septuagint, Psalms 12.4
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Septuagint, 1 Maccabees 10.49
    • 408 BCE, Euripides, Orestes 903
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 3.46
    • 349 BCE, Demosthenes, Second Olynthiac 9
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 1.143
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 2.13
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 8.47
    • 389 BCE – 314 BCE, Aeschines, On the Embassy 2
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Nausimachus and Xenopeithes 20
    1. (not of persons) to prevail
      • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, The Eumenides 621
      • 429 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus the King 356
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Pantaenetus 59
      • 343 BCE, Demosthenes, On the False Embassy 241
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Aristogiton 1 71
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Politics 1280A.28
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, On the Accession of Alexander 9
      • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Septuagint, 2 Chronicles 2.6
      • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Pompey 58
      • 64 BCE – 24 CE, Strabo, Geography 14.2.28
      • The Gospel of Mark 5:4
      • 40 CE – 115 CE, Dio Chrysostom, Collected Works 33.22
  3. to be worth
    • 93 CE – 94 CE, Josephus, Antiquities of the Jews 14.7.1
    • 90 CE – 168 CE, Ptolemy, Tetrabiblos 134
    • 200 BCE – 118 BCE, Polybius, The Histories 5.26.13
