Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From κύκλος (kúklos, “circle”) +‎ -όω (-óō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ky.kló.ɔː/ → /cyˈklo.o/ → /ciˈklo.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ky.kló.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /kyˈklo.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /cyˈklo.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /cyˈklo.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ciˈklo.o/

Verb

[edit]

κυκλόω • (kuklóō)

  1. to encircle, surround
  2. to go round
  3. to form a circle
