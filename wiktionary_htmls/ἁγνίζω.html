Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἁγνός (hagnós, “sacred, pure”) +‎ -ίζω (-ízō, denominative verb suffix).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ha.ɡníz.dɔː/ → /aˈɣni.zo/ → /aˈɣni.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ha.ɡníz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /(h)aˈɡni.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /aˈɣni.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /aˈɣni.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /aˈɣni.zo/

Verb

[edit]

ᾰ̔γνῐ́ζω • (hagnízō) (Attic, Koine)

  1. (transitive) to make pure, to purify, cleanse away, especially by water
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Ajax 655
    • 413 BCE, Euripides, Iphigenia in Tauris 1039
    • 416 BCE, Euripides, Herakles 1324
    • 342 BCE – 291 BCE, Diphilus, Collected Works 126.1
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Septuagint, Exodus 19.10
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Septuagint, Joshua 3.5
    • New Testament, Acts of the Apostles 21:24
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Septuagint, Numbers 6.3
  2. (transitive) to hallow the dead by fire
    • 442 BCE, Sophocles, Antigone 545
    • 423 BCE, Euripides, The Suppliants 1211
    1. (transitive) to burn up, destroy
      • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Fragments 116
      • Palatine Anthology 7.49
