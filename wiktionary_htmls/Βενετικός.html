Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Variant of Ἐνετικός (Enetikós) under influence from Latin veneticus.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /βe.ne.tiˈkos/ → /ve.ne.tiˈkos/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /be.ne.tiˈkos/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /βe.ne.tiˈkos/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ve.ne.tiˈkos/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ve.ne.tiˈkos/

Adjective

[edit]

Βενετῐκός • (Benetikós) m (feminine Βενετῐκή, neuter Βενετῐκόν); first/second declension

  1. (Byzantine) Synonym of Ἐνετικός (Enetikós), Venetian, of or related to Venice
    • Pseudo-Germ. 421 C.
    • Porph. Adm. 123.
    • Pach. I, 162.
