Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From φένᾱξ (phénāx, “cheat, imposter”) +‎ -ῐ́ζω (-ízō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pʰe.naː.kíz.dɔː/ → /ɸe.naˈci.zo/ → /fe.naˈci.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pʰe.naː.kíz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /pʰe.naˈki.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ɸe.naˈci.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /fe.naˈci.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /fe.naˈci.zo/

Verb

[edit]

φενᾱκῐ́ζω • (phenākízō)

  1. (intransitive) to lie
  2. (transitive) to trick, cheat, defraud
