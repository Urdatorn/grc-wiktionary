Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *denḱ- (“to bite”). Cognate with Sanskrit दशति (daśati, “to bite”), Old English ġetingan (“to press upon”), and Albanian darë.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /dá.knɔː/ → /ˈða.kno/ → /ˈða.kno/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /dá.knɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈda.kno/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈða.kno/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈða.kno/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈða.kno/

Verb

[edit]

δάκνω • (dáknō)

  1. to bite
