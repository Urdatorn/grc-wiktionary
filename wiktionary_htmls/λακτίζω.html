Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • λᾰκτῐ́σσω (laktíssō)

Etymology

[edit]

From λᾰ́ξ (láx, “with the foot”) +‎ -ίζω (-ízō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /lak.tíz.dɔː/ → /lakˈti.zo/ → /lakˈti.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /lak.tíz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /lakˈti.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /lakˈti.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /lakˈti.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /lakˈti.zo/

Verb

[edit]

λᾰκτῐ́ζω • (laktízō)

  1. to kick with the heel or foot
  2. to trample, tread on something
  3. (figuratively) to kick, struggle
