Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]
  • According to Frisk, of onomatopoeic origin, from μῡ (mū).
  • According to Beekes there are no plausible comparanda and the word is Pre-Greek.
  • Others compare it to Proto-Slavic *myslь (“idea, thought”), as if via Proto-Indo-European *mewHdʰ-.
  • Ernštedt (1953: 55-57) argues that it derived from Egyptian mdwt (“word, speech, affair”). If so, a cognate to Coptic ⲙⲉⲧ- (met-), and related to Coptic ⲙⲟⲩⲧⲉ (moute).
  • Possibly related to Sanskrit मिथ्या (mithyā, “false, spurious, illusory”), from Proto-Indo-European *mewHdʰ- (“to complain or care about something”).

Noun

[edit]

μῦθος • (mûthos) m (genitive μῡ́θου); second declension

  1. something said: word, speech, conversation
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 11.561:
      ἀλλ’ ἄγε δεῦρο, ἄναξ, ἵν’ ἔπος καὶ μῦθον ἀκούσῃς
      all’ áge deûro, ánax, hín’ épos kaì mûthon akoúsēis
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 4.777:
      μῦθον, ὃ δὴ καὶ πᾶσιν ἐνὶ φρεσὶν ἤραρεν ἥμιν.
      mûthon, hò dḕ kaì pâsin enì phresìn ḗraren hḗmin.
    1. public speech
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 1.358:
        ἔργον ἐποίχεσθαι· μῦθος δ’ ἄνδρεσσι μελήσει
        érgon epoíkhesthai; mûthos d’ ándressi melḗsei
      • 750 BCE – 650 BCE, Hesiod, Works and Days 192
    2. (mostly in plural) talk, conversation
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 4.214
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 4.239
    3. advice, counsel, command, order, promise
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 5.493:
        Ὣς φάτο Σαρπηδών, δάκε δὲ φρένας Ἕκτορι μῦθος·
        Hṑs pháto Sarpēdṓn, dáke dè phrénas Héktori mûthos;
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 7.358:
        οἶσθα καὶ ἄλλον μῦθον ἀμείνονα τοῦδε νοῆσαι.
        oîstha kaì állon mûthon ameínona toûde noêsai.
    4. the subject of a speech or talk
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 22.289
      • 458 BCE, Euripides, Electra 346
    5. a resolve, purpose, design, plan
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 1.545
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 3.140
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 4.676
    6. saying, proverb
      • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, The Libation Bearers 314
    7. the talk of men, rumor, report, message
      • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Ajax 226
      • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Ajax 189
  2. tale, story, narrative,
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 3.94
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 4.324
    1. tale, legend, myth
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.45
      1. (in Attic prose) a legend of the early Greek times, before the dawn of history
        • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 330D
    2. a professed work of fiction, fable, such as those of Aesop
      • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 377A
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Meteorology 2.3.2
    3. the plot of a tragedy or comedy
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Poetics 6.8

Usage notes

[edit]

The scholia on the Odyssey 21.71 says that μῦθος (mûthos) is Aeolic for μόθος (móthos), but compare μυθιήτης (muthiḗtēs). It is used there in the significance of "resolve, purpose".
