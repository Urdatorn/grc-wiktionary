Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ὀνυμαίνω (onumaínō) — Aeolic, Doric
  • οὐνομαίνω (ounomaínō) — Ionic

Etymology

[edit]
PIE word
*h₁nómn̥

From Proto-Indo-European *h₃nómn̥yoh₂ (“am naming”). Equal to ὄνομα (ónoma) +‎ -ι̯ω (-i̯ō), but synchronically exhibits -αίνω (-aínō). Cognate with Proto-Germanic *namnijaną,

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /o.no.mǎi̯.nɔː/ → /o.noˈmɛ.no/ → /o.noˈme.no/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /o.no.mǎi̯.nɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /o.noˈmɛ.no/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /o.noˈmɛ.no/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /o.noˈme.no/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /o.noˈme.no/

Verb

[edit]

ὀνομαίνω • (onomaínō)

  1. to name
  2. to utter, speak
  3. to nominate, appoint
