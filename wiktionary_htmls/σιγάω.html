Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From σῑγή (sīgḗ) +‎ -ᾰ́ω (-áō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /siː.ɡá.ɔː/ → /siˈɣa.o/ → /siˈɣa.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /siː.ɡá.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /siˈɡa.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /siˈɣa.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /siˈɣa.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /siˈɣa.o/

Verb

[edit]

σῑγᾰ́ω • (sīgáō)

  1. to be silent, to be still
    • 525 BCE – 455 BCE, Aeschylus, Prometheus Bound 200
    • 428 BCE, Euripides, Hippolytus 312
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 8.61
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 14.90
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 17.393
    • 7th–6th centuries BC, Homeric Hymn to Hermes 93
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Nemean Ode 10.29
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Phaedrus 276a
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 5.6.27
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cyropaedia 5.5.20
    1. (figuratively)
      • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, The Eumenides 935
      • 470 BCE, Aeschylus, The Suppliants 181
      • 438 BCE, Euripides, Alcestis 78
      • 405 BCE, Euripides, The Bacchae 1084
      • 480 BCE – 406 BCE, Euripides, Fragments 781.13
      • 423 BCE, Euripides, The Suppliants 298
      • 380 BCE, Plato, Euthydemus 300b
      • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Theocritus, Collected Works 2.38
  2. (transitive) to hold silent, keep secret
    • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, Agamemnon 36
    • 525 BCE – 455 BCE, Aeschylus, Prometheus Bound 106
    • 525 BCE – 455 BCE, Aeschylus, Prometheus Bound 441
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 5.21
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 7.104
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Fragments 81
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Olympian Ode 9.103
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Fragments 653
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Theocritus, Collected Works 16.54
