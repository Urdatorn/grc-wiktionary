Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Hellenic *génewyom, from the root of γένῠς (génus, “jaw, mouth”).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ɡé.neː.on/ → /ˈʝe.ni.on/ → /ˈʝe.ni.on/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ɡé.neː.on/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈɡe.ni.on/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈʝe.ni.on/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈʝe.ni.on/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈʝe.ni.on/

Noun

[edit]

γένειον • (géneion) n (genitive γενείου); second declension

  1. chin
  2. beard
  3. lion's mane
  4. chaps, jaws
  5. (in the plural) teeth (of a saw)
