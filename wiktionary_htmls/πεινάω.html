Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From πεῖνᾰ (peîna, “hunger”) +‎ -ᾰ́ω (-áō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /peː.ná.ɔː/ → /piˈna.o/ → /piˈna.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /peː.ná.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /piˈna.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /piˈna.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /piˈna.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /piˈna.o/

Verb

[edit]

πεινᾰ́ω • (peináō)

  1. to be hungry
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 3.25
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 16.758
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 18.162
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.13–14
    • 446 BCE – 386 BCE, Aristophanes, The Acharnians 535
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Fragments 199
    • 480 BCE – 406 BCE, Euripides, Fragments 895
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Theocritus, Collected Works 15.148
    • 371 BCE – 287 BCE, Theophrastus, Enquiry into Plants 8.6.2
  2. (with genitive) to hunger after
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 20.137
    1. (figuratively) to hunger after, long for, crave after
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cyropaedia 8.3.39
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Oeconomicus 13.9
      • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 521A
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cyropaedia 7.5.50
      • 70 CE – 110 CE, The Gospel of Matthew 5:6
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Hellenica 1.1.23

Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • πεινώ (peinó) (less frequent variant)

Etymology

[edit]

From Ancient Greek πεινάω (peináō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): /piˈna.o/
  • Hyphenation: πει‧νά‧ω

Verb

[edit]

πεινάω/πεινώ • (peináo/peinó) (past πείνασα, passive —, ppp πεινασμένος)

  1. to be hungry, be famished
