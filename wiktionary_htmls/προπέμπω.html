Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

προ- (pro-) +‎ πέμπω (pémpō)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pro.pém.pɔː/ → /proˈpem.po/ → /proˈpem.bo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pro.pém.pɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /proˈpem.po/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /proˈpem.po/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /proˈpem.po/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /proˈpem.bo/

Verb

[edit]

προπέμπω • (propémpō)

  1. to send before, send on, send forward, send forth
  2. to conduct, attend
  3. to escort, bring on one's way, accompany
  4. to pursue
