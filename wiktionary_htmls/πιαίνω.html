Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From πῑ́ων (pī́ōn, “fat, grass”) +‎ -αίνω (-aínō, verbal suffix).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /piː.ǎi̯.nɔː/ → /piˈɛ.no/ → /piˈe.no/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /piː.ǎi̯.nɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /piˈɛ.no/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /piˈɛ.no/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /piˈe.no/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /piˈe.no/

Verb

[edit]

πῑαίνω • (pīaínō)

  1. (active voice) to fatten, enrich
  2. (passive voice) to be or become fat
  3. (figuratively) to increase, enlarge
  4. (figuratively) to make wanton, excite
  5. (figuratively) to cherish, cheer
