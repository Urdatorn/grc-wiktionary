Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From the root of χράω (khráō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /kʰrai̯z.mé.ɔː/ → /xrɛzˈme.o/ → /xrezˈme.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /kʰrai̯z.mé.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /kʰrɛzˈme.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /xrɛzˈme.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /xrezˈme.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /xrezˈme.o/

Verb

[edit]

χραισμέω • (khraisméō)

  1. to ward off
  2. to defend, help [with dative ‘someone’]

Dialects other than Attic are not well attested. Some forms are based on conjecture. Use with caution. For more details, see Appendix:Ancient Greek dialectal conjugation.
