Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *(s)penh₁- (“to weave, spin”), with semantic shift "to weave, stretch" > "to tense, strain" > "to exert oneself". Cognate with Lithuanian pìnti (“to braid, plait”), Old Church Slavonic пѧти (pęti, “to tie, fix”), Old English spinnan (“to spin”).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pé.no.mai̯/ → /ˈpe.no.mɛ/ → /ˈpe.no.me/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pé.no.mai̯/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈpe.no.mɛ/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈpe.no.mɛ/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈpe.no.me/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈpe.no.me/

Verb

[edit]

πένομαι • (pénomai)

  1. to toil, labor, exert oneself
