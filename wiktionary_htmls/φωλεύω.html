Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From φωλεός (phōleós, “den, lair”) +‎ -εύω (-eúō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pʰɔː.lěu̯.ɔː/ → /ɸoˈle.βo/ → /foˈle.vo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pʰɔː.lěu̯.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /pʰoˈle.wo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ɸoˈle.βo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /foˈle.vo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /foˈle.vo/

Verb

[edit]

φωλεύω • (phōleúō)

  1. (of animals) to lurk in a hole or den
  2. (figuratively, of a disease) to lurk
