Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ἀγαθουργέω (agathourgéō)

Etymology

[edit]

From ἀγαθός (agathós, “good”) +‎ ἔργον (érgon, “work, deed”) +‎ -έω (-éō, verb-forming suffix). Literally, “to do a good deed”.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /a.ɡa.tʰo.er.ɡé.ɔː/ → /a.ɣa.θo.erˈʝe.o/ → /a.ɣa.θo.erˈʝe.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /a.ɡa.tʰo.er.ɡé.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /a.ɡa.tʰo.erˈɡe.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /a.ɣa.θo.erˈʝe.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /a.ɣa.θo.erˈʝe.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /a.ɣa.θo.erˈʝe.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἀγαθοεργέω • (agathoergéō) (rare, Koine)

  1. (intransitive) to succeed, to do what is good
    • New Testament, Acts of the Apostles 14:17
    • New Testament, First Epistle to Timothy 6:18
