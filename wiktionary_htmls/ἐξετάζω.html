Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐξ- (ex-, “ex-, from”) +‎ ἐτάζω (etázō, “to examine, test”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ek.se.táz.dɔː/ → /ek.seˈta.zo/ → /ek.seˈta.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ek.se.táz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ek.seˈta.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ek.seˈta.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ek.seˈta.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ek.seˈta.zo/

Verb

[edit]

ἐξετάζω • (exetázō)

  1. to examine well or closely, inquire into, scrutinise
  2. to examine or question a person closely
  3. to estimate, compare τι πρός τι one thing by or with another
  4. (of gold) to prove by testing
