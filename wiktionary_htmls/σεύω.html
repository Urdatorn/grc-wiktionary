Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Beekes considers the etymology uncertain. Frisk and Chantraine both consider it to be related to κινέω (kinéō) and κίω (kíō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /sěu̯.ɔː/ → /ˈse.βo/ → /ˈse.vo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /sěu̯.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈse.wo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈse.βo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈse.vo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈse.vo/

Verb

[edit]

σεύω • (seúō)

  1. to chase, drive, impel
  2. (in middle) to rush, hasten, hurry
