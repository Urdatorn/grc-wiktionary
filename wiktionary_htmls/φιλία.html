Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • φῐλῐ́η (philíē) — Ionic

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pʰi.lí.aː/ → /ɸiˈli.a/ → /fiˈli.a/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pʰi.lí.aː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /pʰiˈli.a/

  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /fiˈli.a/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /fiˈli.a/

Etymology 1

[edit]

From φῐλέω (philéō, “to love”) +‎ -ῐ́ᾱ (-íā).

Noun

[edit]

φῐλῐ́ᾱ • (philíā) f (genitive φῐλῐ́ᾱς); first declension

  1. friendship, love, affection, fondness
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Symposium 8.15, (Compare Plato, Symposium 179C, Phaedrus 237C, 255E)
    • 436 BCE – 338 BCE, Isocrates, Collected Works 9B
    • Antidotus 130
    • 385 BCE – 380 BCE, Plato, Symposium 185C
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Hiero 3.7
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Poetics 14.9
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 1.6.3, (Compare Isocrates, Collected Works 352B)
    • 6th century BC, Theognis of Megara, Elegies 306, (Compare Andocides, Collected Works 27.10)
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 7.130
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Memorabilia 2.6.29
    • 428 BCE, Euripides, Hippolytus 253
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cyropaedia 3.1.28
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 3.2.8
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Phaedrus 237C
    • 445 BCE – 380 BCE, Lysias, Collected Works 214D
    • 436 BCE – 338 BCE, Isocrates, Collected Works 424A
    • 6th century BC, Theognis of Megara, Elegies 1102
    • 438 BCE, Euripides, Alcestis 930
    • 436 BCE – 338 BCE, Isocrates, Collected Works 302E
    • 445 BCE – 380 BCE, Lysias, Collected Works 114.2
    • 436 BCE – 338 BCE, Isocrates, Collected Works 118B
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 5.5, (Compare 6.34, 6.78)
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, The Statesman 304E
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Memorabilia 1.2.10
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 823B
    • 436 BCE – 338 BCE, Isocrates, 88 D
    • 436 BCE – 338 BCE, Isocrates, Collected Works 100C
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 1.91
    • 6th century BC, Theognis of Megara, Elegies 600.1102
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 7.7.29
    • 408 BCE, Euripides, Orestes 138
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 3.82
    • 385 BCE – 380 BCE, Plato, Symposium 182C
  2. friendliness, kindliness, without any affection
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Nicomachean Ethics 2.7.13
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Nicomachean Ethics 4.6.5
  3. sexual love, like ἔρως (érōs)
    • Lxx, Proverbs 5.19
  4. (with regard to things) fondness for
    • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 581A
    • 350 BCE, Aristotle, On the Heavens 3.7.10
  5. regarded as the natural force which unites discordant elements and movements, as νεῖκος (neîkos) keeps them apart
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Physics 8.1.3
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Metaphysics 1.4.2
