Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

ἔναρα (énara) +‎ -ίζω (-ízō)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.na.ríz.dɔː/ → /e.naˈri.zo/ → /e.naˈri.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.na.ríz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /e.naˈri.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /e.naˈri.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /e.naˈri.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /e.naˈri.zo/

Verb

[edit]

ἐνᾰρίζω • (enarízō)

  1. to strip of armor, despoil
  2. to kill, slay (especially in battle)
