Ancient Greek

[edit]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ék.seː.mi/ → /ˈek.si.mi/ → /ˈek.si.mi/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ék.seː.mi/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈek.si.mi/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈek.si.mi/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈek.si.mi/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈek.si.mi/

Etymology 1

[edit]

ἐκ- (ek-, “out”) +‎ εἶμι (eîmi, “to go”)

Verb

[edit]

ἔξειμῐ • (éxeimi)

  1. to go out, come out
    • 423 BCE, Aristophanes, The Clouds 633
    • 250 CE – 350 CE, Athenaeus, Deipnosophistae 13.580C
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.139
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 20.179
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 1.374
    • 406 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus at Colonus 909
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.94
    • 155 CE – 235 CE, Cassius Dio, Collected Works 60.10
    1. to come forth
      • 409 BCE, Sophocles, Philoctetes 98
      • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Fragments 105
      • 480 BCE – 406 BCE, Euripides, Fragments 334
    2. (absolute) to march out with army
      • 423 BCE, Aristophanes, The Clouds 633
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 5.13
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cyropaedia 3.3.20
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 1.95
      • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Women of Trachis 159
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Olympiodorus 55
      • 438 BCE, Euripides, Alcestis 610
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Hellenica 4.2.13
      • 250 CE – 350 CE, Athenaeus, Deipnosophistae 8.351D
    3. to come forward on the stage
      • 405 BCE, Aristophanes, The Frogs 946
  2. (of time or incidents) to come to an end, expire
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.139
    • 409 BCE, Sophocles, Philoctetes 767
    • 445 BCE – 380 BCE, Lysias, For the Soldier 6
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cynegeticus 8.3

Etymology 2

[edit]

ἐκ- (ek-, “out”) +‎ εἰμί (eimí, “to be”)

Verb

[edit]

ἔξειμῐ • (éxeimi)

  1. (very rare) to remain after(?)
