Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἀσθενής (asthenḗs, “weak, small”) +‎ -έω (-éō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /as.tʰe.né.ɔː/ → /as.θeˈne.o/ → /as.θeˈne.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /as.tʰe.né.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /as.tʰeˈne.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /as.θeˈne.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /as.θeˈne.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /as.θeˈne.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἀσθενέω • (asthenéō)

  1. to be weak, feeble, sickly
    • 408 BCE, Euripides, Orestes 228
    • 400 BCE – 387 BCE, Plato, Lysis 209E
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Charmides 155B
    • 428 BCE, Euripides, Hippolytus 274
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 7.47
    • 70 CE – 110 CE, The Gospel of Matthew 10:8
    • 349 BCE, Demosthenes, First Olynthiac 13
    • 460 BCE – 370 BCE, Hippocrates, On Ancient Medicine 12
    • 200 BCE – 118 BCE, Polybius, The Histories 31.13.7
  2. to be needy
    • 421 BCE, Aristophanes, Peace 636
  3. (with infinitive) to be too weak to do a thing, to be unable
    • 75 CE, Josephus, The Jewish War 2.15.5
    • 204 CE – 270 CE, Plotinus, Collected Works 3.8.4
