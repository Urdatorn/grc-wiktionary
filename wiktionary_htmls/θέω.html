Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • θείω (theíō) — Epic

Etymology

[edit]

From earlier θεϝ- (thew-), from Proto-Indo-European *dʰew- (“to run, flow”). Cognate to Sanskrit धावति (dhā́vati, “to run, to fly”).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /tʰé.ɔː/ → /ˈθe.o/ → /ˈθe.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /tʰé.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈtʰe.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈθe.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈθe.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈθe.o/

Verb

[edit]

θέω • (théō)

  1. to run (fast)
  2. to fly

Usage notes

[edit]
  • In Attic, the combinations εο, εου, εω are not contracted.
  • The remaining tenses are usually suppleted by τρέχω (trékhō) and *δρέμω (drémō).
