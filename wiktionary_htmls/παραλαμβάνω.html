Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • παλλαμβάνω (pallambánō) — Cretan

Etymology

[edit]

From πᾰρᾰ- (para-) +‎ λαμβᾰ́νω (lambánō, “to take, receive”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pa.rá.lam.bá.nɔː/ → /paˈra.lamˈba.no/ → /paˈra.lamˈba.no/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pa.rá.lam.bá.nɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /paˈra.lamˈba.no/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /paˈra.lamˈba.no/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /paˈra.lamˈba.no/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /paˈra.lamˈba.no/

Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • παραλαβαίνω (paralavaíno) (colloquial, rare)

Etymology

[edit]

Borrowed from Ancient Greek παραλαμβάνω (paralambánō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): /pa.ɾa.laɱˈva.no/
  • Hyphenation: πα‧ρα‧λαμ‧βά‧νω

Verb

[edit]

παραλαμβάνω • (paralamváno) (past παρέλαβα, passive παραλαμβάνομαι, p‑past παραλήφθηκα)

  1. to receive, take delivery of
  2. to collect
