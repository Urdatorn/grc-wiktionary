Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Hellenic *oyépʰō, from Proto-Indo-European *h₃yebʰ-. Cognates include Sanskrit यभति (yábhati) and Proto-Slavic *jebati.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ǒi̯.pʰɔː/ → /ˈy.ɸo/ → /ˈi.fo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ǒi̯.pʰɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈy.pʰo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈy.ɸo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈy.fo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈi.fo/

Verb

[edit]

οἴφω • (oíphō)

  1. (vulgar, slang, transitive) to have sexual intercourse with, to fuck
  2. (vulgar, slang, intransitive) to have sexual intercourse, to fuck
