Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

δια- (dia-) +‎ ἕπω (hépō)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /di.é.pɔː/ → /ðiˈe.po/ → /ðiˈe.po/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /di.é.pɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /diˈe.po/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ðiˈe.po/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ðiˈe.po/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ðiˈe.po/

Verb

[edit]

διέπω • (diépō)

  1. to manage, administer, arrange
  2. (middle voice) to be engaged in

Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Ancient Greek διέπω.

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): /ðiˈe.po/
  • Hyphenation: δι‧έ‧πω

Verb

[edit]

διέπω • (diépo) (past —, passive διέπομαι) found only in the present tense (usually in 3rd person)

  1. to apply, determine, govern
