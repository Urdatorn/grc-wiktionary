Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From τραγῳδός (tragōidós, “tragic singer”), τραγῳδῐ́α (tragōidía, “tragedy”)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /tra.ɡɔːi̯.dɔ̂ː/ → /tra.ɣoˈðo/ → /tra.ɣoˈðo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /tra.ɡɔːi̯.dɔ̂ː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /tra.ɡoˈdo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /tra.ɣoˈðo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /tra.ɣoˈðo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /tra.ɣoˈðo/

Verb

[edit]

τρᾰγῳδέω • (tragōidéō)

  1. to act a tragedy, represent in tragedy
  2. to tell in a tragic style, declaim
