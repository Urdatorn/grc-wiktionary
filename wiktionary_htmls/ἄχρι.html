Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ἄχρῐς (ákhris), ἄχρεις (ákhreis), ἄχροι (ákhroi)

Etymology

[edit]

Zero grade of μέχρῐ (mékhri), from Proto-Indo-European *méǵʰ(s)ri.

The genitive is from the PIE ablative of comparison/extent.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /á.kʰri/ → /ˈa.xri/ → /ˈa.xri/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /á.kʰri/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈa.kʰri/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈa.xri/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈa.xri/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈa.xri/

Adverb

[edit]

ἄχρῐ • (ákhri)

  1. to the very bottom, to the uttermost, utterly
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 4.522
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 16.324
    1. (after Homer, before prepositions)
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 5.4.4
      • 125 CE – 200 CE, Lucian, Nigrinus 36
      • 125 CE – 200 CE, Lucian, Hermotimus 24
      • 300 BCE – 250 BCE, Apollonius of Rhodes, Argonautica 4.1403
      • 300 CE – 400 CE, Quintus Smyrnaeus, Posthomerica 6.177
      • 125 CE – 200 CE, Lucian, Dialogues of the Dead 27.4
      • 125 CE – 200 CE, Lucian, Dialogues of the Dead 2.617
      • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Mark Antony 34

Preposition

[edit]

ἄχρῐ • (ákhri) (governs the genitive)

  1. even to, as far as
    1. (of time) until
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 18.370
      • 341 BCE, Demosthenes, Third Philippic 28
      • 330 BCE, Demosthenes, On the Crown 179
      • Apollodorus, The Disappearing Man 1
      • 638 BCE – 558 BCE, Solon, Collected Works 12.35
      • 200 BCE – 100 BCE, Timostratus, Asotos 1
      • 125 CE – 200 CE, Lucian, Timon 39
      • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Cicero 6
    2. (of space) as far as, even to
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.138
      • 420 BCE – 380 BCE, Timaeus of Locri, Collected Works 101A
      • 60 BCE – 7 BCE, Dionysius of Halicarnassus, Roman Antiquities 2.43
    3. (of measure or degree) up to this point
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Aristocrates 122
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Symposium 4.37
      • 341 BCE, Demosthenes, On the Chersonese 77

Conjunction

[edit]

ἄχρῐ • (ákhri)

  1. (of time) until, so long as

    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 2.3.2
    • 460 BCE – 370 BCE, Hippocrates, Collected Works 884F
    • 460 BCE – 370 BCE, Hippocrates, Collected Works 553

    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cyropaedia 5.4.16
    • 125 CE – 200 CE, Lucian, How to Write History 9
