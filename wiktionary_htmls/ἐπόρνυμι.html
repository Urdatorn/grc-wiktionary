Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ἐπορνύω (epornúō)

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐπι- (epi-) +‎ ὄρνυμι (órnumi).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.pór.nyː.mi/ → /eˈpor.ny.mi/ → /eˈpor.ni.mi/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.pór.nyː.mi/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /eˈpor.ny.mi/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /eˈpor.ny.mi/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /eˈpor.ny.mi/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /eˈpor.ni.mi/

Verb

[edit]

ἐπόρνῡμῐ • (epórnūmi)

  1. to stir up, arouse, excite
  2. to rouse and send against
  3. (passive voice) to rise against, assault, fly upon
