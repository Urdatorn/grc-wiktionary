Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *tḱey- (“to settle”). Cognates include Sanskrit क्षेति (kṣeti), Avestan 𐬱𐬀𐬉𐬌𐬙𐬌 (šaēiti) and, via Iranian borrowing, Old Armenian շէն (šēn).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ktíz.dɔː/ → /ˈkti.zo/ → /ˈkti.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ktíz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈkti.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈkti.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈkti.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈkti.zo/

Verb

[edit]

κτίζω • (ktízō)

  1. to people
  2. to found, build, establish (a city)
  3. to plant (e.g. an orchard)
  4. to create, produce
  5. to make so
  6. to perpetrate
