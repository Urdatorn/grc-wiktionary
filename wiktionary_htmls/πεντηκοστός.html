Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Hellenic *penkʷēkontstós, equivalent to πεντήκοντα (pentḗkonta) +‎ -τός (-tós).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pen.tɛː.kos.tós/ → /pen.ti.kosˈtos/ → /pen.di.kosˈtos/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pen.tɛː.kos.tós/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /pen.te̝.kosˈtos/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /pen.ti.kosˈtos/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /pen.ti.kosˈtos/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /pen.di.kosˈtos/

Adjective

[edit]

πεντηκοστός • (pentēkostós) m (feminine πεντηκοστή, neuter πεντηκοστόν); first/second declension

  1. fiftieth
