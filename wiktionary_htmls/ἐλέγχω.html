Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἔλεγχος (élenkhos, “argument of disproof, refutation”), from Proto-Indo-European *h₁lengʰ- (“to accuse, to scold”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.léŋ.kʰɔː/ → /eˈleŋ.xo/ → /eˈleŋ.xo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.léŋ.kʰɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /eˈleŋ.kʰo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /eˈleŋ.xo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /eˈleŋ.xo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /eˈleŋ.xo/

Verb

[edit]

ἐλέγχω • (elénkhō) (Epic, Attic, Doric, Koine)

  1. (transitive) to disgrace, put to shame
  2. (transitive) to cross-examine, question
  3. (transitive) to test, bring to the proof
