Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • νείφω (neíphō)

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *sneygʷʰ-. Cognates include Latin ningit, Sanskrit स्नेहति (snehati), Old English snīwan (English snew).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ní.pʰɔː/ → /ˈni.ɸo/ → /ˈni.fo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ní.pʰɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈni.pʰo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈni.ɸo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈni.fo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈni.fo/

Verb

[edit]

νῐ́φω • (níphō)

  1. to snow
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 12.280
    1. (impersonal) it snows
      • 446 BCE – 386 BCE, Aristophanes, The Acharnians II41
    2. (passive voice) to be snowed upon
      • 484 BCE – 425 BCE, Herodotus, The Histories 4.31
  2. to rain
    • 350 CE – 450 CE, Nonnus, Dionysiaca 22.283
