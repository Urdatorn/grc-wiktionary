Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *kʷey- (“to perceive, revere, value, honor”). Compare τῑμᾰ́ω (tīmáō).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /tǐː.ɔː/ → /ˈti.o/ → /ˈti.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /tǐː.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈti.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈti.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈti.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈti.o/

Verb

[edit]

τῑ́ω • (tī́ō)

  1. to pay honour to a person (whereas τίνω (tínō) means to pay a price)
