Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • δῐᾰζώω (diazṓō) — Epic, Ionic

Etymology

[edit]

δια- (dia-, “across, through”) +‎ ζάω (záō, “to live”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /di.az.dá.ɔː/ → /ði.aˈza.o/ → /ði.aˈza.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /di.az.dá.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /di.aˈza.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ði.aˈza.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ði.aˈza.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ði.aˈza.o/

Verb

[edit]

διαζάω • (diazáō)

  1. to live through (time or circumstance), to live long, to live to an old age or to senectitude, to live to one's full lifespan, to outlive, to overlive, to survive
