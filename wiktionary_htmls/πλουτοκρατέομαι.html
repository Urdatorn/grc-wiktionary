Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From πλοῦτος (ploûtos, “wealth”) + κρατέω (kratéō, “I rule”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pluː.to.kra.té.o.mai̯/ → /plu.to.kraˈte.o.mɛ/ → /plu.to.kraˈte.o.me/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pluː.to.kra.té.o.mai̯/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /plu.to.kraˈte.o.mɛ/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /plu.to.kraˈte.o.mɛ/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /plu.to.kraˈte.o.me/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /plu.to.kraˈte.o.me/

Verb

[edit]

πλουτοκρᾰτέομαι • (ploutokratéomai)

  1. to be governed by the wealthy
    • 342 BCE – 290 BCE, Menander, Rh. 360S
