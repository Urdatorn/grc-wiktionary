Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • πυρή (purḗ) — Epic, Ionic

Etymology 1

[edit]

Derived from πῦρ (pûr, “fire”).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /py.rǎː/ → /pyˈra/ → /piˈra/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /py.rǎː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /pyˈra/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /pyˈra/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /pyˈra/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /piˈra/

Noun

[edit]

πῠρᾱ́ • (purā́) f (genitive πῠρᾶς); first declension

  1. funeral pyre

Etymology 2

[edit]

Derived from πῦρ (pûr, “fire”). Originally the plural.[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /py.rá/ → /pyˈra/ → /piˈra/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /py.rá/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /pyˈra/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /pyˈra/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /pyˈra/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /piˈra/

Noun

[edit]

πυρά • (purá) n (genitive πυρῶν); second declension

  1. (attested as plural only) watchfires

Greek

[edit]

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): /piˈɾa/
  • Hyphenation: πυ‧ρά

Etymology 1

[edit]

Learned borrowing from Ancient Greek πυρά (purá).

Noun

[edit]

πυρά • (pyrá) f (plural πυρές) (formal)

  1. the stake (when used in English with the definite article - a place of execution)
    θάνατος επί της πυράς ― thánatos epí tis pyrás ― death at the stake
  2. pyre
    η πυρά του βωμού ― i pyrá tou vomoú ― the pyre of the altar

Etymology 2

[edit]

Plural of πυρ (pyr)

Noun

[edit]

πυρά • (pyrá) n

  1. (in the plural: military) firing of arms, Nominative, accusative and vocative plural form of πυρ (pyr).
    τα πυρά των όπλων ― ta pyrá ton óplon ― the firing of arms
