Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From παρα- (para-, “beside, by”) +‎ τείνω (teínō, “to stretch out”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pa.ra.těː.nɔː/ → /pa.raˈti.no/ → /pa.raˈti.no/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pa.ra.těː.nɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /pa.raˈti.no/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /pa.raˈti.no/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /pa.raˈti.no/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /pa.raˈti.no/

Verb

[edit]

πᾰρᾰτείνω • (parateínō)

  1. to stretch out along, beside, or near; to extend, deploy; to extend along; to be stretched at length, laid low
    1. to stretch on the rack, torture; to be tortured; to be worn out
    2. to prolong, protract
      1. to delay
    3. to apply a figure to a straight line
    4. (of the bowels) to relax
    5. to lengthen in pronunciation; (of sound, of echo) to prolong
    6. to stretch or lie beside or along
      1. to extend; to extend over
      2. (of time) to extend; to continue one's life
