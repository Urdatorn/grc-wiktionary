Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

ἀπο- (apo-, “away, back”) +‎ πέμπω (pémpō, “send”)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /a.po.pém.pɔː/ → /a.poˈpem.po/ → /a.poˈpem.bo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /a.po.pém.pɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /a.poˈpem.po/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /a.poˈpem.po/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /a.poˈpem.po/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /a.poˈpem.bo/

Verb

[edit]

ᾰ̓ποπέμπω • (apopémpō)

  1. to send off or away, dismiss, dispatch
  2. (middle voice) to get rid of
  3. to send back
  4. to export
  5. (middle voice) to avert by sacrifice; to banish, exorcise (a disease)
  6. to divorce
