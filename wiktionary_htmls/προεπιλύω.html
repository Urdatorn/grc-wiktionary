Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From προ- (pro-, “before”) +‎ ἐπιλῡ́ω (epilū́ō, “loose, untie”)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pro.e.pi.ly̌ː.ɔː/ → /pro.e.piˈly.o/ → /pro.e.piˈli.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pro.e.pi.ly̌ː.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /pro.e.piˈly.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /pro.e.piˈly.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /pro.e.piˈly.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /pro.e.piˈli.o/

Verb

[edit]

προεπῐλῡ́ω • (proepilū́ō)

  1. to solve previously
