Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἀπο- (apo-, “out, away from”) +‎ πτῡ́ω (ptū́ō, “spit out”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /a.pop.ty̌ː.ɔː/ → /a.popˈty.o/ → /a.popˈti.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /a.pop.ty̌ː.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /a.popˈty.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /a.popˈty.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /a.popˈty.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /a.popˈti.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἀποπτύω • (apoptúō)

  1. to spit out, spit
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 23.781
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 4.426
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Memorabilia 1.2.54
    • 525 BCE – 455 BCE, Aeschylus, Fragments 376
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cyropaedia 1.2.16
    • Philo 1.29
    1. to abominate, spurn, disown
      • 750 BCE – 650 BCE, Hesiod, Works and Days 724
      • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, The Eumenides 303
      • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, Agamemnon 1192
      • 525 BCE – 455 BCE, Aeschylus, Prometheus Bound 1070
      • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, The Libation Bearers 195
