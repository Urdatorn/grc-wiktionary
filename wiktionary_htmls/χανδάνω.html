Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

For *χαδάνω (with -χανδ- imported from other forms of the paradigm),[1] from a nasal-infixed and -suffixed present from Proto-Indo-European *gʰed- (“to seize, grasp, take”). Compare λαμβάνω (lambánō). Cognate with Latin prehendō, English get, Albanian gjej.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /kʰan.dá.nɔː/ → /xanˈda.no/ → /xanˈda.no/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /kʰan.dá.nɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /kʰanˈda.no/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /xanˈda.no/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /xanˈda.no/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /xanˈda.no/

Verb

[edit]

χᾰνδᾰ́νω • (khandánō) (chiefly Epic)

  1. (transitive) to take in, hold, comprise, contain
  2. (figuratively, intransitive) to be capable [with infinitive ‘of doing’]
