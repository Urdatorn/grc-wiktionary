Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Unknown. If related to Σίσυφος (Sísuphos) and σαφής (saphḗs, “speaking clearly”), then likely from a Pre-Greek substrate.[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /so.pʰós/ → /soˈɸos/ → /soˈfos/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /so.pʰós/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /soˈpʰos/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /soˈɸos/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /soˈfos/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /soˈfos/

Adjective

[edit]

σοφός • (sophós) m (feminine σοφή, neuter σοφόν); first/second declension

  1. clever, skillful, cunning, able
  2. intelligent, wise, prudent
