Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From μῖσος (mîsos, “hatred”) +‎ -έω (-éō, denominative verbal suffix).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /miː.sé.ɔː/ → /miˈse.o/ → /miˈse.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /miː.sé.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /miˈse.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /miˈse.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /miˈse.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /miˈse.o/

Verb

[edit]

μῑσέω • (mīséō)

  1. to hate
    • 525 BCE – 455 BCE, Aeschylus, Prometheus Bound 45
    • 414 BCE, Aristophanes, The Birds 36
    • 446 BCE – 411 BCE, Eupolis, Collected Works 351.1
    • c. 440 BCE, Euripides, Rhesus 333
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.119
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 17.272
    • 342 BCE – 290 BCE, Menander, Men at Arbitration 216
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Pythian Ode 4.284
    • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Crassus 6
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Septuagint, Tobit 4.15
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Ajax 818
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Ajax 1134
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 8.83
