Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

ἀντι- (anti-) +‎ ποιέω (poiéō)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /an.ti.poi̯.é.ɔː/ → /an.ti.pyˈe.o/ → /an.di.piˈe.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /an.ti.poi̯.é.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /an.ti.pyˈe.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /an.ti.pyˈe.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /an.ti.pyˈe.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /an.di.piˈe.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἀντῐποιέω • (antipoiéō)

  1. to do in return, retaliate
  2. (middle voice) to exert oneself about, seek after, lay claim to
  3. (middle voice) to contend with
