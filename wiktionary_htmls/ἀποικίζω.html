Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

ἀπο- (apo-) +‎ οἰκίζω (oikízō)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /a.poi̯.kíz.dɔː/ → /a.pyˈci.zo/ → /a.piˈci.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /a.poi̯.kíz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /a.pyˈki.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /a.pyˈci.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /a.pyˈci.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /a.piˈci.zo/

Verb

[edit]

ἀποικίζω • (apoikízō)

  1. to send away from home
  2. to colonize (begin a new colony)
  3. (in passive) to be settled in a far land, to emigrate
