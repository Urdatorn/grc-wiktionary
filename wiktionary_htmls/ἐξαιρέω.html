Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

ἐξ- (ex-) +‎ αἱρέω (hairéō)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ek.sai̯.ré.ɔː/ → /ek.sɛˈre.o/ → /ek.seˈre.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ek.sai̯.ré.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ek.sɛˈre.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ek.sɛˈre.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ek.seˈre.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ek.seˈre.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἐξαιρέω • (exairéō)

  1. to take out
  2. to pick out, choose, select
  3. to pluck out, draw out, root out
  4. to except
  5. to expel
  6. to remove
  7. to take away
  8. to set free, deliver, rescue
  9. to annul, demolish
  10. to bring to an end, accomplish

Usage notes

[edit]

Unrelated to ἐλεέω (eleéō) despite the related meaning and superficial similarity in the aorist forms.
