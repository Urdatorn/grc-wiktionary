Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

ναύτης (naútēs, “sailor”) +‎ -ικός (-ikós, adjectival suffix)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /nau̯.ti.kós/ → /naɸ.tiˈkos/ → /naf.tiˈkos/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /nau̯.ti.kós/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /naʍ.tiˈkos/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /naɸ.tiˈkos/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /naf.tiˈkos/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /naf.tiˈkos/

Adjective

[edit]

ναυτῐκός • (nautikós) m (feminine ναυτῐκή, neuter ναυτῐκόν); first/second declension

  1. seafaring, naval, on the sea
  2. skilled in seamanship
