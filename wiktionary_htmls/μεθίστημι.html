Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

μετα- (meta-) +‎ ἵστημι (hístēmi)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /me.tʰís.tɛː.mi/ → /meˈθis.ti.mi/ → /meˈθis.ti.mi/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /me.tʰís.tɛː.mi/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /meˈtʰis.te̝.mi/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /meˈθis.ti.mi/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /meˈθis.ti.mi/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /meˈθis.ti.mi/

Verb

[edit]

μεθίστημῐ • (methístēmi)

  1. (active voice) to alter, change (transitive)
  2. (passive voice) to alter, change (intransitive)
  3. (active voice) to replace, exchange
  4. (middle voice) to come and stand among
  5. (active voice) to transpose, transfer, move (from one place to another)
  6. (active voice or middle voice, euphemistic) to die
