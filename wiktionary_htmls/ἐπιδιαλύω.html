Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐπι- (epi-, “on, upon”) +‎ διαλύω (dialúō, “dissolve”)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.pi.di.a.ly̌ː.ɔː/ → /e.pi.ði.aˈly.o/ → /e.pi.ði.aˈli.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.pi.di.a.ly̌ː.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /e.pi.di.aˈly.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /e.pi.ði.aˈly.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /e.pi.ði.aˈly.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /e.pi.ði.aˈli.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἐπῐδῐᾰλῡ́ω • (epidialū́ō)

  1. to come as a relief to
