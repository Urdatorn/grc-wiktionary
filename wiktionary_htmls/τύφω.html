Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From a *bʰ-extension of Proto-Indo-European *dʰewH- (“mist, smoke, haze”).[1] Compare also τῦφος (tûphos, “fever”), τυφόω (tuphóō, “delude”), Τυφῶν (Tuphôn, “Typhon”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ty̌ː.pʰɔː/ → /ˈty.ɸo/ → /ˈti.fo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ty̌ː.pʰɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈty.pʰo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈty.ɸo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈty.fo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈti.fo/

Verb

[edit]

τῡ́φω • (tū́phō)

  1. to smoke, fill with smoke
  2. to consume in smoke
