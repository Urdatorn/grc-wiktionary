Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐπι- (epi-) +‎ βουλεύω (bouleúō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.pi.buː.lěu̯.ɔː/ → /e.pi.βuˈle.βo/ → /e.pi.vuˈle.vo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.pi.buː.lěu̯.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /e.pi.buˈle.wo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /e.pi.βuˈle.βo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /e.pi.vuˈle.vo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /e.pi.vuˈle.vo/

Verb

[edit]

ἐπῐβουλεύω • (epibouleúō)

  1. to plot against; to contrive a plan against [with dative]
  2. to plan upon something; to plan to do something [with accusative or infinitive]
  3. (intransitive) to be harmful
  4. (passive voice or future middle voice) to be plotted against
