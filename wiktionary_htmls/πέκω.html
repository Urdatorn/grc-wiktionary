Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • πείκω (peíkō) — Epic

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *peḱ- (“to pluck”). Cognates include Latin pectō (“to comb, card”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pé.kɔː/ → /ˈpe.ko/ → /ˈpe.ko/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pé.kɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈpe.ko/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈpe.ko/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈpe.ko/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈpe.ko/

Verb

[edit]

πέκω • (pékō)

  1. to comb, card
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 14.176
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 18.316
    • Palatine Anthology 6.279
  2. to shear
    • 423 BCE, Aristophanes, The Clouds 1356
    • 175 CE – 235 CE, Claudius Aelianus, On the Nature of Animals 1.38
    • 1115 – 1195, Eustathius of Thessalonica, Collected Works 531.5
    • 750 BCE – 650 BCE, Hesiod, Works and Days 775
    • 556 BCE – 468 BCE, Simonides, Fragmenta 13
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Theocritus, Collected Works 5.98
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Theocritus, Collected Works 28.13
