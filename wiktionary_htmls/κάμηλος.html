Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Ultimately from Proto-Semitic *gamal-; compare Arabic جَمَل (jamal) and Hebrew גָּמָל (gāmā́l),[1] Proto-Nakh *imkal, Old Georgian აქლემი (aklemi) and Tocharian A oṅkaläm (“elephant”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ká.mɛː.los/ → /ˈka.mi.los/ → /ˈka.mi.los/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ká.mɛː.los/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈka.me̝.los/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈka.mi.los/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈka.mi.los/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈka.mi.los/

Noun

[edit]

κᾰ́μηλος • (kámēlos) m or f (genitive κᾰμήλου); second declension

  1. camel
