Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ἀποχρέω (apokhréō) — Doric

Etymology

[edit]

From ἀπο- (apo-) +‎ χράω (khráō, “to declare”)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /a.po.kʰrá.ɔː/ → /a.poˈxra.o/ → /a.poˈxra.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /a.po.kʰrá.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /a.poˈkʰra.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /a.poˈxra.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /a.poˈxra.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /a.poˈxra.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἀποχράω • (apokhráō)

  1. (intransitive) to suffice, be sufficient, be enough
  2. (passive voice) to be contented [with dative ‘with’]
  3. (middle voice) to use to the full [with dative]
  4. (middle voice) to abuse, misuse [with dative]
  5. (middle voice, transitive) to use up, destroy
