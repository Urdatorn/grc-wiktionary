Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ὑπο- (hupo-) +‎ ἐκφεύγω (ekpheúgō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /hy.pekʰ.pʰěu̯.ɡɔː/ → /y.pekˈɸeβ.ɣo/ → /i.pekˈfev.ɣo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /hy.pekʰ.pʰěu̯.ɡɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /(h)y.pekˈpʰew.ɡo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /y.pekˈɸeβ.ɣo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /y.pekˈfev.ɣo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /i.pekˈfev.ɣo/

Verb

[edit]

ὑπεκφεύγω • (hupekpheúgō)

  1. (intransitive) to flee away or escape secretly
  2. (transitive) to escape from
