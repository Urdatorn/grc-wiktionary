Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἀντῐ- (anti-, “against”) +‎ ῐ̔́στημῐ (hístēmi, “to stand”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /an.tʰís.tɛː.mi/ → /anˈθis.ti.mi/ → /anˈθis.ti.mi/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /an.tʰís.tɛː.mi/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /anˈtʰis.te̝.mi/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /anˈθis.ti.mi/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /anˈθis.ti.mi/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /anˈθis.ti.mi/

Verb

[edit]

ᾰ̓νθῐ́στημῐ • (anthístēmi)

  1. to stand against
  2. to oppose
