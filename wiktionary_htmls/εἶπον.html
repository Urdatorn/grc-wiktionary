Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ἔειπον (éeipon) — Epic, Lyric
  • εἶπα (eîpa)
  • ἔειπα (éeipa) — Poetic

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Hellenic *eweikʷon, dissimilated from *eweukʷon, from Proto-Indo-European *h₁éwewkʷom, from *wekʷ- (“to speak, make a sound”), from which also came ὄψ (óps, “voice, word”).[1] Cognate with Sanskrit अवोचम् (á-vocam) and Latin vōx (“voice”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /êː.pon/ → /ˈi.pon/ → /ˈi.pon/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /êː.pon/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈi.pon/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈi.pon/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈi.pon/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈi.pon/

Verb

[edit]

εἶπον • (eîpon)

  1. to say, speak

Usage notes

[edit]

εἶπον generally supplies aorist active forms to the verbs εἴρω (eírō) and λέγω (légō, “say”).
