Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Unknown. Possibly connected to πάλλω (pállō, “to swing”), though this verb itself has no certain etymology. One would have to posit a Proto-Hellenic root *pal- with the suffix -άσσω, also found in σταλάσσω (stalássō), αἱμάσσω (haimássō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pa.lás.sɔː/ → /paˈlas.so/ → /paˈla.so/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pa.lás.sɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /paˈlas.so/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /paˈlas.so/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /paˈlas.so/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /paˈla.so/

Verb

[edit]

πᾰλάσσω • (palássō) (Epic)

  1. to besprinkle, defile, bespatter
  2. (passive voice) to be scattered abroad
