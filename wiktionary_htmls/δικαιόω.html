Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From δίκαιος (díkaios, “decent, righteous”) +‎ -όω (-óō, “cause someone to be”, factitive verb suffix).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /di.kai̯.ó.ɔː/ → /ði.cɛˈo.o/ → /ði.ceˈo.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /di.kai̯.ó.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /di.kɛˈo.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ði.cɛˈo.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ði.ceˈo.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ði.ceˈo.o/

Verb

[edit]

δῐκαιόω • (dikaióō)

  1. to set right
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Fragments 169.13
  2. to hold or deem right, to think fit, to claim or demand as right
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.89
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.133
    • 460 BCE – 370 BCE, Hippocrates, On Fractures 31
    • 429 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus the King 640
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 2.41
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 9.42
    • 409 BCE, Sophocles, Philoctetes 781
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 5.26
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 2.71
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.172
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 3.118
    • 60 BCE – 7 BCE, Dionysius of Halicarnassus, Roman Antiquities 10.1
  3. to do a man right or justice, to judge
    1. to condemn, chastise, punish
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.100
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 3.29
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 934B
      • 155 CE – 235 CE, Cassius Dio, Roman History 16.57.47
      • 155 CE – 235 CE, Cassius Dio, Fragments 57.47
      • 155 CE – 235 CE, Cassius Dio, Roman History 58.5.6
      • 155 CE – 235 CE, Cassius Dio, Roman History 58.11.5
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 3.40
    2. to make just, pronounce and treat as righteous, justify, vindicate
      • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Septuagint, Exodus 23.7
      • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Septuagint, Jeremiah 3.11
      • 50 CE – 100 CE, The Gospel of Luke 16:15
      • 50 CE – 100 CE, The Gospel of Luke 7:35
