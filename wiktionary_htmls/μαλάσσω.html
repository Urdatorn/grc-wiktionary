Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • μαλάττω (maláttō) — Attic

Etymology

[edit]

From μαλακός (malakós) +‎ -σσω (-ssō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ma.lás.sɔː/ → /maˈlas.so/ → /maˈla.so/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ma.lás.sɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /maˈlas.so/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /maˈlas.so/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /maˈlas.so/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /maˈla.so/

Verb

[edit]

μᾰλάσσω • (malássō)

  1. to soften (especially a material like leather or metal)
  2. (figurative) to give (someone) a dressing down, to tan someone's hide
  3. (figurative) to soften, appease, cause to relent
