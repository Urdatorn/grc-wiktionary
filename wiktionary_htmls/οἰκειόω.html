Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • οἰκηϊόω (oikēïóō) — Ionic

Etymology

[edit]

From οἰκεῖος (oikeîos) +‎ -όω (-óō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /oi̯.keː.ó.ɔː/ → /y.ciˈo.o/ → /i.ciˈo.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /oi̯.keː.ó.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /y.kiˈo.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /y.ciˈo.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /y.ciˈo.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /i.ciˈo.o/

Verb

[edit]

οἰκειόω • (oikeióō)

  1. to make one's own, claim as one's own, appropriate
  2. to make someone one's friend
  3. (middle voice) to win someone's favor or affection, conciliate
  4. (passive voice) to be made friendly, be closely united
  5. (Byzantine) to entrust
