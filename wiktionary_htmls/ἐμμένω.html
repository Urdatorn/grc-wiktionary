Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ἐμμίμνω (emmímnō) — Poetic

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐν (en, “in”) + μένω (ménō, “to remain”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /em.mé.nɔː/ → /emˈme.no/ → /eˈme.no/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /em.mé.nɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /emˈme.no/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /emˈme.no/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /emˈme.no/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /eˈme.no/

Verb

[edit]

ἐμμένω • (emménō)

  1. to abide in a place
    • 480 BCE – 406 BCE, Euripides, Fragments 364.12
    • 391 BCE, Aristophanes, Assemblywomen 1120
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 2.23
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 8.31
    1. to abide by, stand by, cleave to, be true to
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 9.106
      • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, The Libation Bearers 977
      • 429 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus the King 351
      • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Ajax 350
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 5.18
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 4.4.16
      • 400 BCE – 387 BCE, Plato, Apology 39B
      • 366 BCE – 348 BCE, Plato, Theaetetus 145C
      • 95 CE – 165 CE, Appian, On Iberia 24
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 4.118
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 844C
      • 408 BCE, Euripides, The Phoenician Women 1241
    2. (of things) to remain fixed, stand fast, hold good
      • 406 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus at Colonus 648
      • 525 BCE – 455 BCE, Aeschylus, Prometheus Bound 534
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 7.151
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 2.2
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 839C
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Phaedrus 258C
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 1.5
