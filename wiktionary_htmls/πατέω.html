Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • βᾰτέω (batéō) — Doric
  • μάτημῐ (mátēmi) — Aeolic

Etymology

[edit]

Related to πάτος (pátos, “path”); see there for more.[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pa.té.ɔː/ → /paˈte.o/ → /paˈte.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pa.té.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /paˈte.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /paˈte.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /paˈte.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /paˈte.o/

Verb

[edit]

πᾰτέω • (patéō)

  1. to walk, to tread
    • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, Agamemnon 1298
    • 50 CE – 100 CE, The Gospel of Luke 10:19
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Olympian Ode 1.115
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Pythian Ode 2.85
    • 234 CE – 305 CE, Porphyry, On Abstinence 1.14
  2. to tread on
    • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, The Libation Bearers 732
    • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, Agamemnon 957
    • 320 CE – 403 CE, Oribasius, Collected Works 5.12.1
    • 630 BCE – 570 BCE, Sappho, Collected Works 54
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Septuagint, Nehemiah 13.15
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Septuagint, Apoc. 19.15
    • 406 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus at Colonus 37
  3. to walk in, dwell in, frequent
    • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, Agamemnon 1193
    • 50 CE – 250 CE, Apollonius Dyscolus, On Pronouns 45.6
    • 414 BCE, Aristophanes, The Birds 471
    • 310 BCE – 240 BCE, Callimachus, Aetia 2079.25
    • 310 BCE – 240 BCE, Callimachus, Del. 248
    • 260 CE – 340 CE, Eusebius, Preparations for the Gospel 10.3
    • Oppian, Cynegetica 2.218
    • 280 BCE – 220 BCE, Philo of Byzantium, Compendium of Mechanics 2.345
    • 110 BCE – 35 BCE, Philodemus, Po.Herc. 1676.10
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Phaedrus 273a
    • 409 BCE, Sophocles, Philoctetes 1060
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Theocritus, Collected Works 18.20
  4. to trample
    • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, Agamemnon 1357
    • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, The Libation Bearers 644
    • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, The Eumenides 110
    • 424 BCE, Aristophanes, The Knights 166
    • 422 BCE, Aristophanes, The Wasps 377
    • 129 CE – 216 CE, Galen, 16 562
    • New Testament, Revelation 11:2
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Phaedrus 248a
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Ajax 1146
    • 442 BCE, Sophocles, Antigone 745
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Fragments 683
