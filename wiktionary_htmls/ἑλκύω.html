Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

The present stem is a late back-formation from εἵλκυσα (heílkusa), aorist tense of ἕλκω (hélkō), and εἵλκυκα (heílkuka), perfect tense of the same.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /hel.ký.ɔː/ → /elˈcy.o/ → /elˈci.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /hel.ký.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /(h)elˈky.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /elˈcy.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /elˈcy.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /elˈci.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἑλκύω • (helkúō)

  1. Synonym of ἕλκω (hélkō)
