Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Hellenic *tréhō, from Proto-Indo-European *tres-. Cognates include Latin terreō, Old Irish tarrach, and Sanskrit त्रसति (trasati). Compare τρέμω (trémō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /tré.ɔː/ → /ˈtre.o/ → /ˈtre.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /tré.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈtre.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈtre.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈtre.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈtre.o/

Verb

[edit]

τρέω • (tréō)

  1. to flee from fear
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 5.256
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 21.288
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 11.546
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 17.332
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 11.745
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 6.138
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 22.143, (Compare 13.515, 17.332)
    • 750 BCE – 650 BCE, Hesiod, The Shield of Heracles 171
    • 470 BCE, Aeschylus, The Suppliants 711
    • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, Agamemnon 549
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Phaedo 117B
    1. (aorist participle, substantive) runaway, coward (compare τρεσᾶς (tresâs), τρεσᾶ (tresâ))
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 14.522
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 7.231, (Compare Tyrtaeus, fragments 11.14)
      • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Agesilaus 30, (Compare Lycurgus 21)
  2. to fear, dread, be afraid of
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 11.554
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 17.663
    • 467 BCE, Aeschylus, Seven Against Thebes 397
    • 467 BCE, Aeschylus, Seven Against Thebes 436
    • 442 BCE, Sophocles, Antigone 1042
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 1.9.6
    • 750 BCE – 650 BCE, Hesiod, Theogony 850
    • 467 BCE, Aeschylus, Seven Against Thebes 790
