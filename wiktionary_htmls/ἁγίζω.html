Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἅγιος (hágios, “holy”) +‎ -ίζω (-ízō, denominative verb suffix).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ha.ɡíz.dɔː/ → /aˈʝi.zo/ → /aˈʝi.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ha.ɡíz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /(h)aˈɡi.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /aˈʝi.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /aˈʝi.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /aˈʝi.zo/

Verb

[edit]

ἁγίζω • (hagízō) (Attic, Doric)

  1. (transitive) to hallow, make sacred, especially by burning a sacrifice
    Πάτερ ἡμῶν ὁ ἐν τοῖς οὐρανοῖς ἁγιασθήτω τὸ ὄνομά σου·
    Páter hēmôn ho en toîs ouranoîs hagiasthḗtō tò ónomá sou;
    Our Father, Who art in heaven, Hallowed be Thy Name.
    • 406 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus at Colonus 1495
    • 388 BCE, Aristophanes, Plutus 681
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Olympian Ode 3.19
    • 60 BCE – 7 BCE, Dionysius of Halicarnassus, Roman Antiquities 1.38
