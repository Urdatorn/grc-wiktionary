Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From σπάδων (spádōn, “eunuch”) +‎ -ίζω (-ízō, “a suffix forming a verb from a noun”)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /spa.do.níz.dɔː/ → /spa.ðoˈni.zo/ → /spa.ðoˈni.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /spa.do.níz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /spa.doˈni.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /spa.ðoˈni.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /spa.ðoˈni.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /spa.ðoˈni.zo/

Verb

[edit]

σπαδονίζω • (spadonízō)

  1. to castrate, emasculate
  2. to make flaccid
