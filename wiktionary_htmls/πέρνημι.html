Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • πιπρᾱ́σκω (piprā́skō) — Attic

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *per- (“to sell”); cognate with περάω (peráō) and πόρνη (pórnē), and with Latin Latin pars and portiō.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pér.nɛː.mi/ → /ˈper.ni.mi/ → /ˈper.ni.mi/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pér.nɛː.mi/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈper.ne̝.mi/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈper.ni.mi/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈper.ni.mi/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈper.ni.mi/

Verb

[edit]

πέρνημι • (pérnēmi)

  1. to sell
