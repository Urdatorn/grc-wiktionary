Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἀπο- (apo-, “from”) +‎ στέλλω (stéllō, “to send”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /a.pos.tél.lɔː/ → /a.posˈtel.lo/ → /a.posˈte.lo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /a.pos.tél.lɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /a.posˈtel.lo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /a.posˈtel.lo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /a.posˈtel.lo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /a.posˈte.lo/

Verb

[edit]

ἀποστέλλω • (apostéllō)

  1. to send off, send away from
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Electra 71
    • 480 BCE – 406 BCE, Euripides, Cyclops 468
    • 408 BCE, Euripides, The Phoenician Women 485
    • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 607B
    1. (absolute) to send away, banish
      • 409 BCE, Sophocles, Philoctetes 450
      • 424 BCE, Euripides, Hecuba 731
    2. (passive voice) to go away, depart, set out
      • 429 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus the King 115
      • 423 BCE, Euripides, The Suppliants 582
      • 412 BCE, Euripides, Helen 660
      • 408 BCE, Euripides, The Phoenician Women 319
      • 413 BCE, Euripides, Iphigenia in Tauris 1409
  2. to send off, dispatch, on some mission or service
    • 409 BCE, Sophocles, Philoctetes 125
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.46
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 7.235
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 5.32
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 1.45
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 4.150
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Politics 5.7.2
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 3.28
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 2.1.5
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 3.26
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 3.26
  3. to put off, doff
    • 425 BCE, Aristophanes, Lysistrata 1084
  4. (intransitive) to go back, retire
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 3.89
