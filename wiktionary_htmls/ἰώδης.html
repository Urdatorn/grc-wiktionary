Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology 1

[edit]

ῐ̓́ον (íon, “violet, Viola odorata”) +‎ -ώδης (-ṓdēs, “smelling of; full of, like”)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /i.ɔ̌ː.dɛːs/ → /iˈo.ðis/ → /iˈo.ðis/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /i.ɔ̌ː.dɛːs/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /iˈo.de̝s/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /iˈo.ðis/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /iˈo.ðis/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /iˈo.ðis/

Adjective

[edit]

ῐ̓ώδης • (iṓdēs) m or f (neuter ῐ̓ῶδες); third declension

  1. violet-coloured, violet
    Synonym: ῐ̓οειδής (ioeidḗs)
    • Hpc., Progn. 40
  2. smelling of violets, violet-scented
    • Diosc. 5.171

Etymology 2

[edit]

ῑ̓ός (īós, “rust, verdigris; poison, venom”) +‎ -ώδης (-ṓdēs, “full of, like”)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /iː.ɔ̌ː.dɛːs/ → /iˈo.ðis/ → /iˈo.ðis/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /iː.ɔ̌ː.dɛːs/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /iˈo.de̝s/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /iˈo.ðis/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /iˈo.ðis/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /iˈo.ðis/

Adjective

[edit]

ῑ̓ώδης • (īṓdēs) m or f (neuter ῑ̓ῶδες); third declension

  1. like verdigris, green
    • 460 BCE – 370 BCE, Hippocrates, Prognostics 11
    • 40 CE – 90 CE, Dioscorides, Materia medica 5.79
    • 129 CE – 216 CE, Galen, Collected Works 10.871
    • 129 CE – 216 CE, Galen, Collected Works 18.107, (of bile)
    1. (figuratively) “green with envy”, envious
      • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Moralia 2.565c:
        κακόνοια…τουτὶ τὸ ἰῶδες…ἀφίησιν
        kakónoia…toutì tò iôdes…aphíēsin
  2. rust-coloured, ferruginous
    • 371 BCE – 287 BCE, Theophrastus, On Stones 37
    • 310 BCE – 240 BCE, Callimachus, Hist. 4
    • 40 CE – 90 CE, Dioscorides, Materia medica 5.152
    • Hpc. 68a
    • Diosc. 5.92
  3. poisonous, venomous
    Synonym: ῑ̓οειδής (īoeidḗs)
    • 250 CE – 350 CE, Athenaeus, Deipnosophistae 2.42a:
      ὕδωρ
      húdōr
    • 129 CE – 216 CE, Galen, Collected Works 11.327
    • Philostr.Jun., Im. 5, (of serpents):
      ὀδόντες
      odóntes
    1. (figuratively, of persons) virulent
      • 90 CE – 168 CE, Ptolemy, Tetrabiblos 158
  4. (ὄδ-ωδα) acrid
    • 460 BCE – 370 BCE, Hippocrates, On Ancient Medicine 19:
      ὀξύτητες
      oxútētes
    • 200 CE – 400 CE, Philumenus, On Poisonous Animals and Their Remedies 36.3:
      ἄσθμα
      ásthma
    • Hpc., V. med. 37
