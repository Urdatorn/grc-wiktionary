Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From γῆ (gê) +‎ -ο- (-o-), with quantitative metathesis.

Prefix

[edit]

γεω- • (geō-)

  1. Combining form of γῆ (gê): geo-
    ‎γεω- (geō-) + ‎-γραφίᾱ (-graphíā, “writing”) → ‎γεωγραφίᾱ (geōgraphíā, “geography”)

Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • γεώ- (geó-)
  • γαιο- (gaio-)
  • γαιό- (gaió-)

Etymology

[edit]

Inherited from Ancient Greek γεω- (geō-), from γῆ (gê).

Prefix

[edit]

γεω- • (geo-)

  1. geo-
    ‎γεω- (geo-) + ‎-γραφία (-grafía, “writing”) → ‎γεωγραφία (geografía, “geography”)
    ‎γεω- (geo-) + ‎προωθητής (proothitís, “pusher”) → ‎γεωπροωθητής (geoproothitís, “bulldozer”)
