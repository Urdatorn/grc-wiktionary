Etymology

[edit]

From Ἄβδηρα (Ábdēra, “Abdera”) +‎ -ῑ́της (-ī́tēs, masculine demonymic suffix).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ab.dɛː.rǐː.tɛːs/ → /aβ.ðiˈri.tis/ → /av.ðiˈri.tis/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ab.dɛː.rǐː.tɛːs/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ab.de̝ˈri.te̝s/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /aβ.ðiˈri.tis/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /av.ðiˈri.tis/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /av.ðiˈri.tis/

Noun

[edit]

Ἀβδηρῑ́της • (Abdērī́tēs) m (genitive Ἀβδηρῑ́του); first declension

  1. An inhabitant of Abdera: Abderite, Abderian
