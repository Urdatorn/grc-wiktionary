Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From κατα- (kata-) +‎ δέω (déō, “to bind, tie”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ka.ta.dé.ɔː/ → /ka.taˈðe.o/ → /ka.taˈðe.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ka.ta.dé.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ka.taˈde.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ka.taˈðe.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ka.taˈðe.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ka.taˈðe.o/

Verb

[edit]

κᾰτᾰδέω • (katadéō)

  1. to bind on or to, bind fast, bind up
  2. to put in bonds, imprison
  3. to convict and condemn of a crime
  4. to tie down, stop, check
