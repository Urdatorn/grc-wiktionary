Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ἐπίημι (epíēmi) — Ionic

Etymology

[edit]

ἐπι- (epi-) +‎ ἵημι (híēmi)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.pʰí.ɛː.mi/ → /eˈɸi.i.mi/ → /eˈfi.i.mi/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.pʰí.ɛː.mi/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /eˈpʰi.e̝.mi/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /eˈɸi.i.mi/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /eˈfi.i.mi/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /eˈfi.i.mi/

Verb

[edit]

ἐφίημῐ • (ephíēmi)

  1. to send to
  2. to let fly
  3. to lay hands on
  4. to incite
  5. to bring, impose (troubles etc. upon someone)
  6. (in middle) to enjoin upon, command
  7. (in middle + gen.) to aim at, (+ inf.) to desire to do
