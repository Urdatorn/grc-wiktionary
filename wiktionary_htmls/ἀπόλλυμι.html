Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

ᾰ̓πο- (apo-, “away”) +‎ ὄλλῡμῐ (óllūmi, “to destroy”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /a.pól.lyː.mi/ → /aˈpol.ly.mi/ → /aˈpo.li.mi/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /a.pól.lyː.mi/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /aˈpol.ly.mi/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /aˈpol.ly.mi/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /aˈpol.ly.mi/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /aˈpo.li.mi/

Verb

[edit]

ᾰ̓πόλλῡμῐ • (apóllūmi)

  1. (active voice)
    1. to destroy utterly, kill, slay
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 5.758
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 1.268
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 5.648
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 2.49
      • 406 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus at Colonus 1454
      • 424 BCE, Euripides, Hecuba 946
      • 341 BCE, Demosthenes, On the Chersonese 70
      • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Electra 1360
      • 423 BCE, Aristophanes, The Clouds 892
      • 446 BCE – 386 BCE, Aristophanes, The Acharnians 470
      • 5th century BC, Pherecrates, Miners 1.20
      • 408 BCE – 334 BCE, Antiphanes Comicus, Philotis 1.8
      • 445 BCE – 380 BCE, Lysias, Collected Works 92.26
    2. to lose utterly
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 2.46, (compare Iliad 18.82)
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 1.354
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 16.861
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 12.350
      • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Electra 26
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 7.51
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 3.4.11, (compare 7.2.22)
      • 366 BCE – 348 BCE, Plato, Theaetetus 154C
  2. (middle, passive, and perfect)
    1. to perish, die
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 1.117
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 1.166
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 9.303
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 3.87
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 5.126
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 10.27
      • 423 BCE, Aristophanes, The Clouds 1077, (compare Plutarch, Moralia 2.185E)
      • 421 BCE, Aristophanes, Peace 355
      • 170 CE – 250 CE, Philostratus, Collected Works 865
      • 446 BCE – 386 BCE, Aristophanes, The Acharnians 151
      • 4th century BC, Eubulus, Chrysilla 1
      • 342 BCE – 290 BCE, Menander, The Woman On Fire 1
      • 436 BCE – 338 BCE, Isocrates, Collected Works 123A, (compare Plutarch, Moralia 2.469E)
      • 388 BCE, Aristophanes, Plutus 713, (compare 456)
      • 446 BCE – 386 BCE, Aristophanes, The Acharnians 865
      • 421 BCE, Aristophanes, Peace 2
      • 125 CE – 200 CE, Lucian, Dialogues of the Gods
    2. to be lost, slip away, vanish
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 11.586
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 7.117
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 10.186
