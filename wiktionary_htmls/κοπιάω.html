Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From κοπῐ́ᾱ (kopíā, “rest from toil”) +‎ -ᾰ́ω (-áō), from κόπος (kópos, “beating, suffering, work”) from κόπτω (kóptō, “to strike”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ko.pi.á.ɔː/ → /ko.piˈa.o/ → /ko.piˈa.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ko.pi.á.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ko.piˈa.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ko.piˈa.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ko.piˈa.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ko.piˈa.o/

Verb

[edit]

κοπῐᾰ́ω • (kopiáō)

  1. to be tired, grow weary
    • 411 BCE, Aristophanes, Thesmophoriazusae 795
    • 446 BCE – 386 BCE, Aristophanes, Fragments 318.8
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Septuagint, Deuteronomy 25.18
    • 414 BCE, Aristophanes, The Birds 735
    • 50 CE – 100 CE, The Gospel of John 4:6
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Problems 881A.14
  2. to work hard, toil
    • 70 CE – 110 CE, The Gospel of Matthew 6:28
    • 120 CE – 175 CE, Vettius Valens, Anthology 266.6
    • New Testament, First Epistle to Timothy 4:10
    • New Testament, Epistle to the Romans 16:6
    • New Testament, First Epistle to Timothy 5:17
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Septuagint, Joshua 24.13
