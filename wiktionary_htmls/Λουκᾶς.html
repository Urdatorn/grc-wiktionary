Etymology

[edit]

Formed with the suffix -ᾶς (-âs) as the hypocoristic of the Greco-Roman name Λούκιος (Loúkios, “Lucius”) or Λουκανός (Loukanós, “Lucanus”) or both.[1][2][3][4][5]

Proper noun

[edit]

Λουκᾶς • (Loukâs) m (genitive Λουκᾶ); first declension

  1. Luke, Lucas
