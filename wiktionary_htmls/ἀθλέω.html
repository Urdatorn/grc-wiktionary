Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ᾱ̓θλῶ (āthlô) — Attic, contracted

Etymology

[edit]

    From ἆθλος (âthlos, “contest, struggle”) or ἆθλον (âthlon, “prize, contest”) +‎ -έω (-éō, “verbal suffix”). In the Attic form ᾱ̓θλῶ (āthlô), ᾰε (ae) is contracted to ᾱ (ā).

    Pronunciation

    [edit]
     
    • IPA(key): /aː.tʰlé.ɔː/ → /aˈθle.o/ → /aˈθle.o/
    • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /aː.tʰlé.ɔː/
    • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /aˈtʰle.o/
    • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /aˈθle.o/
    • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /aˈθle.o/
    • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /aˈθle.o/

    Verb

    [edit]

    ᾱ̓θλέω • (āthléō) (Epic, Attic, Koine)

    1. (transitive, intransitive) to contend for a prize, combat, wrestle [with dative ‘with or against someone’]
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 4.389
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 23.274
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 24.734
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 5.22
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 873E
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 7.453
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 7.212
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.67
      • 360 BCE, Plato, Timaeus 19C, (Compare Laws 830A)
      • 406 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus at Colonus 564
      • 389 BCE – 314 BCE, Aeschines, Collected Works 47.37
    2. to be an athlete, contend for a prize in games
      • Simonides of Ceos 149C
