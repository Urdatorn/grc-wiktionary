Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From κακοῦργος (kakoûrgos, “malicious, evildoer”) +‎ -έω (-éō, “verb forming suffix”)

Verb

[edit]

κακουργέω • (kakourgéō)

  1. do evil, work wickedness, be vicious,
  2. (of discussion) use captious or unfair arguments,
  3. maltreat, injure,
  4. ravage a country
  5. spoil the argument
  6. corrupt, falsify
