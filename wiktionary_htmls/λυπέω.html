Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From λῡ́πη (lū́pē, “pain”) +‎ -έω (-éō).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /lyː.pé.ɔː/ → /lyˈpe.o/ → /liˈpe.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /lyː.pé.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /lyˈpe.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /lyˈpe.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /lyˈpe.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /liˈpe.o/

Verb

[edit]

λῡπέω • (lūpéō)

  1. to give pain to, to pain, distress, grieve, vex
    • 750 BCE – 650 BCE, Hesiod, Works and Days 401
    • 438 BCE, Euripides, Alcestis 239
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Memorabilia 3.10.15
    • 480 BCE – 406 BCE, Euripides, Cyclops 338
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 8.144
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cyropaedia 3.3.50
    • 400 BCE – 387 BCE, Plato, Apology 41E
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 7.7.12
    • 361 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Midias 135
    • 429 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus the King 74
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Electra 59
    • 386 BCE – 367 BCE, Plato, Cratylus 393E
    1. (absolute) to cause pain or grief
      • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Ajax 589
      • 442 BCE, Sophocles, Antigone 573
      • 429 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus the King 1231
    2. (in historical writers, of cavalry and light troops) to harass, annoy
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 9.40
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 6.66
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Hellenica 6.3.14
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 2.3.23
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 4.53
      • 414 BCE, Aristophanes, The Birds 1427
  2. (passive voice, future middle) to be pained, grieved, distressed, sad, to grieve
    • 431 BCE, Euripides, Medea 474
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Ajax 555
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 8.100
    • 445 BCE – 380 BCE, Lysias, On the Murder of Eratosthenes 11
    • 380 BCE, Plato, Gorgias 494A
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Philebus 36A
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Ajax 1086
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 2.64
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Philebus 52B
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Memorabilia 3.9.8
    • 431 BCE, Euripides, Medea 286
    • 330 BCE, Demosthenes, On the Crown 27
    • 413 BCE, Euripides, Ion 632
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 689B
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Protagoras 354D
