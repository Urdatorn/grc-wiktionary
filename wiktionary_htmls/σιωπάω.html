Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From σῐωπή (siōpḗ) +‎ -ᾰ́ω (-áō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /si.ɔː.pá.ɔː/ → /si.oˈpa.o/ → /si.oˈpa.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /si.ɔː.pá.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /si.oˈpa.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /si.oˈpa.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /si.oˈpa.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /si.oˈpa.o/

Verb

[edit]

σῐωπᾰ́ω • (siōpáō)

  1. to be silent or still
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 2.280
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 23.568
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 17.513
    1. (of bees) to be still
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, History of Animals 9.40,53
  2. (transitive) to hold silent, keep secret, to not speak of
    • 480 BCE – 406 BCE, Euripides, Fragments 1024
    • 411 BCE, Aristophanes, Thesmophoriazusae 27
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Symposium 6,10
  3. (middle voice) to make silent, silence
    • 200 BCE – 118 BCE, Polybius, The Histories 18.29,4
