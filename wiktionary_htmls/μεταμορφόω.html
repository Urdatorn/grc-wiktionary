Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • μεταμορφῶ (metamorphô) — contracted

Etymology

[edit]

From μετᾰ- (meta-, “concerning change”) +‎ μορφόω (morphóō, “to form”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /me.ta.mor.pʰó.ɔː/ → /me.ta.morˈɸo.o/ → /me.ta.morˈfo.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /me.ta.mor.pʰó.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /me.ta.morˈpʰo.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /me.ta.morˈɸo.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /me.ta.morˈfo.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /me.ta.morˈfo.o/

Verb

[edit]

μετᾰμορφόω • (metamorphóō)

  1. to transform, disguise
    • 95 CE – 165 CE, Appian, Civil Wars 4.41
    • 250 CE – 350 CE, Athenaeus, Deipnosophistae 8.334c
    • 36 BCE – 30 BCE, Diodorus Siculus, Historical Library 4.81
    • 129 CE – 216 CE, Galen, Collected Works 19.479
    • 125 CE – 200 CE, Lucian, The Ass 11
    • 70 CE – 110 CE, The Gospel of Matthew 17:2
    • New Testament, Epistle to the Romans 12:2
    • 55 CE – 56 CE, Paul the Apostle, Second Epistle to the Corinthians 3:18
    • 280 BCE – 220 BCE, Philo of Byzantium, Compendium of Mechanics 2.559
    • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Moralia 2.52d
