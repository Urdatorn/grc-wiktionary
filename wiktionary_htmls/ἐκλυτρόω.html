Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ἐγλυτρόω (eglutróō)

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐκ- (ek-, “out”) +‎ λυτρόω (lutróō, “to ransom”)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.kly.tró.ɔː/ → /e.klyˈtro.o/ → /e.kliˈtro.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.kly.tró.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /e.klyˈtro.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /e.klyˈtro.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /e.klyˈtro.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /e.kliˈtro.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἐκλυτρόω • (eklutróō)

  1. to set free, release on paying a ransom
  2. to redeem
  3. to release oneself from guilt by ransom
  4. to redeem oneself from a wrongdoing before the divine
