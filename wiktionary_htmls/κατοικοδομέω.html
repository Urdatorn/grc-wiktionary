Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Form κατα- (kata-, “intensifying prefix”) +‎ οικοδομέω (oikodoméō, “build”)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ka.toi̯.ko.do.mé.ɔː/ → /ka.ty.ko.ðoˈme.o/ → /ka.ti.ko.ðoˈme.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ka.toi̯.ko.do.mé.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ka.ty.ko.doˈme.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ka.ty.ko.ðoˈme.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ka.ty.ko.ðoˈme.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ka.ti.ko.ðoˈme.o/

Verb

[edit]

κᾰτοικοδομέω • (katoikodoméō)

  1. to build upon or in a place
  2. to use, squander in building
  3. to shut up in a house
  4. (passive voice) to be built up, blocked up
