Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From νόθος (nóthos, “spurious, counterfeit”) +‎ -εύω (-eúō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /no.tʰěu̯.ɔː/ → /noˈθe.βo/ → /noˈθe.vo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /no.tʰěu̯.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /noˈtʰe.wo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /noˈθe.βo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /noˈθe.vo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /noˈθe.vo/

Verb

[edit]

νοθεύω • (notheúō)

  1. to corrupt, adulterate
  2. to consider spurious
