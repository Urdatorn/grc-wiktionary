Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From γάμος (gámos, “marriage, matrimony”) +‎ -έω (-éō, denominative verbal suffix).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ɡa.mé.ɔː/ → /ɣaˈme.o/ → /ɣaˈme.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ɡa.mé.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ɡaˈme.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ɣaˈme.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ɣaˈme.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ɣaˈme.o/

Verb

[edit]

γαμέω • (gaméō)

  1. to marry (a wife)
  2. (middle voice) to give oneself in marriage, to marry (a man)
  3. (middle voice) to get a spouse for, to betroth (generally of parents for their children)
  4. to take (a woman) as a lover
  5. (euphemistic) to have sex with
