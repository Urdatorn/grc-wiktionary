Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *glag- (“to make a noise, clap, twitter”), from Proto-Indo-European *gal- (“to roop, scream, shout”).[1] Cognate with English clack and Latin clangō (“I clang”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /kláz.dɔː/ → /ˈkla.zo/ → /ˈkla.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /kláz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈkla.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈkla.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈkla.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈkla.zo/

Verb

[edit]

κλᾰ́ζω • (klázō)

  1. to make a sharp piercing sound (scream, bay, clash)
