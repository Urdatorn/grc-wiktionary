Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From λῡσῐτελής (lūsitelḗs, “profitable”) +‎ -έω (-éō, “a verb forming suffix”)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /lyː.si.te.lé.ɔː/ → /ly.si.teˈle.o/ → /li.si.teˈle.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /lyː.si.te.lé.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ly.si.teˈle.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ly.si.teˈle.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ly.si.teˈle.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /li.si.teˈle.o/

Verb

[edit]

λῡσῐτελέω • (lūsiteléō)

  1. to solve expenses, to profit
