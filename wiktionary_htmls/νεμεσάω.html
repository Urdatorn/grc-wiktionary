Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • νεμεσσάω (nemessáō) — Epic

Etymology

[edit]

From νέμεσις (némesis) +‎ -άω (-áō)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ne.me.sá.ɔː/ → /ne.meˈsa.o/ → /ne.meˈsa.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ne.me.sá.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ne.meˈsa.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ne.meˈsa.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ne.meˈsa.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ne.meˈsa.o/

Verb

[edit]

νεμεσάω • (nemesáō)

  1. to feel just resentment, be angry at undeserved fortune, be indignant
  2. (middle voice, passive voice) to be displeased with oneself, be ashamed
