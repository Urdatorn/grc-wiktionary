Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ᾱ̓κούσῐος (ākoúsios) — Attic

Etymology

[edit]

ᾰ̓- (a-) +‎ ἑκούσῐος (hekoúsios)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /a.e.kǔː.si.os/ → /a.eˈku.si.os/ → /a.eˈku.si.os/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /a.e.kǔː.si.os/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /a.eˈku.si.os/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /a.eˈku.si.os/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /a.eˈku.si.os/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /a.eˈku.si.os/

Adjective

[edit]

ᾰ̓εκούσῐος • (aekoúsios) m (feminine ᾰ̓εκούσῐον or ᾰ̓εκουσῐ́ᾱ, neuter ᾰ̓εκούσῐον); first/second declension
(Ionic and Epic, also in anapaests)

  1. (of acts or their consequences) against the will, constrained
    1. (often in Attic, of offences) involuntary
  2. (of persons, only in the adverb ἀκουσίως) involuntarily
