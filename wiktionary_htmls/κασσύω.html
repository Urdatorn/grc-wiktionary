Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • κᾰττῡ́ω (kattū́ō) — Attic

Etymology

[edit]

The presence of the related word καττύς (kattús, “piece of leather”) suggests a Pre-Greek etymology, in view of the variation -σσ-/-ττ-.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /kas.sy̌ː.ɔː/ → /kasˈsy.o/ → /kaˈsi.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /kas.sy̌ː.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /kasˈsy.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /kasˈsy.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /kasˈsy.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /kaˈsi.o/

Verb

[edit]

κᾰσσῡ́ω • (kassū́ō)

  1. to stitch, sew together like a shoemaker
  2. (figuratively) to stitch up a plot, trap, ensnare
