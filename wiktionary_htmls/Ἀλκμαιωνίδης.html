Etymology

[edit]

From Ᾰ̓λκμαίων (Alkmaíōn, “Alcmaeon”) +‎ -ῐ́δης (-ídēs, patronymic suffix).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /alk.mai̯.ɔː.ní.dɛːs/ → /alk.mɛ.oˈni.ðis/ → /alk.me.oˈni.ðis/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /alk.mai̯.ɔː.ní.dɛːs/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /alk.mɛ.oˈni.de̝s/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /alk.mɛ.oˈni.ðis/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /alk.me.oˈni.ðis/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /alk.me.oˈni.ðis/

Proper noun

[edit]

Ᾰ̓λκμαιωνῐ́δης • (Alkmaiōnídēs) m (genitive Ἀλκμαιωνίδου); first declension

  1. a child of Alcmaeon; an Alcmaeonid
