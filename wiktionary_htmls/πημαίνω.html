Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From πῆμᾰ (pêma, “misery”) +‎ -αίνω (-aínō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pɛː.mǎi̯.nɔː/ → /piˈmɛ.no/ → /piˈme.no/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pɛː.mǎi̯.nɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /pe̝ˈmɛ.no/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /piˈmɛ.no/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /piˈme.no/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /piˈme.no/

Verb

[edit]

πημαίνω • (pēmaínō)

  1. to plunge into ruin, undo, bring bane or mischief upon
  2. (in milder sense) to grieve, distress
  3. (absolutive) to hurt, harm, damage
  4. (mediopassive) to violate an oath
