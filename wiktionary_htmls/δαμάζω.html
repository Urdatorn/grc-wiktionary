Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • δαμνάω (damnáō)
  • δάμνημι (dámnēmi)

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *demh₂-. Cognates include Sanskrit दाम्यति (dāmyati), Latin domō.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /da.máz.dɔː/ → /ðaˈma.zo/ → /ðaˈma.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /da.máz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /daˈma.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ðaˈma.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ðaˈma.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ðaˈma.zo/

Verb

[edit]

δᾰμᾰ́ζω • (damázō)

  1. to tame, subdue, control
  2. (of women) to give in marriage
  3. (of women) to seduce or rape
  4. to subdue, conquer, rule over
  5. to kill
  6. to overpower

Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Learnedly, borrowed from Ancient Greek δαμάζω (damázō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): /ðaˈma.zo/
  • Hyphenation: δα‧μά‧ζω

Verb

[edit]

δαμάζω • (damázo) (past δάμασα, passive δαμάζομαι)

  1. to tame, subdue, control
