Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ἀλλᾰ́ττω (alláttō) — Attic

Etymology

[edit]

From ἀλλαγή (allagḗ, “change”) +‎ -σσω (-ssō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /al.lás.sɔː/ → /alˈlas.so/ → /aˈla.so/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /al.lás.sɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /alˈlas.so/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /alˈlas.so/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /alˈlas.so/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /aˈla.so/

Verb

[edit]

ἀλλᾰ́σσω • (allássō)

  1. to change, alter, make other than it is
  2. to give in exchange, barter one thing for another
  3. to take one thing in exchange for another
  4. to take a new position
  5. to have dealings, as a buyer or seller
  6. to alternate
