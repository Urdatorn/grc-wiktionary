Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *l̥h₂-k-sḱéti (“to bark, growl”). Cognate with λακάω (lakáō, “to burst asunder”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /lás.kɔː/ → /ˈlas.ko/ → /ˈlas.ko/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /lás.kɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈlas.ko/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈlas.ko/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈlas.ko/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈlas.ko/

Verb

[edit]

λάσκω • (láskō)

  1. to ring, rattle, crash, crack
  2. to shriek, scream, howl, bay
  3. to shout, scream, cry aloud
  4. to noise abroad
  5. to sing
  6. to shriek forth, utter aloud
  7. to crack or burst asunder
