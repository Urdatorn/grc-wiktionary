Ancient Greek

[edit]
FWOTD – 26 September 2024

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • πῠρέττω (puréttō)

Etymology

[edit]

From πυρετός (puretós, “heat, fever”), from πῦρ (pûr, “fire, fever”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /py.rés.sɔː/ → /pyˈres.so/ → /piˈre.so/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /py.rés.sɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /pyˈres.so/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /pyˈres.so/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /pyˈres.so/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /piˈre.so/

Verb

[edit]

πῠρέσσω • (puréssō)

  1. to be feverish, have a fever
    • 389 BCE – 314 BCE, Aeschines, Against Ctesiphon 115
    • 422 BCE, Aristophanes, The Wasps 813
    • 100 CE – 200 CE, Artemidorus, Oneirocritica 4.30
    • 480 BCE – 406 BCE, Euripides, Cyclops 228
    • 460 BCE – 370 BCE, Hippocrates, Aphorisms 2.28
    • 300 CE – 400 CE, Sallustius, On the Gods and the Cosmos 9
