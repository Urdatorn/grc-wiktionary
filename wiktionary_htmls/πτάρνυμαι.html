Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Presumably imitative or from a Proto-Indo-European root connected to Polish kichać, Russian чихать (čixatʹ), Lithuanian čiáudėti, Sanskrit क्षु (kṣu), Breton strevia and Latin sternuo (“I sneeze”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ptár.ny.mai̯/ → /ˈptar.ny.mɛ/ → /ˈptar.ni.me/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ptár.ny.mai̯/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈptar.ny.mɛ/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈptar.ny.mɛ/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈptar.ny.me/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈptar.ni.me/

Verb

[edit]

πτᾰ́ρνῠμαι • (ptárnumai)

  1. to sneeze
