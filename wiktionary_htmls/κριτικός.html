Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From κρῐτής (kritḗs, “judge”) +‎ -ῐκός (-ikós), from κρῑ́νω (krī́nō, “I judge”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /kri.ti.kós/ → /kri.tiˈkos/ → /kri.tiˈkos/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /kri.ti.kós/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /kri.tiˈkos/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /kri.tiˈkos/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /kri.tiˈkos/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /kri.tiˈkos/

Adjective

[edit]

κρῐτῐκός • (kritikós) m (feminine κρῐτῐκή, neuter κρῐτῐκόν); first/second declension

  1. Able to judge, discerning, critical
  2. (Especially as a substantive concerning language): critic, grammarian, academic
