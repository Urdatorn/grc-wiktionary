Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

χάρις (kháris) +‎ -όω (-óō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /kʰa.ri.tó.ɔː/ → /xa.riˈto.o/ → /xa.riˈto.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /kʰa.ri.tó.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /kʰa.riˈto.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /xa.riˈto.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /xa.riˈto.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /xa.riˈto.o/

Verb

[edit]

χᾰρῐτόω • (kharitóō)

  1. to show grace to, to bestow favor on or upon
  2. (passive voice) to be full of grace, to be highly favored, to be shown grace, to have grace or favor shown to
