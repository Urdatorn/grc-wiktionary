Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From κολάζω (kolázō) +‎ -σις (-sis).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /kó.la.sis/ → /ˈko.la.sis/ → /ˈko.la.sis/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /kó.la.sis/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈko.la.sis/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈko.la.sis/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈko.la.sis/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈko.la.sis/

Noun

[edit]

κόλᾰσῐς • (kólasis) f (genitive κολάσεως); third declension

  1. chastisement, correction, punishment
