Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

The analysis as an expressive transformation of αἰψερός (aipserós, “quick, speedy”) after λάβρος (lábros, “violent, boisterous”) may be possible, but it is not evident.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /lai̯p.sɛː.rós/ → /lɛp.siˈros/ → /lep.siˈros/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /lai̯p.sɛː.rós/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /lɛp.se̝ˈros/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /lɛp.siˈros/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /lep.siˈros/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /lep.siˈros/

Adjective

[edit]

λαιψηρός • (laipsērós) m (feminine λαιψηρᾱ́, neuter λαιψηρόν); first/second declension

  1. light, nimble, swift
    Synonyms: εὐκῐ́νητος (eukínētos), ὀτρηρός (otrērós)
