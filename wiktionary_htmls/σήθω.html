Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

According to Beekes, from Proto-Indo-European *kyéh₂-dʰe-ti, dʰe-present formation of *kyeh₂- (“to sieve”).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /sɛ̌ː.tʰɔː/ → /ˈsi.θo/ → /ˈsi.θo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /sɛ̌ː.tʰɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈse̝.tʰo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈsi.θo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈsi.θo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈsi.θo/

Verb

[edit]

σήθω • (sḗthō)

  1. to sieve, sift, bolt, strain
