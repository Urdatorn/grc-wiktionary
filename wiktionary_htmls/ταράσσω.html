Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • τᾰρᾰ́ττω (taráttō) — Attic
  • θρᾰ́σσω (thrássō)

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *dʰreh₂gʰ- (“to confuse, agitate, disturb”). Cognate with τρᾱχῠ́ς (trākhús) and Proto-Slavic *dražiti (“to annoy, irritate”). Doublet of θρᾱ́σσω (thrā́ssō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ta.rás.sɔː/ → /taˈras.so/ → /taˈra.so/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ta.rás.sɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /taˈras.so/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /taˈras.so/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /taˈras.so/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /taˈra.so/

Verb

[edit]

τᾰρᾰ́σσω • (tarássō)

  1. to stir, trouble, agitate
  2. to trouble the mind, disturb
  3. (of an army) to throw into disorder
  4. (figuratively) to rout or upset
  5. to stir up, startle, excite
