Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ἠέ (ēé) — Epic

Etymology

[edit]

From the Proto-Hellenic *ēwe, from Proto-Indo-European *-wē (“or”).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ɛ̌ː/ → /i/ → /i/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ɛ̌ː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /e̝/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /i/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /i/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /i/

Conjunction

[edit]

ἤ • (ḗ)

  1. or
  2. than, as (in a comparative construction)

Usage notes

[edit]

The pair ἤ... ἦ (ḗ... ê) means ‘either... or...’ or ‘whether... or...’.

Derived terms

[edit]
  • ἤτοι (ḗtoi)

Descendants

[edit]
  • Greek: ή (í)
