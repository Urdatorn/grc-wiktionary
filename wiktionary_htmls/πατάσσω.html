Ancient Greek

[edit]
FWOTD – 25 August 2013

Etymology

[edit]

Possibly, from the same Pre-Greek root of πάταγος (pátagos, “clatter, crash”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pa.tás.sɔː/ → /paˈtas.so/ → /paˈta.so/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pa.tás.sɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /paˈtas.so/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /paˈtas.so/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /paˈtas.so/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /paˈta.so/

Verb

[edit]

πᾰτάσσω • (patássō)

  1. to knock, beat
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 7.216
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 13.282
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 23.370
    • 405 BCE, Aristophanes, The Frogs 54:
      τίς τὴν θύραν ἐπάταξεν;
      tís tḕn thúran epátaxen;
      Who knocked on the door?
    • 86 CE – 160 CE, Arrian, Cyn. 15
  2. (with accusative) to strike
    • 480 BCE – 411 BCE, Antiphon of Rhamnus, Third Tetralogy 3.4
    • 424 BCE, Aristophanes, The Knights 1130
    • 416 BCE, Euripides, Herakles 1007
    • 409 BCE, Sophocles, Philoctetes 748
    • 408 BCE, Euripides, The Phoenician Women 1463
    • 405 BCE, Aristophanes, The Frogs 548
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 8.92
    • 380 BCE, Plato, Gorgias 527c
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 879e
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Meteorology 371b.9
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Aristocrates 76
    • 361 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Midias 33
    • 50 CE – 150 CE, Achilles Tatius, Leucippe and Clitophon 2.7
    • 125 CE – 200 CE, Lucian, A Professor of Public Speaking 19
    1. to slaughter, kill
      • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, 1 Samuel 17.9
      • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Septuagint, Judges 9.43
    2. to afflict
      • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Septuagint, Deuteronomy 28.28
      • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Septuagint, Exodus 7.25
    3. (figuratively)
      • 6th century BC, Theognis of Megara, Elegies 1199
      • 442 BCE, Sophocles, Antigone 1097
      • 405 BCE, Aristophanes, The Frogs 54
      • 400 BCE – 300 BCE, Timocles, Collected Works 20
