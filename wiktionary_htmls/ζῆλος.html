Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *yeh₂- (“to go, travel, search, inquire”). Related to Ζητήρ (Zētḗr).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /zdɛ̂ː.los/ → /ˈzi.los/ → /ˈzi.los/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /zdɛ̂ː.los/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈze̝.los/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈzi.los/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈzi.los/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈzi.los/

Noun

[edit]

ζῆλος • (zêlos) m (genitive ζήλου); second declension

  1. eager rivalry, zealous imitation, emulation, a noble passion
    • 386 BCE – 367 BCE, Plato, Menexenus 242A
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Rhetoric 2.11.1
    • 750 BCE – 650 BCE, Hesiod, Works and Days 193
    • 445 BCE – 380 BCE, Lysias, Collected Works 195.13
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Philebus 47E
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Philebus 50B
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 679C
    • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 550E
    1. (with genitive) zeal for one
      • 406 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus at Colonus 943
      • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Theseus 25
      • 125 CE – 200 CE, Lucian, Demonax 57
    2. (with genitive)
      • 424 BCE, Euripides, Hecuba 352
      • 125 CE – 200 CE, Lucian, The Ignorant Book-Collector 17
      • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Coriolanus 4
      • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Pericles 2
  2. (passive voice) the object of emulation or desire, happiness, bliss, honour, glory
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Ajax 503
    • 330 BCE, Demosthenes, On the Crown 217
    • 330 BCE, Demosthenes, On the Crown 120
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Aristocrates 64, (Compare On the Crown 273, Funeral Speech 33)
  3. (of the style of Asiatic Orators) extravagance, fierceness
    • 64 BCE – 24 CE, Strabo, Geography 648
    • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Mark Antony 2
    • New Testament, Epistle to the Hebrews 10:27
