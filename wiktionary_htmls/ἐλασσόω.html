Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ἐλᾰττόω (elattóō) — Attic

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐλάσσων (elássōn, “smaller, less”) +‎ -όω (-óō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.las.só.ɔː/ → /e.lasˈso.o/ → /e.laˈso.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.las.só.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /e.lasˈso.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /e.lasˈso.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /e.lasˈso.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /e.laˈso.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἐλᾰσσόω • (elassóō)

  1. to make less or smaller, to diminish, reduce in amount
  2. (in early writers) to lower, degrade
  3. to cut down, shorten
  4. (passive voice) to be lessened, depreciated, to suffer loss
  5. (passive voice) to have the worst of it, to be inferior
  6. (passive voice) to be at disadvantage with a person
