Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From πόρος (póros, “way, bridge”) +‎ -εύω (-eúō). From πείρω (peírō, “to pierce, pass through”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /po.rěu̯.ɔː/ → /poˈre.βo/ → /poˈre.vo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /po.rěu̯.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /poˈre.wo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /poˈre.βo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /poˈre.vo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /poˈre.vo/

Verb

[edit]

πορεύω • (poreúō)

  1. (active voice) to cause to go, carry, convey
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Olympian Ode 1.125
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Pythian Ode 11.32
    • 409 BCE, Sophocles, Philoctetes 516
    • 406 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus at Colonus 1476
    • 415 BCE, Euripides, The Trojan Women 1086
    • 424 BCE, Euripides, Hecuba 447
    • 431 BCE, Euripides, Medea 181
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 4.131
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Women of Trachis 559
    • 438 BCE, Euripides, Alcestis 444
    1. (of things) to bring, carry, furnish, bestow, set in motion
      • 406 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus at Colonus 1602
      • 408 BCE, Euripides, The Phoenician Women 985
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 893D
  2. (in middle or passive) to be driven or carried
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Ajax 1254
    • 406 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus at Colonus 845
    1. to go, walk, march
      • 385 BCE – 380 BCE, Plato, Symposium 190D
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, The Statesman 266C
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 2.2.12
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cyropaedia 4.3.13
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 5.3.1
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 8.107
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 7.29
      • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Women of Trachis 392
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 6.95
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 4.5.10
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 2.2.11
      • 413 BCE, Euripides, Ion 1238
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 2.2.12
      • 458 BCE, Euripides, Electra 965
      • 408 BCE, Euripides, Orestes 1068
    2. to walk, i.e. to live
      • 429 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus the King 884
    3. (figuratively)
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cyropaedia 2.2.24
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 812A
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Memorabilia 4.6.15
