Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From θώρηξ (thṓrēx) +‎ -σσω (-ssō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /tʰɔː.rɛ̌ːs.sɔː/ → /θoˈris.so/ → /θoˈri.so/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /tʰɔː.rɛ̌ːs.sɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /tʰoˈre̝s.so/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /θoˈris.so/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /θoˈris.so/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /θoˈri.so/

Verb

[edit]

θωρήσσω • (thōrḗssō)

  1. to arm with a breastplate
    Synonym: θωρακίζω (thōrakízō)
