Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ῥέδδω (rhéddō) — Doric, Boeotian

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *wréǵ-ye-, a *ye-present from *wreǵ-, a metathesis of *werǵ-, the root of ἔργον (érgon) and ἔρδω (érdō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /r̥éz.dɔː/ → /ˈre.zo/ → /ˈre.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /r̥éz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈre.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈre.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈre.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈre.zo/

Verb

[edit]

ῥέζω • (rhézō) (poetic)

  1. (transitive) to do, make
    1. to perform sacrifices
    2. to do something to someone (with two accusatives)
  2. (intransitive) to act (as opposed to speaking or being acted on)
