Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ὀλοφύρρω (olophúrrhō) — Aeolic

Etymology

[edit]

From ὅλος (hólos, “whole”) + φύρομαι (phúromai, “I am mixed”). Compare ὀλολύζω (ololúzō), ὀλολυγμός (ololugmós).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /o.lo.pʰy̌ː.ro.mai̯/ → /o.loˈɸy.ro.mɛ/ → /o.loˈfi.ro.me/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /o.lo.pʰy̌ː.ro.mai̯/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /o.loˈpʰy.ro.mɛ/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /o.loˈɸy.ro.mɛ/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /o.loˈfy.ro.me/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /o.loˈfi.ro.me/

Verb

[edit]

ὀλοφῡ́ρομαι • (olophū́romai)

  1. (intransitive) to lament, wail, moan
    • 460 BCE – 370 BCE, Democritus, Collected Works 107a
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.141
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 5.871
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 24.328
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 10.409
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 22.447
    • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 329a
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 6.78
    1. to feel pity
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 16.450
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 8.33
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 8.202
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 22.169
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 11.418
    2. to lament (+ infinitive)
      • 445 BCE – 380 BCE, Lysias, Against Philocrates 4
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 22.232
  2. (transitive) to lament over, bewail
    • c. 440 BCE, Euripides, Rhesus 896
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 5.4
    • 445 BCE – 380 BCE, Lysias, Funeral Oration 37
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 19.522
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Electra 148
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 2.44
    1. to pity
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 8.245
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 4.364
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 10.157
