Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *h₂ḱrós (“sharp”). Cognates include Old Church Slavonic остръ (ostrŭ), Latin ācer, Old Armenian ասեղն (asełn, “needle”) and Old English eċġ (English edge).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /á.kros/ → /ˈa.kros/ → /ˈa.kros/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /á.kros/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈa.kros/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈa.kros/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈa.kros/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈa.kros/

Adjective

[edit]

ᾰ̓́κρος • (ákros) m (feminine ᾰ̓́κρᾱ, neuter ᾰ̓́κρον); first/second declension

  1. at the edge, extreme, beginning, end: outermost (especially of the top)
  2. pointed, sharp
  3. being the most of any characteristic: best, oldest, first
