Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *ǵn̥h₁-yé-tor. Compare γίγνομαι (gígnomai), Latin gigno, gnatus and Sanskrit जनति (jánati).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ɡěː.no.mai̯/ → /ˈʝi.no.mɛ/ → /ˈʝi.no.me/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ɡěː.no.mai̯/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈɡi.no.mɛ/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈʝi.no.mɛ/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈʝi.no.me/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈʝi.no.me/

Verb

[edit]

γείνομαι • (geínomai)

  1. to be born
  2. to beget, bring into being
