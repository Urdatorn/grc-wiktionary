Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Hellenic *pluňňō, from a nasal-present formation of Proto-Indo-European *plew- (“to wash”), the same root of Latin pluit (“it rains”) and Old Armenian լուանամ (luanam, “to wash”).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ply̌ː.nɔː/ → /ˈply.no/ → /ˈpli.no/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ply̌ː.nɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈply.no/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈply.no/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈply.no/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈpli.no/

Verb

[edit]

πλῡ́νω • (plū́nō)

  1. (especially of clothes) to wash, clean
