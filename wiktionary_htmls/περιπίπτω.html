Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From περι- (peri-) +‎ πῑ́πτω (pī́ptō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pe.ri.pǐːp.tɔː/ → /pe.riˈpip.to/ → /pe.riˈpip.to/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pe.ri.pǐːp.tɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /pe.riˈpip.to/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /pe.riˈpip.to/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /pe.riˈpip.to/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /pe.riˈpip.to/

Verb

[edit]

περῐπῑ́πτω • (peripī́ptō)

  1. to fall around, so as to embrace [with dative]
  2. to fall upon (a weapon) [with dative]
  3. to fall in with, encounter [with dative]
  4. to fall foul of
  5. to change suddenly
  6. to fall on one side
