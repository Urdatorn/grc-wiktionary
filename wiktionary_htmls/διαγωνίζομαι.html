Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From δῐᾰ- (dia-, “across”) +‎ ᾰ̓γωνῐ́ζομαι (agōnízomai, “contend for a prize”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /di.a.ɡɔː.níz.do.mai̯/ → /ði.a.ɣoˈni.zo.mɛ/ → /ði.a.ɣoˈni.zo.me/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /di.a.ɡɔː.níz.do.mai̯/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /di.a.ɡoˈni.zo.mɛ/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ði.a.ɣoˈni.zo.mɛ/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ði.a.ɣoˈni.zo.me/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ði.a.ɣoˈni.zo.me/

Verb

[edit]

δῐᾰγωνῐ́ζομαι • (diagōnízomai)

  1. to contend, struggle or fight against
  2. to fight desperately, contend earnestly

Descendants

Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Ancient Greek διαγωνίζομαι (diagōnízomai). Morphologically, from δια- (“through”) +‎ αγωνίζομαι (“struggle”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): /ðʝa.ɣoˈni.zo.me/, /ðia.ɣoˈni.zo.me/
  • Hyphenation: δι‧α‧γω‧νί‧ζο‧μαι

Verb

[edit]

διαγωνίζομαι • (diagonízomai) deponent (past διαγωνίστηκα)

  1. to compete, take part in a competition or examination
