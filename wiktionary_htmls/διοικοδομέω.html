Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From δῐᾰ- (dia-, “across”) +‎ οἰκοδομέω (oikodoméō, “to build a house”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /di.oi̯.ko.do.mé.ɔː/ → /ði.y.ko.ðoˈme.o/ → /ði.i.ko.ðoˈme.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /di.oi̯.ko.do.mé.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /di.y.ko.doˈme.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ði.y.ko.ðoˈme.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ði.y.ko.ðoˈme.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ði.i.ko.ðoˈme.o/

Verb

[edit]

δῐοικοδομέω • (dioikodoméō)

  1. to build across, wall off
  2. to barricade
