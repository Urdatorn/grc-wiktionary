Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *(ts)ptyew- (“to spit, vomit”). Akin to Latin spuō, Albanian pështyj, Old English spīwan (English spew), Old Armenian թուք (tʻukʻ), Old Church Slavonic пльвати (plĭvati), Sanskrit ष्ठीवति (ṣṭhīvati).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pty̌ː.ɔː/ → /ˈpty.o/ → /ˈpti.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pty̌ː.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈpty.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈpty.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈpty.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈpti.o/

Verb

[edit]

πτῡ́ω • (ptū́ō)

  1. to spit out or up
  2. (of the sea) to disgorge
