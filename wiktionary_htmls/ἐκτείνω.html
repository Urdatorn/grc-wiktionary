Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐκ- (ek-, “out”) +‎ τείνω (teínō, “to stretch”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ek.těː.nɔː/ → /ekˈti.no/ → /ekˈti.no/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ek.těː.nɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ekˈti.no/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ekˈti.no/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ekˈti.no/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ekˈti.no/

Verb

[edit]

ἐκτείνω • (ekteínō)

  1. to stretch out
  2. to prolong, draw out
  3. (passive voice) to lay outstretched
  4. to strain, push to the limit
  5. (grammar) to lengthen a vowel
