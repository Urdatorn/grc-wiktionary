Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • Κορκῡραῖος (Korkūraîos)

Etymology

[edit]

From Κέρκῡρᾰ (Kérkūra, “Corcyra, Corfu”) +‎ -αῖος (-aîos, demonymic suffix).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ker.kyː.râi̯.os/ → /cer.cyˈrɛ.os/ → /cer.ciˈre.os/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ker.kyː.râi̯.os/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ker.kyˈrɛ.os/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /cer.cyˈrɛ.os/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /cer.cyˈre.os/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /cer.ciˈre.os/

Adjective

[edit]

Κερκῡραῖος • (Kerkūraîos) m (feminine Κερκῡραίᾱ, neuter Κερκῡραῖον); first/second declension (Attic, Ionic)

  1. Corcyrean, Corfiot, originating from or located in Corcyra or Corfu
