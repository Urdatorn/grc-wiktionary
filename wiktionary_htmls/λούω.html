Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • λοέω (loéō)
  • λόω (lóō)

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *lewh₃-. Cognates include Albanian laj, Latin lavō, Old Armenian լոգանամ (loganam), and Old English lēaþor (English lather).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /lǔː.ɔː/ → /ˈlu.o/ → /ˈlu.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /lǔː.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈlu.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈlu.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈlu.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈlu.o/

Verb

[edit]

λούω • (loúō)

  1. to wash, bathe (generally of people)
  2. to purify, cleanse
  3. (middle voice) to bathe oneself, take a bath
    Synonym: καθαίρω (kathaírō)
