Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἀνα- (ana-) + an unattested *ἁλίσκω (*halískō), originally meaning "to tear up"; see ἁλίσκομαι (halískomai, “to be captured”) for more on the second component.[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /a.naː.lís.kɔː/ → /a.naˈlis.ko/ → /a.naˈlis.ko/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /a.naː.lís.kɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /a.naˈlis.ko/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /a.naˈlis.ko/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /a.naˈlis.ko/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /a.naˈlis.ko/

Verb

[edit]

ἀνᾱλίσκω • (anālískō)

  1. to spend
  2. to kill, destroy
