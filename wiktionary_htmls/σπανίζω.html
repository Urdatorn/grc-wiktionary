Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From σπάνιος (spánios) +‎ -ίζω (-ízō)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /spa.níz.dɔː/ → /spaˈni.zo/ → /spaˈni.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /spa.níz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /spaˈni.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /spaˈni.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /spaˈni.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /spaˈni.zo/

Verb

[edit]

σπᾰνίζω • (spanízō)

  1. to be rare, scarce, scanty (of things)
  2. (active voice or passive voice) to lack or be in want of [with genitive] (of persons)
