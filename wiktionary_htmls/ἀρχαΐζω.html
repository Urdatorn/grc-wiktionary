Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἀρχαῖος (arkhaîos, “old, ancient”) +‎ -ίζω (-ízō, denominative verb suffix).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ar.kʰa.íz.dɔː/ → /ar.xaˈi.zo/ → /ar.xaˈi.zo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ar.kʰa.íz.dɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ar.kʰaˈi.zo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ar.xaˈi.zo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ar.xaˈi.zo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ar.xaˈi.zo/

Verb

[edit]

ἀρχαΐζω • (arkhaḯzō)

  1. to be old-fashioned, copy the ancients (in manners, language, etc.)
