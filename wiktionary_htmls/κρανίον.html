Etymology

[edit]

Form of κᾰ́ρᾱ (kárā, “the head”) +‎ -ῐ́ον (-íon, diminutive suffix).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /kraː.ní.on/ → /kraˈni.on/ → /kraˈni.on/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /kraː.ní.on/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /kraˈni.on/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /kraˈni.on/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /kraˈni.on/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /kraˈni.on/

Noun

[edit]

κρᾱνῐ́ον • (krāníon) n (genitive κρᾱνῐ́ου); second declension

  1. (anatomy) The upper part of the head, the cranium.
    1. (metonymy) The skull.
    2. (metonymy) The head.
  2. A headache.
