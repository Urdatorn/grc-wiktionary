Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐπι- (epi-, “in addition to”) +‎ καταλῡ́ω (katalū́ō, “put down, destroy”)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.pi.ka.ta.ly̌ː.ɔː/ → /e.pi.ka.taˈly.o/ → /e.pi.ka.taˈli.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.pi.ka.ta.ly̌ː.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /e.pi.ka.taˈly.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /e.pi.ka.taˈly.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /e.pi.ka.taˈly.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /e.pi.ka.taˈli.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἐπῐκᾰτᾰλῡ́ω • (epikatalū́ō)

  1. to reduce yet further
