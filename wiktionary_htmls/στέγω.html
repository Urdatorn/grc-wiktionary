Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *(s)teg- (“to cover, roof”). Cognate with Lithuanian stíegiu (“to thatch, to cover a roof”), Latin tegō (“I cover”), Proto-Germanic *þakjaną (“to cover”).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /sté.ɡɔː/ → /ˈste.ɣo/ → /ˈste.ɣo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /sté.ɡɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈste.ɡo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈste.ɣo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈste.ɣo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈste.ɣo/

Verb

[edit]

στέγω • (stégō)

  1. to cover closely, so as to keep water either out or in
