Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐπι- (epi-, “on”) +‎ κατοικέω (katoikéō, “inhabit, dwell”)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.pi.ka.toi̯.ké.ɔː/ → /e.pi.ka.tyˈce.o/ → /e.pi.ka.tiˈce.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.pi.ka.toi̯.ké.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /e.pi.ka.tyˈke.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /e.pi.ka.tyˈce.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /e.pi.ka.tyˈce.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /e.pi.ka.tiˈce.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἐπῐκᾰτοικέω • (epikatoikéō)

  1. to live at, inhabit
