Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • φορύσσω (phorússō)

Etymology

[edit]

The formation with the suffix -ύνω (-únō) is similar to μολύνω (molúnō, “to stain, defile”) and παλύνω (palúnō, “to strew, sprinkle”). The element φορυ- (phoru-) cannot be analyzed. Perhaps it belongs to φύρω (phúrō, “to mix, defile, mingle”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pʰo.ry̌ː.nɔː/ → /ɸoˈry.no/ → /foˈri.no/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pʰo.ry̌ː.nɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /pʰoˈry.no/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ɸoˈry.no/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /foˈry.no/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /foˈri.no/

Verb

[edit]

φορῡ́νω • (phorū́nō)

  1. (passive voice) to be mingled, stained, defiled
    Synonyms: μολῠ́νω (molúnō), φῡ́ρω (phū́rō)
