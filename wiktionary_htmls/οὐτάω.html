Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Morphologically and etymologically unclear. Often connected with ὠτειλή (ōteilḗ, “fresh wound”) and with ἀάω (aáō, “to hurt, damage”). Already Karl Meister[1] thought it of Pre-Greek origin, which seems to be the best solution, by the interpretation of ὠτειλή (ōteilḗ) as a variant.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /uː.tá.ɔː/ → /uˈta.o/ → /uˈta.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /uː.tá.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /uˈta.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /uˈta.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /uˈta.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /uˈta.o/

Verb

[edit]

οὐτᾰ́ω • (outáō)

  1. to stab, hit, wound
