Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From αὐλός (aulós, “flute”) +‎ -έω (-éō, denominative verbal suffix).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /au̯.lé.ɔː/ → /aˈβle.o/ → /aˈvle.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /au̯.lé.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /awˈle.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /aˈβle.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /aˈvle.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /aˈvle.o/

Verb

[edit]

αὐλέω • (auléō)

  1. to play on the flute
    • Alcman 71
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.141
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.60
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Symposium 9.3
    • 422 BCE, Aristophanes, The Wasps 582
  2. (passive voice, of tunes) to be played on the flute
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Symposium 9.3
    • 413 BCE, Euripides, Iphigenia in Tauris 367
    1. (passive voice, of persons) to be played to, to hear music
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 6.1.11
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cyropaedia 4.5.7
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Problems 19.1
