Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἀλλοῖος (alloîos, “different”) +‎ -όω (-óō, factitive verb-forming suffix).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /al.loi̯.ó.ɔː/ → /al.lyˈo.o/ → /a.liˈo.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /al.loi̯.ó.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /al.lyˈo.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /al.lyˈo.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /al.lyˈo.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /a.liˈo.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἀλλοιόω • (alloióō)

  1. (transitive) to make different, change, alter
  2. (passive voice)
    1. to become different, be changed, undergo an alteration
    2. to be estranged
    3. to be changed for the worse
    4. to be deranged in mind
