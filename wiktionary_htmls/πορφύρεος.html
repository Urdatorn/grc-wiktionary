Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • πορφύριος (porphúrios) — Aeolic
  • πορφῠροῦς (porphuroûs) — Attic

Etymology

[edit]

πορφύρᾱ (porphúrā) +‎ -εος (-eos), of unknown, perhaps Semitic, origin. The association with πορφύρω (porphúrō, “to surge up (of waves)”), through an association with the “wine-dark sea”, is a folk etymology.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /por.pʰý.re.os/ → /porˈɸy.re.os/ → /porˈfi.re.os/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /por.pʰý.re.os/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /porˈpʰy.re.os/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /porˈɸy.re.os/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /porˈfy.re.os/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /porˈfi.re.os/

Adjective

[edit]

πορφύρεος • (porphúreos) m (feminine πορφυρέη, neuter πορφύρεον); first/second declension (Epic)

  1. purple (in color)
