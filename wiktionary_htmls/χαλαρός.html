Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From χαλάω (khaláō, “to slacken, loosen”) +‎ -ρός (-rós).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /kʰa.la.rós/ → /xa.laˈros/ → /xa.laˈros/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /kʰa.la.rós/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /kʰa.laˈros/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /xa.laˈros/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /xa.laˈros/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /xa.laˈros/

Adjective

[edit]

χᾰλᾰρός • (khalarós) m (feminine χᾰλᾰρᾱ́, neuter χᾰλᾰρόν); first/second declension

  1. slack, loose, supple
  2. languid, effeminate

Greek

[edit]

Adjective

[edit]

χαλαρός • (chalarós) m (feminine χαλαρή, neuter χαλαρό)

  1. loose, slack
    Synonym: αναχλός (anachlós)
  2. (figuratively) lax, relaxed
