Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • σᾱκόω (sākóō) — Doric

Etymology

[edit]

σηκός (sēkós, “pen, enclosure, fence”) +‎ -όω (-óō, “causative verbal suffix”), thus "to make into an enclosure" > "to compare" > "to balance, weigh against".[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /sɛː.kó.ɔː/ → /siˈko.o/ → /siˈko.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /sɛː.kó.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /se̝ˈko.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /siˈko.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /siˈko.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /siˈko.o/

Verb

[edit]

σηκόω • (sēkóō)

  1. to weigh against
  2. to balance, equalize
  3. to compensate
