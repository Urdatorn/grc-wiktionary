Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἀνα- (ana-, “on, up”) +‎ ἅπτω (háptō, “touch, fasten, ignite”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /a.náp.tɔː/ → /aˈnap.to/ → /aˈnap.to/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /a.náp.tɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /aˈnap.to/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /aˈnap.to/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /aˈnap.to/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /aˈnap.to/

Verb

[edit]

ᾰ̓νᾰ́πτω • (anáptō)

  1. (transitive, ditransitive) bind or make fast to
    1. (middle voice, transitive) moor, form a close connection with, confer, to fasten to oneself
    2. (passive voice) be fastened to, cling
    3. (transitive) hang up in a temple, offer up
    4. (figuratively, transitive) attach to, ascribe, refer
  2. (transitive) light up, kindle
    1. (intransitive) to be lighted up, ignited
