Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ἀποπλήττω (apoplḗttō) — Attic

Etymology

[edit]

From ἀπο- (apo-) +‎ πλήσσω (plḗssō, “to strike”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /a.po.plɛ̌ːs.sɔː/ → /a.poˈplis.so/ → /a.poˈpli.so/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /a.po.plɛ̌ːs.sɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /a.poˈple̝s.so/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /a.poˈplis.so/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /a.poˈplis.so/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /a.poˈpli.so/

Verb

[edit]

ἀποπλήσσω • (apoplḗssō)

  1. to strike to earth
  2. to cripple with a stroke, disable
  3. (passive voice) to lose one's senses, become dizzy, become dumbfounded
    • 442 BCE, Sophocles, Antigone 1189
  4. (middle voice) to drive back, repulse
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Problems 899b.24
