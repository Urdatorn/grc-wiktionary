Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • εἰρωτᾰ́ω (eirōtáō) — Epic

Etymology

[edit]

Related to ἐρεείνω (ereeínō, “to ask”), ἔρομαι (éromai, “to ask”) (which it replaced in Attic), ἐρευνάω (ereunáō, “to search, inquire”), of uncertain origin.[1] The hypothetical reconstructed root, Proto-Indo-European *h₁rew- (“to search, inquire”), appears to be related to Icelandic raun (“trial, test”)[2] and Old Armenian որոնեմ (oronem, “to seek, search for”) (though there exist phonetic issues with the Armenian).

Verb

[edit]

ἐρωτᾰ́ω • (erōtáō)

  1. to ask
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 4.347
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 17.138
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 9.364
    • 429 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus the King 1122
    • 423 BCE, Aristophanes, The Clouds 641
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 895E
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cyropaedia 1.4.3
    1. to ask about a thing
      • 525 BCE – 455 BCE, Aeschylus, Prometheus Bound 226
      • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 508A
      • 366 BCE – 348 BCE, Plato, Theaetetus 185C
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 3.61
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Memorabilia 4.2.23
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 662D
    2. (followed by a relative word)
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 15.423
      • 460 BCE – 370 BCE, Hippocrates, Collected Works 682.46
      • 467 BCE, Aeschylus, Seven Against Thebes 182
      • 446 BCE – 386 BCE, Aristophanes, The Acharnians 648
  2. to question
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 5.97
    • 480 BCE – 411 BCE, Antiphon of Rhamnus, Collected Works 144.7
    • 413 BCE, Euripides, Ion 236
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Anabasis 3.1.7
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Memorabilia 1.3.1
    • 407 BCE, Euripides, Iphigenia in Aulis 1130
    1. (in dialectic argument) to elicit conclusions from the opponent by means of questioning
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Prior Analytics 1.1.3
      • 125 CE – 200 CE, Lucian, How to Write History 17
  3. (Koine) to beg, entreat, solicit
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, 1 Samuel 30.21
    • 50 CE – 100 CE, The Gospel of Luke 8:37
    • 50 CE – 100 CE, The Gospel of Luke 7:36
    • 50 CE – 100 CE, The Gospel of Luke 7:3
    • 50 CE – 100 CE, The Gospel of John 16:26

Usage notes

[edit]

In the dialogues of Plato, ἠρόμην (ērómēn) typically supplies the aorist tense-forms for ἐρωτάω.[3]
