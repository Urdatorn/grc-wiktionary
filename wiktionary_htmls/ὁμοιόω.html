Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ὅμοιος (hómoios, “like, similar”) +‎ -όω (-óō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ho.moi̯.ó.ɔː/ → /o.myˈo.o/ → /o.miˈo.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ho.moi̯.ó.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /(h)o.myˈo.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /o.myˈo.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /o.myˈo.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /o.miˈo.o/

Verb

[edit]

ὁμοιόω • (homoióō)

  1. to make similar, assimilate
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, On Generation and Corruption 324a.10
    • 490 BCE – 430 BCE, Empedocles, Collected Works 22.5
    • 405 BCE, Euripides, The Bacchae 1348
    • 412 BCE, Euripides, Helen 33
    • 431 BCE, Euripides, Medea 890
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 7.158
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 8.28
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 1.187
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 3.120
    • 436 BCE – 338 BCE, Isocrates, To Philip 114
    • 436 BCE – 338 BCE, Isocrates, Evagoras 75
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Phaedrus 261e
    • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 393c
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 2.97
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 3.82
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 5.103
  2. to compare, liken
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.123
    • 70 CE – 110 CE, The Gospel of Matthew 11:16
