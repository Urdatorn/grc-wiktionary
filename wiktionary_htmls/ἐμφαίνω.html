Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐν- (en-) +‎ φαίνω (phaínō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /em.pʰǎi̯.nɔː/ → /emˈɸɛ.no/ → /emˈfe.no/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /em.pʰǎi̯.nɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /emˈpʰɛ.no/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /emˈɸɛ.no/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /emˈfe.no/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /emˈfe.no/

Verb

[edit]

ἐμφαίνω • (emphaínō)

  1. (transitive) to let be seen in a mirror, reflect
  2. (transitive) to exhibit, display
  3. (passive voice) to become visible
  4. (Byzantine, intransitive) to appear
  5. (Byzantine, intransitive) to light up
