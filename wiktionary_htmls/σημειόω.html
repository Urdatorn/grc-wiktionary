Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • σᾱμειόω (sāmeióō) — Doric

Etymology

[edit]

σημεῖον (sēmeîon) +‎ -όω (-óō)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /sɛː.meː.ɔ̂ː/ → /si.miˈo/ → /si.miˈo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /sɛː.meː.ɔ̂ː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /se̝.miˈo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /si.miˈo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /si.miˈo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /si.miˈo/

Verb

[edit]

σημειόω • (sēmeióō)

  1. to mark, stamp
  2. to seal (e.g. letters)
  3. to signal
  4. (in middle) to take notice of, honor
  5. (in middle) to interpret as a sign or portent, infer as from a sign
  6. (in middle, medicine) to diagnose, examine
  7. (in middle) to note (for oneself)
