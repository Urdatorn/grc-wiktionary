Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From κατά (katá) + οἰκέω (oikéō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ka.toi̯.ké.ɔː/ → /ka.tyˈce.o/ → /ka.tiˈce.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ka.toi̯.ké.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ka.tyˈke.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ka.tyˈce.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ka.tyˈce.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ka.tiˈce.o/

Verb

[edit]

κατοικέω • (katoikéō)

  1. to dwell in, to settle in, to colonise
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 7.164
    • 431 BCE, Euripides, Medea 10
    • 330 BCE, Demosthenes, On the Crown 91
    • 409 BCE, Sophocles, Philoctetes 40
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Politics 1266B.2
    1. (absolute) to settle, dwell
      • 406 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus at Colonus 362
      • 414 BCE, Aristophanes, The Birds 153
      • 412 BCE, Euripides, Helen 1651
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 666E
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 3.34
      • 436 BCE – 338 BCE, Isocrates, To Demonicus 36
      • New Testament, Revelation 3:10
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.96
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 4.8
      • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 1.120
  2. to administer, govern
    • 110 BCE – 35 BCE, Philodemus, On Rhetoric 2.225S
    • 406 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus at Colonus 1004
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 683A
  3. (intransitive, of cities) to lie, be situated
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 677C
    • 436 BCE – 338 BCE, Isocrates, To Philip 123
