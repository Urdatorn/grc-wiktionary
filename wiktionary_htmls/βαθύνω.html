Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From βᾰθῠ́ς (bathús, “deep, profound”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ba.tʰý.nɔː/ → /βaˈθy.no/ → /vaˈθi.no/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ba.tʰý.nɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /baˈtʰy.no/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /βaˈθy.no/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /vaˈθy.no/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /vaˈθi.no/

Verb

[edit]

βᾰθῠ́νω • (bathúnō)

  1. (of a torrent) to deepen, hollow out, dig
  2. (passive voice) to become deep, be deepened
  3. (mathematics) to add a third dimension
  4. (intransitive) to sink deep, crumble
  5. (figuratively) to go deeply into a subject
