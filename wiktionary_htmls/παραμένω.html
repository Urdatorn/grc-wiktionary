Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • παρμένω (parménō) — Poetic
  • παραμίμνω (paramímnō) — Poetic

Etymology

[edit]

From παρά (pará, “beside”) + μένω (ménō, “I remain”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pa.ra.mé.nɔː/ → /pa.raˈme.no/ → /pa.raˈme.no/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pa.ra.mé.nɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /pa.raˈme.no/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /pa.raˈme.no/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /pa.raˈme.no/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /pa.raˈme.no/

Verb

[edit]

πᾰρᾰμένω • (paraménō)

  1. to stay near, stand beside
    • 421 BCE, Aristophanes, Peace 1108
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 11.402
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 15.400
    • 400 BCE – 387 BCE, Plato, Apology 39e
    • 386 BCE – 367 BCE, Plato, Meno 97d
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Oeconomicus 3.4
  2. to stand one's ground, stand fast
    • 388 BCE, Aristophanes, Plutus 440
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.82
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 6.14
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 13.151
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Pythian Ode 1.48
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Pythian Ode 1.89
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Pythian Ode 8.40
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 3.10
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 7.15
  3. to stay behind
    • 440 BCE – 390 BCE, Andocides, On the Mysteries 2
    • 480 BCE – 411 BCE, Antiphon of Rhamnus, On the Murder of Herodes 13
    • 484 BCE – 425 BCE, Herodotus, 1 64
  4. to survive
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.30
  5. (of things) to endure, last
    • 213 CE – 273 CE, Cassius Longinus, Collected Works 9.13
    • 458 BCE, Euripides, Electra 942
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 3.57
    • 436 BCE – 338 BCE, Isocrates, Areopagiticus 13
    • 445 BCE – 380 BCE, Lysias, Defence against a Charge of subverting the Democracy 28
    • 64 BCE – 24 CE, Strabo, Geography 11.10.1
    • 400 BCE – 300 BCE, Timocles, Collected Works 9.1
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cyropaedia 1.6.17

Greek

[edit]

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): /pa.ɾaˈme.no/
  • Hyphenation: πα‧ρα‧μέ‧νω

Etymology 1

[edit]

Learnedly, from Ancient Greek παραμένω (paraménō), from παρά (pará, “at”) + μένω (ménō, “stay”)

Verb

[edit]

παραμένω • (paraméno) (past παρέμεινα, passive —)

  1. to remain, stay (at a location)
  2. to remain, stay (unchanged)

Etymology 2

[edit]

From Modern Greek πάρα (pára, “very much”) + μένω (méno, “stay”).

Verb

[edit]

παραμένω • (paraméno) (past παραέμεινα, passive —)

  1. to stay, remain at a place for too long, more than expected
