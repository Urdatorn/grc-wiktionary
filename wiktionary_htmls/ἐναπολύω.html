Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐν- (en-, “in”) +‎ ἀπολῡ́ω (apolū́ō, “loose from”)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.na.po.ly̌ː.ɔː/ → /e.na.poˈly.o/ → /e.na.poˈli.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.na.po.ly̌ː.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /e.na.poˈly.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /e.na.poˈly.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /e.na.poˈly.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /e.na.poˈli.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἐνᾰπολῡ́ω • (enapolū́ō)

  1. to acquit
