Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

παρα- (para-) +‎ αἰτέω (aitéō)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /pa.rai̯.té.o.mai̯/ → /pa.rɛˈte.o.mɛ/ → /pa.reˈte.o.me/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /pa.rai̯.té.o.mai̯/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /pa.rɛˈte.o.mɛ/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /pa.rɛˈte.o.mɛ/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /pa.reˈte.o.me/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /pa.reˈte.o.me/

Verb

[edit]

πᾰραιτέομαι • (paraitéomai)

  1. to beg of or from, ask as a favor
    1. to move by entreaty, obtain leave from
    2. to entreat one to do or be something
  2. to avert by entreaty
  3. to intercede for, beg off
    1. to decline, shun
