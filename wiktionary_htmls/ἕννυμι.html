Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ἑννῠ́ω (hennúō)
  • εἵνῡμῐ (heínūmi), εἱνῠ́ω (heinúō) — Ionic

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Hellenic *wehnūmi, from Proto-Indo-European *wes- (“to dress”); see there for the cognates.

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /hén.nyː.mi/ → /ˈen.ny.mi/ → /ˈe.ni.mi/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /hén.nyː.mi/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈ(h)en.ny.mi/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈen.ny.mi/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈen.ny.mi/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈe.ni.mi/

Verb

[edit]

ἕννῡμῐ • (hénnūmi)

  1. to put clothes on, wear, dress
