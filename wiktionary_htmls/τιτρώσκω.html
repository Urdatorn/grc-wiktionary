Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Ultimately from Proto-Indo-European *terh₃- (“to hurt”).[1] Inchoative suffix -σκω (-skō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ti.trɔ̌ːs.kɔː/ → /tiˈtros.ko/ → /tiˈtros.ko/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ti.trɔ̌ːs.kɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /tiˈtros.ko/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /tiˈtros.ko/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /tiˈtros.ko/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /tiˈtros.ko/

Verb

[edit]

τῐτρώσκω • (titrṓskō)

  1. to harm, hurt
