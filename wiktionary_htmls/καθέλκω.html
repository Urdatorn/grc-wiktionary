Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From κᾰθ- +‎ ἕλκω (“drag”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ka.tʰél.kɔː/ → /kaˈθel.ko/ → /kaˈθel.ko/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ka.tʰél.kɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /kaˈtʰel.ko/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /kaˈθel.ko/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /kaˈθel.ko/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /kaˈθel.ko/

Verb

[edit]

κᾰθέλκω • (kathélkō)

  1. (shipping) to draw to the see, launch
  2. to drag down

Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Ancient Greek καθέλκω (kathélkō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): /kaˈθelˈko/
  • Hyphenation: κα‧θέλ‧κω

Verb

[edit]

καθέλκω • (kathélko) (past καθείλκυσα, passive καθέλκομαι)

  1. (shipping, formal, learned) Alternative form of καθελκύω (kathelkýo, “launch”)
