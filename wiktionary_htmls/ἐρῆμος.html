Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ἔρημος (érēmos) — Attic, early

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *h₁reh₁mós (“dark”), from Proto-Indo-European *h₁reh₁- (“to separate”).[1] Compare Latin rēte, rārus and Sanskrit ऋते (r̥té).[2]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.rɛ̂ː.mos/ → /eˈri.mos/ → /eˈri.mos/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.rɛ̂ː.mos/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /eˈre̝.mos/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /eˈri.mos/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /eˈri.mos/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /eˈri.mos/

Adjective

[edit]

ἐρῆμος • (erêmos) m (feminine ἐρήμη, neuter ἐρῆμον); first/second declension

  1. lonely, lonesome, solitary
    1. (of places)
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 3.270
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 10.520
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 3.32
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.32
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 3.102
      • 175 CE – 235 CE, Claudius Aelianus, On the Nature of Animals 7.48
    2. (of persons or animals)
      • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 5.140
      • 472 BCE, Aeschylus, The Persians 734
      • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, Agamemnon 862
      • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Women of Trachis 530
      • 409 BCE, Sophocles, Philoctetes 228
      • 388 BCE, Aristophanes, Plutus 447
      • 440 BCE – 390 BCE, Andocides, Against Alcibiades 15
      • 361 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Midias 111
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 927C
      • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Julius Caesar 63
      • 423 BCE, Euripides, The Suppliants 775
      • 446 BCE – 386 BCE, Aristophanes, Fragments 393
    3. (of conditions)
      • 406 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus at Colonus 1114
      • 406 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus at Colonus 1716
  2. (with genitive) bereft of, void or destitute of, undefended
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 2.32
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 4.17
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 6.23
    • 429 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus the King 57
    • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Electra 1405
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 8.96
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 9.118
    1. (of persons) abandoned by
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 7.160
      • 406 BCE, Sophocles, Oedipus at Colonus 1717
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 927D
      • 442 BCE, Sophocles, Antigone 919
      • 4th century BC, Isaeus, Apollodorus 31
    2. (with no bad sense) wanting, without
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 9.63
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 862E
  3. (substantive, usually feminine, occasionally masculine) an undefended action, in which one part does not appear, and judgement goes against them by default
    • 480 BCE – 411 BCE, Antiphon of Rhamnus, First Tetralogy 1.7
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 6.61
    • 361 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Midias 81
    • 445 BCE – 380 BCE, Lysias, For Polystratus 18
    • 361 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Midias 85
    • 361 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Midias 87
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Apatourius 33
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Boeotus 2 17
    • 361 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Midias 93
    • 400 BCE – 387 BCE, Plato, Apology 18C
  4. (feminine substantive) desert
