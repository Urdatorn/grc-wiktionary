Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἀνα- (ana-) +‎ γράφω (gráphō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /a.na.ɡrá.pʰɔː/ → /a.naˈɣra.ɸo/ → /a.naˈɣra.fo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /a.na.ɡrá.pʰɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /a.naˈɡra.pʰo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /a.naˈɣra.ɸo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /a.naˈɣra.fo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /a.naˈɣra.fo/

Verb

[edit]

ἀναγράφω • (anagráphō)

  1. to engrave and set up (treaties, laws, etc.)
  2. to inscribe, register
  3. to register someone’s name
  4. (mathematics, middle voice) to describe
  5. to entitle
  6. (Byzantine) to paint
