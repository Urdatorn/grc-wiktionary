Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From μάντις (mántis, “seer”) +‎ -εύομαι (-eúomai, “mediopassive of -εύω”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /man.těu̯.o.mai̯/ → /manˈte.βo.mɛ/ → /manˈde.vo.me/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /man.těu̯.o.mai̯/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /manˈte.wo.mɛ/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /manˈte.βo.mɛ/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /manˈte.vo.me/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /manˈde.vo.me/

Verb

[edit]

μᾰντεύομαι • (manteúomai)

  1. to divine, prophesy
    • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, The Eumenides 716
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 4.67
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 1.107
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 2.300
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 16.859
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Iliad 19.420
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 2.170
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 9.510
  2. to presage, forbode
    • 458 BCE, Aeschylus, Agamemnon 1367
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Generation of Animals 765a27
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Rhetoric 1373b7
    • 386 BCE – 367 BCE, Plato, Cratylus 411b
    • Module:Quotations:449: attempt to compare number with nil
  3. (of animals) to get scent of
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Theocritus, Collected Works 21.45
  4. to consult an oracle, to seek divinations
    • Aeshin., 3 107
    • 414 BCE, Aristophanes, The Birds 593
    • 422 BCE, Aristophanes, The Wasps 159
    • 413 BCE, Euripides, Ion 346
    • 413 BCE, Euripides, Ion 431
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.46
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 4.172
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 6.76
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 8.36
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Olympian Ode 6.38
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Olympian Ode 7.31
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Pythian Ode 4.163
    • 400 BCE – 387 BCE, Plato, Apology 21a
  5. (of the god) to give an oracle
    • 330 BCE, Demosthenes, On the Crown 253
    • 125 CE – 200 CE, Lucian, Alexander the False Prophet 19
    • 125 CE – 200 CE, Lucian, Soloecista 9
  6. (later Greek, in active and here in the passive) to give an oracle
    • 86 CE – 160 CE, Arrian, Ind. 11.5
    • 200 CE – 400 CE, Heliodorus of Emesa, Aethiopica 7.1
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 5.45
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 5.114

Greek

[edit]

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): /manˈde.vo.me/
  • Hyphenation: μα‧ντεύ‧ο‧μαι

Verb

[edit]

μαντεύομαι • (mantévomai) passive (past μαντεύτηκα, active μαντεύω)

  1. to be foretold
  2. to be guessed
