Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From νόσος (nósos, “sickness”) +‎ -έω (-éō, denominative verbal suffix).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /no.sé.ɔː/ → /noˈse.o/ → /noˈse.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /no.sé.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /noˈse.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /noˈse.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /noˈse.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /noˈse.o/

Verb

[edit]

νοσέω • (noséō)

  1. to be sick, to ail
    • 525 BCE – 455 BCE, Aeschylus, Prometheus Bound 386
    • 480 BCE – 411 BCE, Antiphon of Rhamnus, Against the Stepmother for Poisoning 30

    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Rhetoric 1392a.11

    • 428 BCE, Euripides, Hippolytus 293
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.19
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.105
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 3.33
    • 125 CE – 200 CE, Lucian, Octogenerians 22

    • 409 BCE, Sophocles, Philoctetes 41
    • 371 BCE – 287 BCE, Theophrastus, On the Causes of Plants 5.9.9
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 1.138
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 2.31
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Constitution of the Athenians 2.6
  2. to be mad, passionate
    • 519 BCE – 422 BCE, Cratinus, Collected Works 329
    • 342 BCE – 290 BCE, Menander, Monostichoi 550
