Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ποθήω (pothḗō) — Aeolic

Etymology

[edit]

From πόθος (póthos) and/or ποθή (pothḗ) +‎ -έω (-éō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /po.tʰé.ɔː/ → /poˈθe.o/ → /poˈθe.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /po.tʰé.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /poˈtʰe.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /poˈθe.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /poˈθe.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /poˈθe.o/

Verb

[edit]

ποθέω • (pothéō)

  1. to long for, yearn after, desire
  2. to miss or regret (what is lost)
  3. to require
  4. to be anxious (to do something)
