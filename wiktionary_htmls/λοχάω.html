Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From λόχος (lókhos, “ambush”) +‎ -άω (-áō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /lo.kʰá.ɔː/ → /loˈxa.o/ → /loˈxa.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /lo.kʰá.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /loˈkʰa.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /loˈxa.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /loˈxa.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /loˈxa.o/

Verb

[edit]

λοχάω • (lokháō)

  1. (transitive) to lie in wait for, waylay, ambush
  2. (intransitive) to lie in wait or ambush
  3. (transitive) to prepare an ambush on (a road etc.)
