Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ἐπῐτᾰ́μνω (epitámnō) — Ionic

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐπῐ- (epi-, “upon”) +‎ τέμνω (témnō, “to cut”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.pi.tém.nɔː/ → /e.piˈtem.no/ → /e.piˈtem.no/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.pi.tém.nɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /e.piˈtem.no/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /e.piˈtem.no/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /e.piˈtem.no/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /e.piˈtem.no/

Verb

[edit]

ἐπῐτέμνω • (epitémnō)

  1. to cut upon the surface, make an incision, gash
  2. to cut short
    1. to abridge, shorten, epitomize
    2. to cut off
    3. (passive voice) to be cut short, perish
