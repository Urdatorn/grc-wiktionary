Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From νῆστις (nêstis, “fasting”) +‎ -εύω (-eúō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /nɛːs.těu̯.ɔː/ → /nisˈte.βo/ → /nisˈte.vo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /nɛːs.těu̯.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ne̝sˈte.wo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /nisˈte.βo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /nisˈte.vo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /nisˈte.vo/

Verb

[edit]

νηστεύω • (nēsteúō)

  1. to fast, abstain from food

Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Inherited from Ancient Greek νηστεύω (nēsteúō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): /niˈste.vo/
  • Hyphenation: νη‧στεύ‧ω

Verb

[edit]

νηστεύω • (nistévo) (past νήστεψα, passive —)

  1. to fast
  2. (religious) to abstain from something, fast
