Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Seems to be from ἐν (en, “in, at”) + Proto-Indo-European *gew- (“hand; to curve”) (whence γυῖον (guîon, “hand”), ἐγγῠ́η (engúē, “pledge, security”)), thus meaning "at hand (i.e. nearby)".[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /eŋ.ɡýs/ → /eɲˈɟys/ → /eɲˈɟis/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /eŋ.ɡýs/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /eŋˈɡys/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /eɲˈɟys/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /eɲˈɟys/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /eɲˈɟis/
