Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Uncertain, but undoubtedly related to ῥυσός (rhusós, “shrivelled”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /r̥y.tís/ → /ryˈtis/ → /riˈtis/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /r̥y.tís/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ryˈtis/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ryˈtis/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ryˈtis/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /riˈtis/

Noun

[edit]

ῥῠτῐ́ς • (rhutís) f (genitive ῥῠτῐ́δος); third declension

  1. a fold or pucker in the face, wrinkle
    • 388 BCE, Aristophanes, Plutus 1051
    • 385 BCE – 380 BCE, Plato, Symposium 190 E, 191 A
