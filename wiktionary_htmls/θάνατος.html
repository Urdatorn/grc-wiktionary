Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • δάνος (dános) — Macedonian

Etymology

[edit]

From the root of θνῄσκω (thnḗiskō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /tʰá.na.tos/ → /ˈθa.na.tos/ → /ˈθa.na.tos/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /tʰá.na.tos/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈtʰa.na.tos/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈθa.na.tos/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈθa.na.tos/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈθa.na.tos/

Noun

[edit]
Greek Wikipedia has an article on:
θάνατος
Wikipedia el

θᾰ́νᾰτος • (thánatos) m (genitive θᾰνᾰ́του); second declension (Epic, Attic, Ionic, Doric, Koine)

  1. death
  2. corpse

Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Ancient Greek θάνατος (thánatos). Compare Mariupol Greek тъа́натос (θánatos).

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): /ˈθa.na.tos/
  • Hyphenation: θά‧να‧τος

Noun

[edit]

θάνατος • (thánatos) m (plural θάνατοι)

  1. death
