Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἀνα- (ana-, “up(wards)”) +‎ βάλλω (bállō, “to throw”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /a.na.bál.lɔː/ → /a.naˈβal.lo/ → /a.naˈva.lo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /a.na.bál.lɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /a.naˈbal.lo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /a.naˈβal.lo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /a.naˈval.lo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /a.naˈva.lo/

Verb

[edit]

ἀναβάλλω • (anabállō)

  1. (transitive) to throw up
  2. (transitive) to help someone mount
  3. (middle voice) to prelude, to begin to play
  4. (middle voice, transitive) to delay
