Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *kel- (“to drive”).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ké.lo.mai̯/ → /ˈce.lo.mɛ/ → /ˈce.lo.me/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ké.lo.mai̯/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈke.lo.mɛ/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈce.lo.mɛ/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈce.lo.me/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈce.lo.me/

Verb

[edit]

κέλομαι • (kélomai)

  1. to command
  2. to urge, exhort
