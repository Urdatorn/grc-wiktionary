Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

ἀντι- (anti-) +‎ βολέω (boléō), from the root of βάλλω (bállō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /an.ti.bo.lé.ɔː/ → /an.ti.βoˈle.o/ → /an.di.voˈle.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /an.ti.bo.lé.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /an.ti.boˈle.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /an.ti.βoˈle.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /an.ti.voˈle.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /an.di.voˈle.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἀντῐβολέω • (antiboléō)

  1. to meet by chance (especially in battle)
  2. to be present at
  3. to partake of, have one's share of
  4. to fall to one's lot
  5. to entreat, supplicate
