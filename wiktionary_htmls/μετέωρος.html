Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • μετήορος (metḗoros) — Epic

Etymology

[edit]

For μετήορος (metḗoros), from μετά (metá, “beyond”) + *ἀορος (*aoros), o-grade noun from ἀείρω (aeírō, “I lift up, raise”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /me.té.ɔː.ros/ → /meˈte.o.ros/ → /meˈte.o.ros/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /me.té.ɔː.ros/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /meˈte.o.ros/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /meˈte.o.ros/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /meˈte.o.ros/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /meˈte.o.ros/

Adjective

[edit]

μετέωρος • (metéōros) m or f (neuter μετέωρον); second declension

  1. lifted up, on high, in air
  2. (nominalized, neuter plural) astronomical phenomena

Descendants

[edit]

From the neuter μετέωρον:

  • → Bulgarian: метео́р (meteór)
  • → Latin: meteōrum (see there for further descendants)
  • → Macedonian: метеор (meteor)
  • → Russian: метео́р (meteór)
    • → Kazakh: метеор (meteor)
  • → Turkish: meteor
