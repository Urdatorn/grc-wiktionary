Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From ἐπι- (epi-, “upon”) +‎ στρέφω (stréphō, “to turn”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /e.pis.tré.pʰɔː/ → /e.pisˈtre.ɸo/ → /e.pisˈtre.fo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /e.pis.tré.pʰɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /e.pisˈtre.pʰo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /e.pisˈtre.ɸo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /e.pisˈtre.fo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /e.pisˈtre.fo/

Verb

[edit]

ἐπιστρέφω • (epistréphō)

  1. to turn about, turn round, wheel about
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 7.141
    • 430 BCE, Euripides, Herakles' Children 942
    • 10 CE – 70 CE, Hero, Automata 23.3
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 2.90
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Hellenica 6.4.9
    • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Sulla 19
    • 100 BCE – 1 BCE, Asclepiodotus, Tactics 10.5
    1. to turn towards
      • 6th century BC, Theognis of Megara, Elegies 1083
      • 6th century BC, Theognis of Megara, Elegies 213
      • 125 CE – 200 CE, Lucian, Timon 11
      • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Moralia 2.21C
      • 412 BCE – 323 BCE, Diogenes of Sinope, Letters 34.1
      • 497 BCE – 405 BCE, Sophocles, Women of Trachis 1182
      • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Mark Antony 42
      1. (intransitive) to turn oneself towards
        • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, On Horsemanship 8.12
        • 204 CE – 270 CE, Plotinus, Collected Works 5.3.1
        • 412 CE – 485 CE, Proclus, Elements of Theology 15
    2. to turn or convert from an error, to correct, make to repent
      • 125 CE – 200 CE, Lucian, How to Write History 5
      • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Alcibiades 16
      • 46 CE – 120 CE, Plutarch, Cato the Younger 14
      • 230 CE – 238 CE, Philostratus, Lives of the Sophists 1.7.1
      1. (intransitive) to repent
        • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Septuagint, Judith 5.19
        • 70 CE – 110 CE, The Gospel of Matthew 13:15
        • 50 CE – 100 CE, The Gospel of Luke 22:32
    3. to curve, twist
      • 388 BCE, Aristophanes, Plutus 1131
      • Palatine Anthology 11.376.8
      • 460 BCE – 370 BCE, Hippocrates, Aphorisms 4.35
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Problems 963B.10
      • 371 BCE – 287 BCE, Theophrastus, Enquiry into Plants 3.8.4
      • 371 BCE – 287 BCE, Theophrastus, Enquiry into Plants 3.9.6
  2. (middle voice, passive voice, especially in second aorist) to turn oneself around, turn about
    • Oppian, Cynegetica 4.179
    • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 3.156
    • 438 BCE, Euripides, Alcestis 187
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, History of Animals 629B.15
    • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 616C
    • 442 BCE, Sophocles, Antigone 1111
    1. to go back and forth
      • Second Homeric Hymn to Artemis 10
      • 470 BCE, Aeschylus, The Suppliants 508
      • 750 BCE – 650 BCE, Hesiod, Theogony 753
      • 412 BCE, Euripides, Helen 83
      • 413 BCE, Euripides, Ion 352
      • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Oeconomicus 4.13
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Phaedrus 247A
      • 100 CE – 300 CE, Dionysius Periegetes, Periegesis 584
    2. to turn the mind towards, pay attention to
      • 409 BCE, Sophocles, Philoctetes 599
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 1.88
      • c. 440 BCE, Euripides, Rhesus 400
      • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Demosthenes, Against Aristocrates 136
    3. (perfect passive participle) earnest, vehement
      • 460 BCE – 420 BCE, Herodotus, Histories 8.62
      • 230 CE – 238 CE, Philostratus, Lives of the Sophists 1.7.1
