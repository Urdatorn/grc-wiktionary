Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ἀβάω (abáō) — Aeolic
  • ἡβίω (hēbíō) — Cretan

Etymology

[edit]

From ἥβη (hḗbē, “youth; vigor; pubes”) +‎ -ᾰ́ω (-áō).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /hɛː.bá.ɔː/ → /iˈβa.o/ → /iˈva.o/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /hɛː.bá.ɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /(h)e̝ˈba.o/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /iˈβa.o/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /iˈva.o/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /iˈva.o/

Verb

[edit]

ἡβᾰ́ω • (hēbáō)

  1. to be in the prime of youth, adolescence; to be young
  2. to be pubescent, attain the age of puberty; reach adulthood; grow up into a mature young adult
  3. (figurative) to be fresh, vigorous, youthful, like an adolescent; possess the energy and vigor characteristic of a passionate young man
