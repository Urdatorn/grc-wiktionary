Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • ἀμφέπω (amphépō) — only so in tragedies

Etymology

[edit]

ἀμφι- (amphi-) +‎ ἕπω (hépō)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /am.pʰi.é.pɔː/ → /am.ɸiˈe.po/ → /am.fiˈe.po/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /am.pʰi.é.pɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /am.pʰiˈe.po/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /am.ɸiˈe.po/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /am.fiˈe.po/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /am.fiˈe.po/

Verb

[edit]

ἀμφιέπω • (amphiépō)

  1. to go about, to surround
  2. to be busy about, to perform
    Homer, Iliad, 24.804
    ὣς οἵ γ᾽ ἀμφίεπον τάφον Ἕκτορος ἱπποδάμοιο.
    Thus they performed the funeral of Hector the tamer of horses.
  3. to care for
