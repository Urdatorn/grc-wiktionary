Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

μάκαρ (mákar, “blessed”) +‎ -ιος (-ios, adjective suffix).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ma.ká.ri.os/ → /maˈka.ri.os/ → /maˈka.ri.os/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ma.ká.ri.os/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /maˈka.ri.os/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /maˈka.ri.os/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /maˈka.ri.os/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /maˈka.ri.os/

Adjective

[edit]

μακάριος • (makários) m (feminine μακαρία, neuter μακάριον); first/second declension

  1. (mostly of men) blessed, happy
    • 522 BCE – 443 BCE, Pindar, Pythian Ode 5.61
    • 408 BCE, Euripides, Orestes 86
    • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 354A
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Nichomachean Ethics 1.10.14–16
    • 342 BCE – 290 BCE, Menander, The Demiurge 2, (compare Monostiches 357)
    • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Protagoras 309C
    • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 432D
    • 424 BCE, Aristophanes, The Knights 186, (compare The Wasps 1512)
    • 380 BCE, Plato, Euthydemus 303C
    • 422 BCE, Aristophanes, The Wasps 1292
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cyropaedia 8.3.39
    1. (often in Plato as a plural substantive) the rich and better educated
      • 380 BCE, Plato, The Republic 335E, (compare Aristotle, Nicomachean Ethics 8.5.3, Aristotle, Politics 7.1.4)
      • 386 BCE – 367 BCE, Plato, Meno 71A
      • 342 BCE – 290 BCE, Menander, The Harp-Player 1.6
    2. (of the dead)
      • 428 BCE – 347 BCE, Plato, Laws 947D, (compare Aristophanes, Fragments 445)
    3. (of states, qualities, and the like)
      • 408 BCE, Euripides, Orestes 1208
      • 415 BCE, Euripides, The Trojan Women 327

Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Learned borrowing from Ancient Greek μακάριος (makários, “blessed”).[1]

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): /maˈka.ɾi.os/
  • Hyphenation: μα‧κά‧ρι‧ος

Adjective

[edit]

μακάριος • (makários) m (feminine μακάρια, neuter μακάριο)

  1. blissful (extremely happy; full of joy; experiencing, indicating, causing, or characterized by bliss)
    μακάρια άγνοια (ironic) ― makária ágnoia ― blissful ignorance
  2. (dated) blessed (having divine aid, or protection, or other blessing)
    Synonym: ευλογημένος (evlogiménos)
