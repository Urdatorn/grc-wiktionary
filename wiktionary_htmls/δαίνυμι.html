Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From Proto-Indo-European *deh₂y- (“to share, divide”) +‎ -νῡμι (-nūmi).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /dǎi̯.nyː.mi/ → /ˈðɛ.ny.mi/ → /ˈðe.ni.mi/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /dǎi̯.nyː.mi/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ˈdɛ.ny.mi/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ˈðɛ.ny.mi/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ˈðe.ny.mi/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ˈðe.ni.mi/

Verb

[edit]

δαίνῡμῐ • (daínūmi) (chiefly poetic)

  1. (active voice) to give a feast, host a feast
  2. (middle voice) to partake in a feast
