Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

From κᾰτᾰ- (kata-, “denoting motion downwards”) +‎ λέγω (légō, “to choose, to say”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /ka.ta.lé.ɡɔː/ → /ka.taˈle.ɣo/ → /ka.taˈle.ɣo/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /ka.ta.lé.ɡɔː/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /ka.taˈle.ɡo/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /ka.taˈle.ɣo/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /ka.taˈle.ɣo/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /ka.taˈle.ɣo/

Verb

[edit]

κᾰτᾰλέγω • (katalégō)

  1. to pick out, to choose out of, to levy/enlist/enrol
  2. to tell or count up, to recount, to reckon up (i.e. to go over in detail, e.g. a pedigree)
    1. to repeat
  3. to lay down
