Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

συν- (sun-, “with”) +‎ ἐργάζομαι (ergázomai, “to work”)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /sy.ner.ɡáz.do.mai̯/ → /sy.nerˈɣa.zo.mɛ/ → /si.nerˈɣa.zo.me/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /sy.ner.ɡáz.do.mai̯/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /sy.nerˈɡa.zo.mɛ/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /sy.nerˈɣa.zo.mɛ/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /sy.nerˈɣa.zo.me/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /si.nerˈɣa.zo.me/

Verb

[edit]

σῠνεργάζομαι • (sunergázomai)

  1. to work with, cooperate; to contribute
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Generation of Animals 753a18
    • 384 BCE – 322 BCE, Aristotle, Problems 880b23
    • 125 CE – 200 CE, Lucian, Demosthenes 38
    • 125 CE – 200 CE, Lucian, The Cock 24
    • 320 CE – 403 CE, Oribasius, Collected Works 9.26.1
    • 371 BCE – 287 BCE, Theophrastus, On the Causes of Plants 3.12.1
    • 371 BCE – 287 BCE, Theophrastus, Enquiry into Plants 6.3.3
    • 371 BCE – 287 BCE, Theophrastus, On Fatigue 15
    • 371 BCE – 287 BCE, Theophrastus, On Winds 21
    • 460 BCE – 395 BCE, Thucydides, History of the Peloponnesian War 1.93
    • 430 BCE – 354 BCE, Xenophon, Cyropaedia 7.1.33
    • Palatine Anthology 9.807

Greek

[edit]

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): /si.neɾˈɣa.zo.me/
  • Hyphenation: συ‧νερ‧γά‧ζο‧μαι
  • Old Hyphenation: συν‧ερ‧γά‧ζο‧μαι

Verb

[edit]

συνεργάζομαι • (synergázomai) deponent (past συνεργάστηκα)

  1. to collaborate, to cooperate, to synergise (UK), synergize (US)
