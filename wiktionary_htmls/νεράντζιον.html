Ancient Greek

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • νεράντζιν (nerántzin), νεράντζι (nerántzi), νεράτζι (nerátzi), ἐράντζι (erántzi)

Etymology

[edit]

From Arabic نَارَنْج (nāranj) +‎ -ιον (-ion, diminutive suffix), ultimately from Persian نارنگ (nârang).

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /neˈrand.zi.on/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /neˈrant.zi.on/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /neˈrand.zi.on/

Noun

[edit]

νεράντζιον • (nerántzion) n (indeclinable) (Byzantine)

  1. bitter orange, Seville orange (fruit)

Derived terms

[edit]
  • νεραντζέα (nerantzéa)
  • νεραντζόζουμον (nerantzózoumon)

Descendants

[edit]
  • Greek: νεράντζι (nerántzi), νεράτζι (nerátzi)
  • Pontic Greek: νεράντζιν (nerántzin), αναράντζιν (anarántzin), ανάραντζιν (anárantzin), αναράντζ' (anarántz') — Trapezounta, αραράντζι (ararántzi), νεράντζι (nerántzi) — Oinoe
    • → Laz: არანძი (aranżi), არარანცი (araranʒi) — Khopa, ანძი (anżi) — Vitse
