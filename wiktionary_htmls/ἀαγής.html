Ancient Greek

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

ᾰ̓- (a-, “un-”) +‎ ᾱ̓́γνῡμῐ (ā́gnūmi, “break”) +‎ -ής (-ḗs, adjectival suffix)

Pronunciation

[edit]
 
  • IPA(key): /a.aː.ɡɛ̌ːs/ → /a.aˈʝis/ → /a.aˈʝis/
  • (5th BCE Attic) IPA(key): /a.aː.ɡɛ̌ːs/
  • (1st CE Egyptian) IPA(key): /a.aˈɡe̝s/
  • (4th CE Koine) IPA(key): /a.aˈʝis/
  • (10th CE Byzantine) IPA(key): /a.aˈʝis/
  • (15th CE Constantinopolitan) IPA(key): /a.aˈʝis/

Adjective

[edit]

ἀᾱγής • (aāgḗs) m or f (neuter ἀᾱγές); third declension (Epic, Doric)

  1. unbreakable
  2. solid
  3. strong
    • 800 BCE – 600 BCE, Homer, Odyssey 11.575
    • 300 BCE – 200 BCE, Theocritus, Collected Works 24.123
    • 300 BCE – 250 BCE, Apollonius of Rhodes, Argonautica 3.1251
